Senator Cynthia Lummis Biograph

Senator Cynthia Marie Lummis Wiederspahn (R) is an American politician. She is serving as a junior US Senator from Wyoming. She was a member of the state’s House of Representatives. She also served in the Wyoming Senate before her election to the US House of Representatives.

Lummis was born in Cheyenne, Wyoming, on September 10, 1954. Senator Cynthia Lummis attended Cheyenne East High School. She earned her Bachelor’s degree in Animal Science from the University of Wyoming in 1976. After her Bachelor’s degree, she worked as a teacher at the Rock River School. She also received a Bachelor’s degree in Biology in 1978. She furthered and received a law degree from the University of Wyoming in 1985.

Senator Cynthia Lummis Career

Lummis started her political career in 1978. She became a member of the Wyoming House of Representatives. She represented Laramie County in the state legislature. She won again in 1980 and served till 1982. She decided not to seek reelection in 1982 but came back to the statehouse in 1984. She served till 1992.

In 1992, Senator Cynthia Lummis ran for a seat at the State Senate in Wyoming. She defeated Democratic Elizabeth Byrd in the general election. But, she left the State Senate in 1994 to focus on her family. Yet, in 1998, Lummis announced her decision to run for State Treasurer after Stan Smith stepped down. She won the election. She was speculated to run for the 2002 state gubernatorial election. But, she declined and sought reelection as the Treasurer in 2002.

In 2008, Lummis announced her desire to run for a vacant seat at the US House of Representatives. She won the Republican primary. She also defeated Democratic nominee Gary Trauner in the general election.

She was re-elected in 2010 and served till 2016. But she decided not to run for reelection in 2016. She also declined to run for the state’s gubernatorial election in 2018. In 2019, Lummis continued her political career as a candidate to succeed retiring Senator Mike Enzi. She won the Republican primary and also won the general election. She was the first woman to represent Wyoming in the US Senate.

Senator Cynthia Lummis Accomplishments

Lummis is a reputable and principled conservative lawmaker. She fought to reduce the federal deficit and combat excessive spending. She was also instrumental in the economic development of her state.

Born on a cattle ranch, Lummis has worked towards infrastructural development, especially in rural areas. She introduced the first approved Interior and Environmental Appropriations bill. She has also worked to increase the harnessing of public land. Apart from all these, her administration has maintained over 157,000 miles of trails in the national forest.

Her administration also fosters environmental protection and development. She championed the improvement of Wyoming’s mineral and energy resources. She also supported nuclear development in Alaska. To improve access to quality and affordable healthcare, Lummis supported the Affordable Care Act. She was also instrumental in several other important legislatures and foreign policies.

Senator Cynthia Lummis Criticisms

Senator Lummis was criticized for questionable interest in the practice of house slaughtering in the country. The practice was widely considered inhumane and was subsequently amended.

Lummis was also criticized for supporting President Trump after the January 6 insurrection. Her reactions were tagged delusion and dishonesty.

According to Lummis, she claimed Trump’s supporters are peaceful, happy, and patriotic people. She so denied that Trump’s supporters were responsible, adding that they are Pro-America. She also voted in opposition to the creation of an independent commission.

Final Thoughts
