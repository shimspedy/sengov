Brad Yeager is a member of the Republican Party and represents District 56 in the Florida House of Representatives. He assumed office on November 8, 2022, and his current term ends on November 5, 2024.

Brad Yeager's career experience includes working as a general manager with ADESA, a regional sales director with TradeRev, and a senior manager with Cox Automotive, Inc. These roles have equipped him with a wealth of experience and knowledge that he brings to his role as a representative.

In the 2023-2024 term, Yeager was assigned to the Commerce Committee, further expanding his influence and responsibilities within the House of Representatives.

Yeager is running for re-election to the Florida House of Representatives to represent District 56. He declared his candidacy for the Republican primary scheduled on August 20, 2024.

In the previous election cycle, Yeager won the election in the general election for Florida House of Representatives District 56. The general election was canceled, and no candidate was nominated to challenge him. He defeated Scott Moore and Jayden Cocuzza in the Republican primary for Florida House of Representatives District 56 on August 23, 2022.

Brad Yeager was born and raised in Florida, having moved to the state in 1978. He is a Christian by faith and is married to Lisa Yeager. He completed his education at Charlotte High School, Punta Gorda, Florida, in 1994.

In addition to his political career, Yeager is also a General Manager in the Automotive Industry and a Small Business Owner. His diverse career path and experiences have shaped his perspectives and approach to his role as a representative.
