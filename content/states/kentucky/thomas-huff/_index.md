Thomas Huff is an American politician serving as a member of the Kentucky House of Representatives from the 49th district. He was born on June 10, 1963, in Shepherdsville, Kentucky, where he was also raised.

Before his political career, Huff worked for Huff's Used Cars & Auto Parts. His career in politics began when he was elected to the Kentucky House of Representatives in November 2018. He assumed office on January 1, 2019.

During his tenure, Huff has been assigned to several committees. In the 2023-2024 period, he served on the Economic Development & Workforce Investment Committee, House Enrollment Committee, Licensing, Occupations, and Administrative Regulations Committee, House State Government Committee, and House Transportation Committee. In the 2021-2022 period, he was part of the Economic Development & Workforce Investment Committee, Licensing, Occupations, and Administrative Regulations Committee, Tourism and Outdoor Recreation Committee, and House Transportation Committee. In his first term from 2019-2020, he was assigned to the Economic Development & Workforce Investment Committee, Licensing, Occupations, and Administrative Regulations Committee, and House Transportation Committee.

Huff is a member of the Republican Party. His current term ends on January 1, 2025. He is running for re-election to the Kentucky House of Representatives to represent District 49 and is on the ballot in the Republican primary on May 21, 2024.
