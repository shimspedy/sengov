Vanessa Grossl, born on February 22, 1980, is a dedicated American politician currently serving as a member of the Kentucky House of Representatives. Representing the 88th district, which encompasses parts of Fayette and Scott Counties, she assumed office on January 1, 2025. Grossl's journey into politics began with her election in the 2024 Kentucky House of Representatives election, where she secured 50.3 percent of the vote, defeating the incumbent Democratic representative Cherlynn Stevenson.

Grossl's educational background includes a bachelor's degree from the University of Louisville, earned in 2003, and a graduate degree from the University of Kentucky, completed in 2005. Her professional career is diverse, with experience as an account executive, K-12 and higher education teacher, civil servant, policy analyst, advancement manager, and business development coordinator. This extensive experience has equipped her with a broad understanding of various sectors and the challenges they face.

In addition to her professional achievements, Grossl is actively involved in several organizations. She has been affiliated with Rotary International, the Junior League of Lexington, and the Japan America Society of Kentucky. These affiliations reflect her commitment to community service and her dedication to fostering international relations.

As a member of the Kentucky House of Representatives, Grossl serves on several committees, including the Economic Development & Workforce Investment Committee, the Postsecondary Education Committee, and the Small Business & Information Technology Committee.
