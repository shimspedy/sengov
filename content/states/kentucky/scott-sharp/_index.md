Scott Sharp is an American politician who has been serving as a member of the Kentucky House of Representatives since January 1, 2021. He represents Kentucky's 100th House district. He was first elected in the 2020 Kentucky House of Representatives election and was reelected in the 2022 Kentucky House of Representatives election.

Before his political career, Scott Sharp served in the United States Army. He earned a Master of Science in Organizational Psychology from St. Joseph University and a Master of Arts in Management from Webster University. His career experience includes working as a special agent with the Criminal Investigation Command and teaching at Ohio University Southern Campus. He is a Catholic.

In terms of his political career, Scott Sharp is a member of the Republican Party. He assumed office on January 1, 2021, and his current term ends on January 1, 2025. He is running for re-election to the Kentucky House of Representatives to represent District 100.

In his tenure, Sharp has been assigned to various committees. For the years 2023-2024, he was assigned to the Economic Development & Workforce Investment Committee, Health and Family Services Committee, House Judiciary Committee, and House Veterans, Military Affairs, and Public Protection Committee, where he served as Vice Chair. In the previous term (2021-2022), he was also assigned to the Economic Development & Workforce Investment Committee, Health and Family Services Committee, and House Veterans, Military Affairs, and Public Protection Committee.

Scott Sharp's dedication to his work and his constituents is evident in his service. His contributions to the Kentucky House of Representatives and his district are commendable.
