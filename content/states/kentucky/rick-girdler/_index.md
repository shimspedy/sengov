Rick Girdler, born on May 23, 1955, is an American politician who has been serving in the Kentucky Senate from the 15th district since 2017. He is a member of the Republican Party.

Girdler was born in Somerset, Kentucky. He completed his high school education at Pulaski County High School and later attended Cumberland College.

In addition to his political career, Girdler has had a diverse professional background. He has worked with Cumberland Life Underwriters and served as a teacher with the Life Underwriter Training Council. He has also been a member of the Northern School Site Based Council, a board member and coach with Somerset Little League, and a self-employed insurance agent.

During his tenure, Girdler has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 term, he served as the Vice Chair of the Senate Banking and Insurance Committee and the Economic Development, Tourism and Labor Committee. He was also a member of the Committee on Families and Children and the Senate Veterans, Military Affairs, and Public Protection Committee, where he served as the Chair.

Girdler's commitment to public service extends beyond his role as a senator. He is known for his active participation in community activities and his dedication to improving the lives of the people in his district.
