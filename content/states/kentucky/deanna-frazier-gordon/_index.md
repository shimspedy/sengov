Deanna Frazier Gordon is a member of the Kentucky House of Representatives, representing District 81. She is a member of the Republican Party. She assumed office on January 1, 2019, and her current term ends on January 1, 2025.

Born in Richmond, Kentucky, she earned a Bachelor of Science in Communication Disorders from Eastern Kentucky University, a Master's degree in Communication Disorders from the University of Cincinnati, and a Doctor of Audiology from the Arizona School of Health Sciences.

She has also been a part of the following Interim Joint Committees: Banking & Insurance, Health Services, Appropriations & Revenue, Local Government, and Tourism, Small Business, and Information Technology.

In addition to her legislative duties, Frazier Gordon is a member of several caucuses, including the Central Kentucky Caucus, Aerospace/Aviation Caucus, Kentucky History Caucus, Kentucky Rural Electric Cooperatives Caucus, Kentucky Sportsmen's Caucus, and the Pro-Life Caucus.
