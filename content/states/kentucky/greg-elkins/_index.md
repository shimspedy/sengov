Greg Elkins, born on October 10, 1964, is an American politician from Kentucky. He is a member of the Republican Party and has been representing District 28 in the Kentucky Senate since June 8, 2023.

Elkins won a special election on May 16, 2023, which led to his assumption of office. His current term is set to end on January 1, 2027. Prior to his tenure in the Kentucky State Senate, Elkins served two terms as a Clark County Magistrate in Winchester, Kentucky.

In addition to his political career, Elkins is also a businessman. He is the CEO of Central Kentucky Hauling, which is considered the largest privately held solid waste company in Kentucky. The company provides a fleet of trucks to handle commercial, industrial, construction, and residential waste removal needs.

Elkins was born and raised in Kentucky. He served in the Kentucky National Guard, further demonstrating his commitment to public service. His career experience includes working as the CEO of a waste management company.

Elkins' victory in the special general election for Kentucky State Senate District 28 was decisive. He defeated Robert Sainte and Richard Henderson, securing 49.8% of the votes.

His dedication to his constituents and his extensive experience in both the public and private sectors make him a respected figure in Kentucky politics.
