Phillip Pratt is a member of the Republican Party and has been serving in the Kentucky House of Representatives, representing District 62, since 2017. He was born on May 12, 1955, in Georgetown, Kentucky.

Pratt earned a Bachelor of Science in Agricultural Economics from the University of Kentucky. Before his political career, he founded Pratt's Lawn & Landscape, showcasing his entrepreneurial spirit.

During his tenure, Pratt has been assigned to various committees. For the 2023-2024 term, he served on the House Agriculture Committee, Economic Development & Workforce Investment Committee, Licensing, Occupations, and Administrative Regulations Committee, and chaired the Small Business & Information Technology Committee. In the 2021-2022 term, he was assigned to the House Agriculture Committee, Licensing, Occupations, and Administrative Regulations Committee, and chaired the Small Business & Information Technology Committee. In the 2019-2020 term, he served as the Vice Chair of the House Appropriations and Revenue Committee, and was also a member of the House Agriculture Committee and Licensing, Occupations, and Administrative Regulations Committee.

Pratt ran for re-election to the Kentucky House of Representatives to represent District 62 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. His current term ends on January 1, 2025. However, he did not file to run for re-election in 2024.

Throughout his political career, Pratt has sponsored numerous bills, contributing to the legislative process and representing the interests of his constituents in District 62.
