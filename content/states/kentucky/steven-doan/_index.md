Steven Doan, born on October 14, 1986, is a member of the Republican Party and currently serves in the Kentucky House of Representatives, representing District 69. He assumed office on January 1, 2023, and his current term ends on January 1, 2025.

Doan earned a Bachelor's degree and a Juris Doctor from Northern Kentucky University in 2009 and 2012, respectively. His career experience includes working as an attorney. Before his political career, he served on the Erlanger City Council.

In 2022, he ran against incumbent representative Adam Koenig. Doan defeated Koenig in the primary by 7.4 points, and he went on to face Chris Brown on November 8, 2022. Doan defeated Brown by 20 points, and he assumed office on January 1, 2023.

Doan is running for re-election to the Kentucky House of Representatives to represent District 69. He is on the ballot in the Republican primary on May 21, 2024.

His district comprises parts of Boone and Kenton counties. As a legislator, Doan has sponsored several bills, contributing to the legislative process. He is also a member of various committees, including the House Local Government Committee.

Steven Doan is not just a politician but also a dedicated public servant with a strong commitment to his constituents and the betterment of his district and the state of Kentucky.
