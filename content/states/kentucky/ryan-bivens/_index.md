Ryan Bivens is a dedicated public servant and a prominent figure in Kentucky politics. Born on January 29, 1979, he has spent his life deeply rooted in the agricultural community. Bivens earned a bachelor's degree in agriculture from the University of Kentucky in 2001, which laid the foundation for his career as a farmer. His extensive experience in agriculture has given him a unique perspective on the challenges and opportunities faced by rural communities.

Bivens' political journey began with his election to the Kentucky House of Representatives, representing District 24, which includes Green, Hart, and LaRue Counties. He assumed office on January 1, 2025, after winning the general election on November 5, 2024, with an impressive 81.6 percent of the vote. His victory in the Republican primary against Asa L. T. Waggoner further solidified his position as a rising star in Kentucky politics.

Throughout his tenure, Bivens has been an active member of several key committees, including the House Session Standing Committee on Elections, Constitutional Amendments & Intergovernmental Affairs, the Transportation Committee, and the Agriculture Committee. His commitment to these committees reflects his dedication to addressing the needs of his constituents and improving the overall quality of life in his district.

Bivens' background as a self-employed farmer and his strong ties to the agricultural community have made him a passionate advocate for policies that support farmers and rural development. He has consistently worked to promote sustainable farming practices, enhance infrastructure in rural areas, and ensure that the voices of rural Kentuckians are heard in the state legislature.

In addition to his professional achievements, Bivens is known for his strong family values and his active involvement in his local community. He resides in Hodgenville, Kentucky, where he continues to engage with his constituents and work towards creating a better future for all residents of District 24. His dedication to public service and his unwavering commitment to the people of Kentucky make him a respected and influential figure in the state's political landscape.
