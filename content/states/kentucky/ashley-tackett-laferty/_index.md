Ashley Tackett Laferty is an American attorney and politician serving as a member of the Kentucky House of Representatives from the 95th district. She was elected in November 2018 and assumed office on January 1, 2019. Laferty is considered a conservative Southern Democrat.

Early Life and Education:
Laferty was born in Martin, Kentucky. She pursued her higher education at Eastern Kentucky University where she earned a Bachelor of Arts degree. Following this, she attended the University of Louisville School of Law to earn her Juris Doctor degree.

Career:
After graduating from law school, Laferty began her career as an attorney. Her political journey started when she was elected to the Kentucky House of Representatives in November 2018, defeating incumbent Republican Larry Brown. She assumed office on January 1, 2019. Laferty was re-elected in 2020 with 60% of the vote, beating Republican challenger William Matt Reynolds. She was again re-elected in 2022 with 60% of the vote, defeating Republican Brandon Spencer.

Committee Assignments:
Throughout her tenure, Laferty has been assigned to various committees. These include the Economic Development & Workforce Investment Committee, Tourism and Outdoor Recreation Committee, House Transportation Committee, and House Veterans, Military Affairs, and Public Protection Committee.

Personal Life:
In her personal life, Laferty is married to Chuck and they have one child. She continues to serve her community and state with dedication and commitment.
