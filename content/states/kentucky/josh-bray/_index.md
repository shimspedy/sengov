Josh Bray (born March 28, 1985) is an American politician who has served as a member of the Kentucky House of Representatives since January 1, 2021. He represents Kentucky's 71st House district. Outside of politics, he is a beef farmer.

Bray was born on March 28, 1985. He is a member of the Republican Party and is married to Jana Bray. They have two children together. Bray earned a Bachelor of Business Administration from Eastern Kentucky University. He is a Baptist.

Bray's political career began when he was elected on November 8, 2020, in the 2020 Kentucky House of Representatives election. He was reelected in the 2022 Kentucky House of Representatives election. His current term ends on January 1, 2025. Bray is running for re-election to the Kentucky House of Representatives to represent District 71.

In addition to his political career, Bray operates a beef farm. His career experience includes being a business executive. He has been assigned to various committees including the House Appropriations and Revenue Committee, House Banking and Insurance Committee, Health and Family Services Committee, House Judiciary Committee, and House Local Government Committee.

Bray's work in the Kentucky House of Representatives has been marked by his commitment to rural Kentucky. He has sponsored several bills during his tenure, demonstrating his active involvement in the legislative process.
