Shane Baker is an American politician who is currently serving as a member of the Kentucky House of Representatives from the 85th district. He was elected in November 2020 and assumed office on January 1, 2021.

Before his political career, Baker worked as a field representative with the Kentucky Department for Local Government. He also operated a small business, showcasing his entrepreneurial spirit and commitment to local economic development.

In his role as a representative, Baker has been involved in various committees. These include the Economic Development & Workforce Investment Committee, the House Education Committee, and the Committee on Families and Children. His involvement in these committees reflects his dedication to improving education, supporting families, and fostering economic growth in his district.

Baker's legislative efforts also extend to health matters. He sponsored House Bill 112, which aimed to prohibit requiring COVID-19 vaccinations for kids without the consent of all guardians. This bill demonstrates his commitment to individual rights and informed consent in healthcare decisions.

Baker resides in Somerset, Kentucky. His career experience and dedication to public service make him a significant figure in Kentucky's political landscape.
