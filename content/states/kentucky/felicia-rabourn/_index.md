Felicia Rabourn, born on December 22, 1990, in Henry County, Kentucky, is an American politician serving in the Kentucky House of Representatives from the 47th district. She assumed office on January 1, 2021.

Early Life

Felicia Rabourn was born and raised in Henry County, Kentucky. She spent her early career working for Edward Jones Investments as a branch office administrator.

Political Career

Rabourn's political journey began when she became the youth chair of the Henry County Republican Party. Her first major political challenge came in 2020 when she won a close primary by just 2.2 percent.

In the 2020 election, Rabourn won with 69.4 percent of the vote. She won Carroll County with 68.2 percent of the vote, Gallatin County with 74.9 percent of the vote, Henry County with 71.6 percent of the vote, and Trimble County with 61.5 percent of the vote.

Political Positions

Rabourn is described as a “Young Conservative Republican”. However, she has been criticized for supporting plans that would take government-paid healthcare coverage away from many people in Kentucky.

Current Status

Felicia Rabourn (Republican Party) is running for re-election to the Kentucky House of Representatives to represent District 47. Her current term ends on January 1, 2025.
