Kimberly Poore Moser, born on June 8, 1962, is an American politician who has been serving in the Kentucky House of Representatives from the 64th district since 2017. She was born in Taylor Mill, Kentucky, and is a member of the Republican Party.

Moser has a rich educational background. She earned a Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) and became a Registered Nurse (RN) from Spalding University.

In terms of her career, Moser has a diverse experience. She has worked as a NICU and flight nurse. Beyond her nursing career, she served as a director with the Northern Kentucky Office of Drug Control Policy and was the president of the AMA Alliance. She has also been a member of the American Society of Addiction Medicine.

In her political career, Moser has held the office in the Kentucky House of Representatives since 2017. Her current term ends on January 1, 2025. She has been assigned to several committees over the years, including the Elections, Constitutional Amendments & Intergovernmental Affairs Committee, Health and Family Services Committee (which she chaired), House Judiciary Committee, Judiciary Committee, State Government Committee, and Licensing, Occupations, and Administrative Regulations Committee.

Moser is running for re-election to the Kentucky House of Representatives to represent District 64. She is on the ballot in the Republican primary.
