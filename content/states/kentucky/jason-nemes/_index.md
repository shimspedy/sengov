Jason Michael Nemes, born on March 27, 1978, is an American politician who has been serving in the Kentucky House of Representatives from the 33rd district since 2017. He is a member of the Republican Party.

Jason was born and raised in Louisville, Kentucky. He is the son of Mike Nemes. Jason earned a Bachelor of Arts from Western Kentucky University and a law degree from the University of Louisville. His career experience includes working as an attorney.

In his political career, Jason assumed office in 2017. His current term ends on January 1, 2025. He is running for re-election to the Kentucky House of Representatives to represent District 33.

Over the years, Jason has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 tenure, he was assigned to the House Committee On Committees, House Judiciary Committee, and House Rules Committee. In the 2021-2022 tenure, he was assigned to the House Appropriations and Revenue Committee, Elections, Const. Amendments & Intergovernmental Affairs Committee, House Judiciary Committee, Small Business & Information Technology Committee, and House State Government Committee.

Jason Nemes is not just a politician, but also a Majority Whip and an attorney. His dedication and service to the people of the 33rd district of Kentucky have been commendable.
