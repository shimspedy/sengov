Daniel Grossberg is a member of the Kentucky House of Representatives, representing District 30. He assumed office on January 1, 2023, and his current term ends on January 1, 2025. He is a member of the Democratic Party.

Born on September 30, 1978, Grossberg graduated from Teaneck High School in 1996. He earned a Bachelor of Arts in Mathematics and Sciences from Grinnell College in 2006. After his graduation, he served in the U.S. Army from 2006 to 2007.

Before his tenure in the Kentucky House of Representatives, Grossberg held the position of Jefferson County Commissioner in District A. He succeeded Thomas Burch in the Kentucky House of Representatives. His successor in the Jefferson County Commission was Noah Grimes.

In addition to his political career, Grossberg has experience working as a realtor with Semonin Realtors. He has also served as the president of the Metro Democratic Club and on the Kentucky Democratic State Central Committee.

Grossberg is currently running for re-election to the Kentucky House of Representatives to represent District 30. He is on the ballot in the Democratic primary on May 21, 2024.

His compensation includes a base salary of $203.28 per calendar day during the session and a per diem of $203.28 per day for members whose terms began after 2023.

In his legislative role, Grossberg has been assigned to several committees, including the House Natural Resources and Energy Committee, Small Business & Information Technology Committee, Tourism and Outdoor Recreation Committee, Natural Resources and Environment Committee, and Small Business & Information Technology Committee.

Grossberg is Jewish and his profession outside of politics is as a real estate agent.
