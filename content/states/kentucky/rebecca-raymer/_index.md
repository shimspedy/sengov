Rebecca Raymer is an American politician who has been serving as a member of the Kentucky House of Representatives since January 1, 2023. She represents Kentucky's 15th House district. Outside of politics, she is a nurse.

Rebecca Raymer was born in Butler County, Kentucky. She graduated from Butler County High School in 1999 and later attended Western Kentucky University, graduating in 2004.

She began her political career when she was elected on November 8, 2022, in the 2022 Kentucky House of Representatives election against Democratic opponent Britt Hernandez-Stevenson. She assumed office on January 1, 2023.

In addition to her political career, Rebecca Raymer has had a successful career in healthcare. She has worked as a case management nurse with HCA Healthcare.

Rebecca Raymer has also been involved in community service. She has served as the vice president of the Butler County chapter of Stop the Landfill LLC and a precinct officer with the Butler County Republican Party.

As a legislator, Rebecca Raymer has been assigned to several committees including the Health and Family Services Committee, House Local Government Committee, and House Veterans, Military Affairs, and Public Protection Committee.

Rebecca Raymer is currently running for re-election to the Kentucky House of Representatives to represent District 15. She is on the ballot in the Republican primary.
