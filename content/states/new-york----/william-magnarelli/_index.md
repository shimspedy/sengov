William B. Magnarelli, born on March 20, 1949, is a member of the New York State Assembly, representing the 129th district. He is a Democrat. Magnarelli represents the Northside, Westside, Eastwood and Valley areas of the City of Syracuse, as well as the towns of Geddes and Van Buren. He first won election in 1998 for an open Assembly seat and has been reelected multiple times.

Magnarelli grew up in Syracuse, attended Our Lady of Pompei School and St. John the Baptist Academy. He received his bachelor's degree from Syracuse University in 1970, majoring in history. He attended Syracuse University Law School, graduating with honors in 1973. After graduating, Bill Magnarelli served in the U.S. Army Reserve for six years, attaining the rank of captain.

Some of Assemblyman Magnarelli’s most notable accomplishments include securing $37m for the Syracuse Center of Excellence (CoE), funding the initial $20 million for the Syracuse University Connective Corridor, and helping secure $615.5 million for projects in the CNY Region as a voting member of the CNY Regional Economic Development Council (REDC). He also helped secure the Upstate Regional Initiative (URI) award of $500 million for the CNY region.

Magnarelli has voted to cut taxes for middle class families, reduce the marriage penalty tax, and grant funding to school districts for a wide variety of teaching, security and athletic measures. In 2016, he voted for $28.5 billion in School Aid, the highest amount ever. He took the lead in Albany to establish the “Say Yes to Education” program in the City of Syracuse.

He has authored legislation that became law mandating hospitals and birth centers to provide education regarding the dangers of “Shaken Baby Syndrome”. He supported the Women’s Health and Wellness Act, giving New York the most comprehensive women’s health coverage law in the country. He helped preserve and expand the Elderly Pharmaceutical Insurance Coverage (EPIC) program which provides seniors low-cost prescriptions.

Magnarelli was married for over 40 years to his wife Karen, a former city school teacher, who passed away in 2017. Together they raised their three children and have four grandchildren, Olivia, Nora, Ella and Wesley.
