Chris Huff is a Republican member of the South Carolina House of Representatives, representing District 28. He assumed office on November 11, 2024, and his current term is set to end on November 9, 2026.

Chris Huff's journey into politics began with his deep commitment to serving his community and advocating for policies that align with his values. A graduate of Erskine College, where he earned a bachelor's degree, Huff has a background that combines education and practical experience. Before entering public office, he was self-employed, which gave him firsthand insight into the challenges and opportunities faced by small business owners.

In 2024, Huff ran for election to the South Carolina House of Representatives to represent District 28. His campaign focused on addressing local issues, fostering economic growth, and improving education. After a competitive Republican primary and runoff, he emerged as the party's nominee. In the general election, he defeated his Democratic opponent, securing his place in the state legislature.

As a member of the South Carolina House of Representatives, Huff serves on the House Education and Public Works Committee. His legislative priorities include enhancing the quality of education, supporting infrastructure development, and promoting policies that benefit his constituents in Greenville County. Huff's dedication to his role is evident in his active participation in legislative sessions and his efforts to engage with the community he represents.

Beyond his professional and political life, Chris Huff is a family man. He resides in Pelzer, South Carolina, with his wife, Bromley Ann, and their four children. His personal experiences and values continue to shape his approach to governance and his commitment to making a positive impact on his district and the state of South Carolina.
