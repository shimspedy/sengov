Tom Buco is a distinguished member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Carroll 1. He is a member of the Democratic Party. His political journey in the New Hampshire House of Representatives has been quite dynamic. He served the Carroll 1 district from 2004 to 2010. He then represented the Carroll 2nd district from 2012 until he assumed office for Carroll 1 again on December 7, 2022. His current term is set to end on December 4, 2024.

Tom Buco has a strong educational background. He earned a Bachelor of Science degree from Providence College in 1985. His career experience spans across the construction and real estate industries, which adds a unique perspective to his political career.

Over the years, Representative Buco has been assigned to several committees. For the 2023-2024 term, he was assigned to the House Finance Committee and the Finance - Division II Committee. He had similar assignments in the 2021-2022 term. In the 2019-2020 legislative session, he served on the Finance - Division II Committee and the House Finance Committee. At the beginning of the 2017 legislative session, he served on the Finance - Division II committee. In the 2015 legislative session, he served on the Finance and Finance - Division II committees. In the 2013-2014 legislative session, he served on the Finance and Finance - Division II committees, where he was the Vice Chair. In the 2009-2010 legislative session, he served on the Finance and Finance - Division I committees.

Representative Buco has also sponsored numerous bills during his tenure, demonstrating his active participation in legislative matters. In the general election for the New Hampshire House of Representatives Carroll 1 on November 8, 2022, he emerged victorious, further cementing his position as a key figure in New Hampshire politics.
