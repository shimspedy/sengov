Nicholas Germana is a Democratic Party member serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He represents Cheshire District 15, having assumed office on December 4, 2024. His current term is set to conclude on December 2, 2026.

Nicholas Germana, often referred to as Nick, has a distinguished academic background and professional career. He earned a Ph.D. in European intellectual history from Boston College in 2006. His academic journey began at Keene State College, where he graduated in 1997. Germana has since become a respected professor of history at Keene State College, specializing in the intellectual, cultural, and political history of modern Germany. His scholarly contributions include two published books that explore the reception of Indian philosophy and religions in Germany during the late 18th and early 19th centuries.

Beyond his academic pursuits, Germana is actively involved in community and cultural initiatives. He is serving his first term as a member of the Board of Directors for New Hampshire Humanities, an organization dedicated to fostering cultural understanding and education. His commitment to public service extends to his legislative work, where he is a member of the Environment and Agriculture Committee. In this role, he addresses critical issues such as agricultural challenges, land use, environmental protection, and waste management.

Nicholas Germana is also a family man, married with three children and two grandchildren. He balances his professional and personal life with a passion for running, having completed several marathons. His dedication to both his constituents and his academic field underscores his multifaceted contributions to his community and state.
