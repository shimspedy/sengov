Andrew "Andy" Renzullo is an American politician and a member of the Republican Party. He was born on October 25, 1944. He is currently serving as a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing the Hillsborough 37 district. 

Renzullo first assumed office in December 2016 and has been serving ever since. Prior to this, he had served from 2004 to 2014. He has also been a co-chair of the New Hampshire House Republican Alliance, a member of the Municipal and County Government Committee, and president of the Hudson Town Council.

Renzullo was born and raised in Hudson, New Hampshire, U.S. He is married to Faith and they have three children. 

In terms of education, Renzullo graduated from Lewis Mills Regional High School. He earned a B.S. in chemical engineering from Northeastern University in 1968, an M.S. in chemistry from Suffolk University in 1975, and a J.D. from the Suffolk University School of Law in 1976. His professional experience includes working as a principal engineer, quality engineer, and engineer.

Throughout his political career, Renzullo has been assigned to various committees. He has served as the Chair of the Resources, Recreation and Development Committee. He has also served on the Labor, Industrial and Rehabilitative Services Committee.

Renzullo's dedication to public service and his extensive experience in engineering make him a unique figure in the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
