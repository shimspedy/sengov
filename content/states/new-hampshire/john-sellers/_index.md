John Sellers is an American politician who serves as a Republican member for the Grafton 18th district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He assumed office on December 7, 2022.

Born in Weymouth, Massachusetts, Sellers now resides in Bristol, New Hampshire. Prior to his political career, he served in the United States Air Force, demonstrating his commitment to public service from an early age.

In September 2022, Sellers ran for election to represent the Grafton 18th district in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He faced Andrew Ware in the Republican primary election and emerged victorious. Following his primary win, he went on to compete in the general election held in November 2022. In a closely contested race, Sellers defeated Carolyn Fluehr-Lobban, securing 50 percent of the votes.

Sellers' victory in the general election marked the beginning of his tenure in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. Since assuming office, he has been an active participant in the legislative process, contributing to the development and enactment of policies that impact the residents of the Grafton 18th district and the state of New Hampshire at large.

In addition to his legislative duties, Sellers has also been involved in various community service initiatives. He served as a member of the Bristol Budget Committee for four years and was also a member of the Newfound Area School District Budget Committee for the same duration.

Throughout his career, Sellers has demonstrated a strong commitment to serving his community and the state of New Hampshire. His experiences in the military, in business, and in public service have equipped him with a unique set of skills and perspectives that he brings to his role as a state representative. As he continues his tenure, Sellers remains dedicated to representing the interests of his constituents and working towards the betterment of the Grafton 18th district and the state of New Hampshire.
