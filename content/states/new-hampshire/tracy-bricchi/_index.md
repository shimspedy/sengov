Tracy Bricchi is a member of the Democratic Party serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She represents Merrimack District 15 and assumed office on December 4, 2024. Her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Tracy Bricchi has dedicated her life to public service and education. With over three decades of experience as a public school educator, she has taught high school mathematics and physics, and even served as an administrator for several years. After retiring in 2018, she briefly worked as a bookkeeper before deciding to step into the world of politics. Her passion for education and community service has been a driving force throughout her career.

In addition to her legislative duties, Tracy has been actively involved in local governance. She has served as the Chair of the Merrimack Valley School District School Board and as the President of the New Hampshire Retired Educators Association. Her commitment to improving education is evident in her efforts to address funding challenges and advocate for better resources for schools and teachers.

Tracy is also known for her personal touch and community engagement. She enjoys knitting and has even expressed interest in starting a "yarn caucus" to bring people together through shared hobbies. A lifelong resident of Concord, New Hampshire, she has a deep connection to her community and works tirelessly to represent its interests in the state legislature.

Her journey from educator to legislator highlights her dedication to making a positive impact on the lives of others. Tracy Bricchi continues to champion equality, education, and human rights, striving to create a brighter future for her constituents and the state of New Hampshire.
