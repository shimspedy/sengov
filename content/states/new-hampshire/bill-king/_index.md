Bill King is a prominent politician from New Hampshire. He was born in Queensbury, New York. After graduating from high school, he joined the United States Navy and served there for four years. During his time in the Navy, he was trained to be an aviation electrician's mate.

After spending four years in the Navy, he pursued his education further and earned a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering. His career spans over 36 years in manufacturing, during which he worked for General Motors. He held various positions including that of a manufacturing engineer, engineering manager, general manager, and business unit leader.

On November 3, 2020, King was elected to the New Hampshire House of Representatives where he represents the Hillsborough 23 district. He assumed office on December 2, 2020. He is a member of the Republican Party. His current term ends on December 4, 2024.

King resides in Milford, New Hampshire. He married Linda in 1985 and together, they had four children. They are all grown now and supporting themselves with the education - both college and self-study - they received. Unfortunately, in 2016, his wife Linda passed away due to sarcoma.

Throughout his political career, King has shown dedication and commitment to his role. His experience and background have equipped him with the skills and knowledge to effectively represent his constituents in the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
