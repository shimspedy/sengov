Marie Louise Bjelobrk is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Grafton District 5. She assumed office on December 4, 2024, and her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Marie Bjelobrk has dedicated her career to public service and community engagement. Before entering politics, she worked as a physical therapist for over two decades, establishing her own private practice. Her experience as a small business owner has shaped her approach to governance, emphasizing fiscal responsibility and the importance of balancing budgets. Marie is deeply committed to addressing the concerns of her constituents, focusing on issues such as rising taxes, the cost of living, and maintaining the quality of life for residents in her district.

As a state representative, Marie has been a strong advocate for preserving New Hampshire's unique values, including the absence of state sales or income tax. She is passionate about defending constitutional rights, ensuring secure elections, and supporting parental rights in education. Her legislative priorities also include improving infrastructure and promoting the right-to-work policies that benefit her constituents.

Marie represents the towns of Benton, Haverhill, Landaff, Piermont, and Warren within Grafton District 5. Her dedication to her community is evident in her commitment to listening to the voices of her constituents and addressing their needs through thoughtful and effective legislation. She continues to work tirelessly to ensure that New Hampshire remains a place where residents can thrive and enjoy a high quality of life.
