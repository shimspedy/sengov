Nancy A. Murphy is a dedicated and accomplished politician from New Hampshire. She has served as a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives for one term. In April 2021, Murphy won a seat on the Merrimack town council, further demonstrating her commitment to public service.

Born in Cambridge, Massachusetts, Murphy now resides in Merrimack, New Hampshire. She completed her high school education at Mount Saint Joseph Academy in Brighton, Massachusetts. Following this, she pursued higher education at Boston University, where she earned a Bachelor of Science in Nursing.

Murphy's professional career has been diverse and impactful. She has worked as a registered nurse, primarily in pediatrics, mental health, and child protection. Her experience in these fields has given her a unique perspective on healthcare and social issues, which she brings to her role in the House of Representatives.

In addition to her nursing career, Murphy has served as a nurse coordinator for the state Division for Children, Youth and Families' foster care health program. This role allowed her to make a direct impact on the lives of some of New Hampshire's most vulnerable residents.
