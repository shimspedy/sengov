Julie Gilman is a dedicated and experienced public servant who has been serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives since 2016. She represents Rockingham District 11 and is a member of the Democratic Party.

Born and raised in Exeter, New Hampshire, Julie has a deep connection to her community. She graduated from the Rhode Island School of Design with a Bachelor's degree in Architecture. Her career experience includes working as a manager of information systems with Cambridge Seven Associates, Inc.

Julie assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term ends on December 4, 2024. She has been a strong advocate for her constituents, working tirelessly to represent their interests in the state legislature.

In addition to her role in the state legislature, Julie has served as a selectwoman of the Town of Exeter. She has also been on the board of governors of the American Independence Museum and on the Exeter Heritage Commission. These roles have allowed her to contribute to her community in various ways and have given her a broad perspective on the issues facing her constituents.

Julie has been assigned to the Municipal and County Government Committee during her tenure in the House of Representatives. This committee considers all matters pertaining to town, city, and county officers; changes in municipal and county government; the boundary lines of towns, cities, and counties; the creation of new towns, cities, and counties; local revenue, including fees, property taxes, and exemptions; and such other matters as may be referred to it.

In the 2022 elections, Julie ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Rockingham 11. She won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Julie is married to Greg and they have two children, Meredith and Guinevere. She continues to be an active member of her community and is committed to serving her constituents to the best of her ability.
