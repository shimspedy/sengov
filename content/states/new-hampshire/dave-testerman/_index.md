Dave Testerman is a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Merrimack 3. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024. Testerman is affiliated with the Republican Party.

Testerman lives in New Hampshire. He served in the U.S. Air Force and has a rich educational background. He earned a Bachelor of Science in Aero-Engineering from California Polytechnic State University and a Master's in Business from the University of Northern Colorado.

His career experience includes working as a program manager and engineer with BAE/Sanders. In addition to his role as a representative, Testerman has served on various committees. These include the House Judiciary Committee (2023-2024), the Criminal Justice and Public Safety Committee (2019-2020, 2021-2022), and the New Hampshire committee assignments in 2017.

Testerman has also been involved in local governance, serving as a member of the SAU 18 (Franklin) School Board and a planning and zoning board member. His diverse experience extends to his service as a pilot, an engineer, a program manager, and a veteran of the US Air Force.

In the 2022 elections, Testerman ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Merrimack 3. He won in the general election on November 8, 2022. His victory was a testament to his dedication and commitment to serving the people of New Hampshire.

Dave Testerman is married and has four children. His personal and professional journey reflects his dedication to public service and his commitment to making a positive impact in his community.
