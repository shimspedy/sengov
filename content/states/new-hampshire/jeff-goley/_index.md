Jeffrey Goley is an American politician who serves as a Democratic member for the Hillsborough 21st district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He has been in office since December 7, 2022. His current term ends on December 4, 2024. 

Before his current term, Goley served in the House previously, representing Hillsborough 8 from 1998 to 2002. He was a candidate in Hillsborough 37 in 1996, and in Hillsborough 49 in 2002. Goley also ran for the New Hampshire State Senate in District 16 against incumbent Dave Boutin (R) in a special election on February 16, 2010.

In addition to his political career, Jeffrey Goley's experience includes working as the captain of the Manchester Fire Department. He has served as the vice president of the National Assembly of Sportsmen's Caucuses and as a moderator of Manchester Ward 1.

Throughout his political career, Goley has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 legislative session, Goley was assigned to the House Executive Departments and Administration Committee and the Fish and Game and Marine Resources Committee, where he served as Vice Chair. In the 2021-2022 legislative session, Goley was assigned to the House Executive Departments and Administration Committee. In the 2019-2020 legislative session, Goley served as the Chair of the House Executive Departments and Administration Committee.

Jeff is currently serving his 12th term in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He is a former chair of the Labor, Industrial, and Rehabilitative Services Committee and former chair of the Executive Department and Administration Committee.
