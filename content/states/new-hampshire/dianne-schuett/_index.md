Dianne Schuett is an American politician who serves as a Democratic member for the Merrimack 12th district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She has been a representative in the New Hampshire House for the 7th term, serving from 2009-10 & 2013-2022, representing Pembroke and Chichester.

She was born in California and grew up in Los Angeles and the San Fernando Valley. She graduated from James Monroe High School in Sepulveda, California, and attended Pierce College in Woodland Hills, California. 

Before her political career, she worked as the clerk of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She has also volunteered on the Town of Pembroke's Capital Improvement Projects Committee, as an EMT with Tri-Town Ambulance in Pembroke and Allenstown, as recording secretary of the New Hampshire Federal Credit Union's supervisory committee, and as quartermaster of the Blue Knights Law Enforcement Motorcycle Club.

In her political career, she has served in various capacities and on different committees. She has been assigned to the House Executive Departments and Administration Committee multiple times. She has also served on the Labor, Industrial and Rehabilitative Services committee, the Legislative Administration committee, and the Municipal and County Governments committee.

She is a widow of a Law Enforcement Officer and has three stepchildren, six grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren. Her current term in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Merrimack 12, began on December 7, 2022, and will end on December 4, 2024.
