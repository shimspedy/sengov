Travis Toner is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Belknap District 4. He assumed office on December 4, 2024, and his current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Born and raised in New Hampshire, Travis Toner has deep roots in the community he serves. He pursued higher education at the University of New Hampshire in Durham, where he earned two degrees, showcasing his dedication to academic and personal growth. Before entering politics, Toner gained valuable experience working in various state departments for a total of 15 years, including a stint as a corrections officer for two years. This background provided him with a comprehensive understanding of government operations and the challenges faced by public servants.

In addition to his professional endeavors, Toner is an entrepreneur, running his own home improvement and home security/automation company. His commitment to innovation and service extends beyond his business, as he is also an avid outdoorsman. Passionate about hunting and fishing, he has vowed to protect and defend the rights of sportsmen and gun owners, reflecting his dedication to preserving the traditions and values of his constituents.

Toner's journey into politics began with his election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives in 2024. Despite being a newcomer to elected office, he quickly established himself as a dedicated representative for the people of Belmont and the surrounding areas. His work on the Election Law Committee highlights his focus on ensuring fair and transparent electoral processes, as well as addressing issues related to campaign finance and redistricting.

Through his diverse experiences and unwavering commitment to his community, Travis Toner continues to serve as a strong advocate for the residents of Belknap District 4, striving to make a positive impact on their lives and the state of New Hampshire as a whole.
