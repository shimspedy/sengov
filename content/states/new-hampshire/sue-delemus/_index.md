Susan DeLemus is a Republican Party member serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She represents Strafford District 1. Susan assumed office on December 4, 2024, and her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Susan DeLemus resides in Rochester, New Hampshire, and has been actively involved in her community for many years. She has previously served in various capacities within the New Hampshire House of Representatives, showcasing her dedication to public service and her constituents. Over the years, she has been a member of several committees, including the State-Federal Relations and Veterans Affairs Committee, where she contributed to discussions and decisions impacting both state and federal policies.

Beyond her legislative work, Susan has been involved in local governance and community organizations. She served as the chair of the board of directors for Dustin Homestead, reflecting her commitment to addressing local issues and supporting community development. Her background also includes experience in education and advocacy, which she brings to her role as a legislator.

Susan's approach to governance is rooted in her belief in representing the voices of her constituents and addressing their concerns effectively. Her tenure in the New Hampshire House of Representatives is marked by her focus on legislative priorities that align with the needs and aspirations of the people she represents.
