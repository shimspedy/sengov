Jodi Nelson is a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Rockingham 13. She is a member of the Republican Party. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term ends on December 4, 2024.

Before her current tenure, she also served in the New Hampshire House of Representatives for Rockingham 6. As a legislator, she has sponsored several bills, contributing to the legislative process.

Jodi Nelson's career experience includes working as a businesswoman. This background provides her with a unique perspective on economic issues and the challenges faced by businesses in New Hampshire.

In the 2022 elections, she ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Rockingham 13. She won in the general election on November 8, 2022, demonstrating the trust and confidence that the people of Rockingham 13 have in her leadership.

As a representative, she is assigned to the Children and Family Law Committee. This committee considers matters relating to children and youth, their rights, obligations, and protection, including all matters covered by RSA 169-B, 169-C, and 169-D, divorce, custody and child support. She also serves on the Special Committee on Childcare.
