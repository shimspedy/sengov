Beth Richards is a prominent politician from New Hampshire. She is a member of the Democratic Party and represents the Merrimack 17 district in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term is set to end on December 4, 2024.

Beth Richards was elected to the New Hampshire House of Representatives on November 8, 2016, where she initially represented the Merrimack 13 district. She assumed office later in 2016. Her political journey has been marked by dedication and commitment to her constituents.

In terms of her educational background, Beth Richards earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Art History from the University of Pennsylvania in 1993. This academic foundation has undoubtedly played a role in shaping her perspective and approach to her political career.

Beyond her political endeavors, Beth Richards is also a successful business owner. She owns Local Baskit, a testament to her entrepreneurial spirit. Additionally, she has worked as the principal of Organized Communications, the development and marketing director of the Safe Routes to School National Partnership, and a senior consultant with M+R Strategic Services. She also served as the state vice president for development of the American Cancer Society.

Throughout her tenure, Beth Richards has been assigned to various committees. For the 2023-2024 term, she was assigned to the Legislative Administration Committee. In the 2021-2022 term, she was also part of the same committee. During the 2019-2020 term, she was assigned to the Environment and Agriculture Committee. At the beginning of the 2017 legislative session, she served on the Environment and Agriculture Committee as well.

Beth Richards ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Merrimack 17 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. Her victory is a testament to the trust and confidence that the people of Merrimack 17 have in her leadership.

On a personal note, Beth Richards resides in Concord, New Hampshire, and is a proud mother of two children. Her personal life reflects her commitment to family and community, values that are evident in her political work as well.
