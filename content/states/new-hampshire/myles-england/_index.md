Myles England is a Democratic Party member serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He represents Strafford District 12, having assumed office on December 4, 2024. His current term is set to conclude on December 2, 2026.

Myles England grew up in Rollinsford, New Hampshire, where he developed a strong sense of community and fairness. He attended Somersworth High School and later earned a bachelor's degree in economics from the University of New Hampshire. Early in his career, Myles worked in banking and investments in the Boston area. However, his passion for data management led him to a long-standing career at Liberty Mutual, where he has spent over two decades designing and developing data systems.

Myles has been deeply involved in local governance and community service. He has served as Chair of the Rollinsford Select Board and the Rollinsford Planning Board. Additionally, he has contributed to the Rollinsford Budget Committee, where he continues to apply his expertise in financial management and strategic planning. Beyond his professional and political endeavors, Myles has dedicated over a decade to supporting non-profit organizations like Homemakers Health Services.

Currently residing in Rollinsford with his spouse and two dogs, Myles remains committed to enhancing the economic and social vibrancy of New Hampshire. His leadership in the state legislature reflects his dedication to community-centered policies and his vision for a thriving and inclusive future for his constituents.
