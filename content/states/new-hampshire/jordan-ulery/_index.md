Jordan Ulery is an American politician born on October 19, 1949, in Elkhart, Indiana, U.S. He is a member of the Republican Party and currently represents the Hillsborough 13th district in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He first assumed office in 2004.

Ulery's tenure in the New Hampshire House of Representatives has been marked by his commitment to the Republican Party. He has been a delegate to the New Hampshire Republican convention and a candidate for New Hampshire House of Representatives, Hillsborough 66 in 2002. He has also been a member of the Hillsborough County Republican Committee.

In terms of education, Ulery is an alumnus of St. Anselm College, where he earned his B.A. in political science in 1971. He also holds a certificate in paramedical science from the New Hampshire Technical Institute.

Before his political career, Ulery worked in various professional roles. He has experience as an Emergency Medical Technician (EMT), manager, chief investigator, and investigator. He has also worked in insurance and other investigations for nearly 20 years and has been a member of the New Hampshire League of Investigators since 1994.

Throughout his legislative career, Ulery has been assigned to the House Ways and Means Committee for multiple terms. His current term in the New Hampshire House of Representatives began on December 7, 2022, and is set to end on December 4, 2024.

Ulery resides in Hudson, New Hampshire, U.S. His dedication to public service and his extensive professional experience make him a notable figure in New Hampshire politics.
