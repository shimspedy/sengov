George Grant is a member of the Republican Party serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He represents Sullivan County's District 5. Grant assumed office on December 4, 2024, and his current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

George Grant's career has been shaped by his experience as a business owner, specifically in the contracting industry. His professional background has provided him with a practical understanding of the challenges faced by small businesses and working professionals, which he brings to his role as a legislator. As a representative, Grant is committed to addressing the needs of his constituents in Springfield and Sunapee, the towns he represents. His focus includes fostering economic growth, supporting local businesses, and ensuring that the voices of his district are heard in the state legislature.

Grant's journey into politics began with his desire to make a tangible difference in his community. His election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives marked a significant milestone, as he successfully defeated the incumbent, Linda Tanner, in the 2024 general election. This victory underscored his ability to connect with voters and address their concerns effectively.

In addition to his legislative duties, Grant serves on the Housing Committee, where he works on policies aimed at improving housing affordability and accessibility. His role on the committee reflects his dedication to tackling pressing issues that impact the daily lives of New Hampshire residents.

Outside of his political and professional life, George Grant is known for his strong ties to his community. He resides in Sunapee, New Hampshire, where he continues to engage with local residents and participate in community events. His approachable demeanor and commitment to public service have earned him the respect and trust of his constituents.
