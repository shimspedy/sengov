Erin Kerwin is a dedicated public servant and a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough District 40. She assumed office on December 4, 2024, after winning the general election on November 5, 2024. Her commitment to her constituents and her passion for improving the lives of New Hampshire residents have been the driving forces behind her political career.

Born in Cleveland, Ohio, Erin Kerwin's journey into public service began with her strong educational foundation. She graduated from Shaker Heights High School and went on to earn a bachelor's degree from New York University in 1998. Later, she pursued a graduate degree from Simmons College in 2004. Her academic achievements reflect her dedication to learning and her belief in the transformative power of education.

Before entering politics, Erin Kerwin built a diverse career that included roles as a teacher, social worker, and homemaker. These experiences provided her with a deep understanding of the challenges faced by individuals and families in her community. Her work as a social worker, in particular, instilled in her a profound sense of empathy and a commitment to advocating for those in need.

Erin's involvement in local politics began when her eldest child started kindergarten. Her passion for education and community improvement quickly expanded into broader political engagement. Over the years, she has canvassed for local candidates, organized political events, and worked tirelessly to support initiatives that benefit her community.

As a representative of Manchester Wards 1, 3, 10, 11, and 12, Erin Kerwin is dedicated to ensuring access to quality jobs, excellent schools, and affordable healthcare for all New Hampshire residents. She serves on the Housing Committee, where she works to address critical issues such as affordable housing and homelessness. Her legislative priorities include advancing policies that improve the lives of her constituents while opposing measures that could harm the state.
