Raymond Peeples is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough District 14. He assumed office on December 4, 2024, and his current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Born in Buffalo, New York, Raymond Peeples has built a career as an information technology professional. He graduated from Lancaster Center High School and pursued further education at Daniel Webster College. Over the years, Peeples has been actively involved in his local community, serving in various capacities such as a selectman, a member of the budget committee, and a participant in the planning board. His dedication to public service extends to his involvement in the Merrimack Valley Water District Charter Committee and the Nashua Regional Planning Commission's Executive Board.

In addition to his professional and civic engagements, Peeples has a strong connection to his constituents in Litchfield, New Hampshire, where he resides with his family. His campaign focused on addressing pressing issues such as economic challenges, inflation, and the need for innovative conservative policies. Peeples aims to bring a fresh perspective to the legislative process, advocating for solutions that prioritize fiscal responsibility and community well-being.

Throughout his tenure, Peeples has been a vocal advocate for parental rights and has worked to address the impact of economic pressures on local and state budgets. His approach emphasizes finding creative ways to manage spending without imposing additional taxes, reflecting his commitment to practical and sustainable governance. As a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, Peeples continues to work diligently to represent the interests of his district and contribute to the state's legislative progress.
