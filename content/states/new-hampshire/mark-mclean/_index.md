Mark McLean is an American politician who serves as a Republican member for the Hillsborough 15th district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He has been in office since December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024.

McLean's political journey began when he served in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough 15 from 2014 to 2016. However, he was defeated in the general election held on November 8, 2016. Despite this setback, McLean remained undeterred and ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Hillsborough 15. His perseverance paid off when he won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

In addition to his political career, McLean has an impressive educational background. He earned a bachelor's degree in chemical engineering from the University of New Brunswick in 1993 and a master's degree in chemical engineering from Northeastern University in 1996. His career experience includes working as the principal engineer with ams OSRAM, a technical account manager with Cookson Electronics, and an engineering section head with Photocircuits Corporation.

McLean's contributions extend beyond his professional and political life. He has served as the treasurer of the Manchester Republican Committee, demonstrating his commitment to his party and his community. As a legislator, McLean has been assigned to several committees, including the Health, Human Services and Elderly Affairs Committee in 2023-2024, the House Judiciary Committee in 2021-2022 (where he served as Vice-Chair), and the House Judiciary Committee in 2019-2020.

His commitment to his constituents and his party is evident in his service in the New Hampshire House of Representatives and his involvement in the Manchester Republican Committee.
