James Guzofski is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He represents Rockingham District 1 and assumed office on December 4, 2024. His current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

James Guzofski has a rich and varied background that informs his work as a legislator. He holds a theology degree from the Baptist Bible College East and has been deeply involved in ministry for over three decades. For the past 14 years, he has served as the pastor of Destiny Christian Church, demonstrating his commitment to community and spiritual leadership. Beyond his pastoral duties, Guzofski has also been active in local government, serving as a selectman in Northwood, New Hampshire. In this role, he has contributed to various committees, including the budget committee and the police commission, showcasing his dedication to civic engagement.

In addition to his governmental and religious roles, Guzofski has a history of humanitarian work. He has partnered with an orphanage in Tanzania, making multiple trips to provide direct support and organize pastoral conferences for local leaders. His efforts reflect a global perspective and a commitment to service that extends beyond his immediate community.

Guzofski is also a chaplain for the Northwood Fire Department, further emphasizing his dedication to public service. In his personal life, he enjoys hobbies such as metal detecting and watching sports, which provide a balance to his professional and community-focused endeavors. His multifaceted career and personal interests make him a well-rounded and dedicated representative for his constituents.
