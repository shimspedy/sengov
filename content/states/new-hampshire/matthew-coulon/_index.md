Matthew Coulon is a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Grafton 5. He is a member of the Republican Party. His tenure began in 2022 and his current term is set to end in 2024.

Coulon ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Grafton 5. He won in the general election on November 8, 2022. 

In terms of his professional background, Coulon's career experience includes owning a business and working as a farmer. This experience has likely provided him with a unique perspective on the needs of small business owners and agricultural workers in his district.

In addition to his work as a legislator, Coulon has been assigned to the Environment and Agriculture Committee for the 2023-2024 term. This committee is responsible for considering all matters concerning agricultural and farm problems of the state, physical land use including current use, the various agricultural organizations, the protection of the state's environment from forms of pollution other than air or water pollution, and waste management issues.

In the 2022 general election for the New Hampshire House of Representatives Grafton 5, Coulon won alongside incumbent Roderick Ladd. Coulon received 44.6% of the votes, securing his position in the House of Representatives.

As a representative, Coulon is dedicated to serving his constituents in Grafton 5, which includes the towns of Benton, Haverhill, Landaff, Piermont, and Warren. His commitment to his role is evident in his active participation in the legislative process and his dedication to addressing the needs and concerns of his constituents.
