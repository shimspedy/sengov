Judy Aron is a prominent politician from New Hampshire. She is a member of the Republican Party and currently represents the Sullivan 4th district in the New Hampshire House of Representatives.

Aron's journey into politics began with her education. She earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Economics from the State University of New York at New Paltz. Her academic prowess was evident as she graduated Magna Cum Laude, also minoring in Business Administration and Computer Science.

Her political career took off on November 6, 2018, when she was elected to the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She assumed office on December 5, 2018, and has been serving the people of the Sullivan 4th district ever since.

In addition to her political career, Aron has also been involved in community service. She is the Secretary of the Acworth Community Project, an initiative that operates the Acworth Village Store. Furthermore, she has served as the president of the Acworth Woman's Club and worked as a columnist for the Acworth Newsletter.

Aron's personal life is rooted in South Acworth, New Hampshire, where she resides. She is married and is a proud mother of three children.

Throughout her tenure, Aron has been a part of several committees. In the 2023-2024 term, she was assigned to the Environment and Agriculture Committee, where she served as the Chair. In the 2021-2022 term, she was the Vice-Chair of the same committee and was also a part of the House Transportation Committee. In the 2019-2020 term, she served on the Environment and Agriculture Committee.
