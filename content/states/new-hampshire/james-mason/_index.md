James Mason, a member of the Republican Party, is a representative in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Merrimack 3. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024.

Mason ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Merrimack 3 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. He lives in Franklin, New Hampshire and earned a Bachelor of Science degree from the University of New Hampshire. His career experience includes owning businesses.

In addition to his role as a representative, Mason has served in other capacities. He was a former Chair of the Franklin Planning Board and a former Franklin City Councilor. He is currently retired.

During his tenure, Mason has been assigned to various committees. For the 2023-2024 term, he was assigned to the Labor, Industrial and Rehabilitative Services Committee. In the 2021-2022 term, he was assigned to the Environment and Agriculture Committee.

In the 2022 elections, Mason, along with incumbent Dave Testerman, defeated Scott Burns and Miriam Kovacs in the general election for New Hampshire House of Representatives Merrimack 3 on November 8, 2022.

Mason is married and has four children. He continues to serve his constituents and make a difference in his community.
