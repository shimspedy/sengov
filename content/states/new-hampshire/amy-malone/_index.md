Amy Malone is a Democratic Party member serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She represents Strafford District 9, having assumed office on December 4, 2024. Her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Amy has been a dedicated resident of Rochester, New Hampshire, for over 25 years. During this time, she has immersed herself in community service, both as a volunteer and through her professional endeavors. Her experiences as a mother to two adult children and as a foster parent have given her a deep understanding of the challenges faced by families. This personal insight has fueled her passion for advocating for children, families, and vulnerable populations.

In her role as a state representative, Amy is committed to fostering inclusivity and compassion within her community. She has been actively involved in initiatives aimed at empowering youth, supporting families, and addressing issues such as affordable housing and mental health resources. Her dedication to these causes is rooted in her belief that every individual deserves to feel valued and supported.

Beyond her legislative responsibilities, Amy has a history of local government involvement, including serving on the school board for four years and the city council for two years. Her work with local nonprofits has further demonstrated her commitment to creating a positive impact, whether through organizing community clean-up events or supporting programs for young adults and individuals experiencing homelessness.

Amy's approach to governance is centered on collaboration and listening to the voices of her constituents. She strives to build a community where everyone, regardless of their background, has the opportunity to thrive. Her leadership is characterized by a focus on practical solutions and a genuine desire to make a difference in the lives of those she represents.
