Joseph Barton is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Grafton District 1. He assumed office on December 4, 2024, and his current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Joseph Barton has been an active participant in the legislative process since his election. Representing the towns of Bath, Lisbon, Littleton, Lyman, Monroe, and Sugar Hill, he has focused on addressing the needs and concerns of his constituents. As a member of the Legislative Administration Committee, Barton has contributed to discussions and decisions regarding the legislative process, including matters related to elections, house resolutions, and the creation of study committees.

Before his tenure in the House of Representatives, Barton ran a successful campaign in the 2024 general election, securing his position as one of the representatives for Grafton District 1. His dedication to public service and his commitment to the values of his party have made him a notable figure in New Hampshire politics. Barton continues to work towards policies that reflect the interests of his district and the state as a whole.
