Kathleen Paquette is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough District 25. She assumed office on December 4, 2024, and her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Kathleen has been a proud resident of New Hampshire since her early childhood. She graduated from Memorial High School and went on to build a rewarding career in physical therapy, dedicating over 25 years to the field. Her work spanned short-term nursing facilities, the Visiting Nurses Association, and the Manchester School District, where she made a significant impact on the lives of her patients and colleagues.

In addition to her professional achievements, Kathleen is a small business owner in the travel industry. Her entrepreneurial spirit reflects her commitment to serving her community in diverse ways. Beyond her career, she is a devoted mother to four children and a proud grandmother to two. Her family remains a central part of her life, and she cherishes the time spent with them.

Kathleen's interests extend to exploring the natural beauty of New Hampshire. She enjoys hiking the state's trails with her two rescue pugs, Luna and Neville. She is also an enthusiast of craft breweries and live music, which she often incorporates into her leisure time. Her passion for travel and adventure is evident in her love for cruising and discovering new destinations.

As a first-term representative, Kathleen serves on the Criminal Justice and Public Safety Committee. In this role, she addresses critical issues such as the criminal code, corrections, sentencing, and public safety. Her dedication to her constituents and her diverse background make her a valuable member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
