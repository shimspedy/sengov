Timothy Soucy is a member of the Democratic Party and currently serves in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Merrimack 21. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024. 

Before his current tenure, Soucy served in the New Hampshire House of Representatives for Hillsborough 25, Hillsborough 34, and Merrimack 16. He ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Merrimack 21 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Soucy's career experience includes working as a firefighter. This background has likely shaped his perspective and approach to his legislative duties. 

Throughout his legislative career, Soucy has been assigned to several committees. In the 2023-2024 legislative session, he was assigned to the Labor, Industrial and Rehabilitative Services Committee. He also served on this committee in the 2021-2022 and 2019-2020 sessions. In the 2017 legislative session, he served on the Transportation committee, a position he also held in the 2015, 2013-2014, and 2009-2010 sessions.

In addition to his legislative duties, Soucy has sponsored numerous bills, demonstrating his active involvement in shaping state policy. 

Outside of his political career, Soucy is a retired firefighter from Nashua. He is married to Karen Soucy and they have five children and two grandchildren. He represents Concord Ward: 7.

Soucy's dedication to public service, his extensive experience in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, and his background as a firefighter make him a unique and valuable member of the state legislature.
