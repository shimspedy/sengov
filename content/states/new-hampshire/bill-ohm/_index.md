Bill Ohm is a Republican Party member serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He represents Hillsborough District 10 and assumed office on December 4, 2024. His current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Bill Ohm has a distinguished academic and professional background. He earned a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1968 and later pursued an MBA at Harvard Business School, graduating in 1972. His career spans various leadership roles, including serving as Vice President and General Manager at ADE Technologies, where he contributed significantly to the high-tech industry.

In addition to his professional achievements, Ohm has been actively involved in community service and local governance. He has served as a trustee for a non-profit aid society and participated in the regional Medical Reserve Corps. His commitment to public service extends to his role as Deputy for Emergency Management in Chelmsford, Massachusetts. Beyond his political and professional endeavors, Ohm is an outdoor enthusiast, enjoying activities such as hiking and target shooting. He is also a licensed ham radio operator, showcasing his diverse interests and skills.

Throughout his tenure in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, Bill Ohm has been a dedicated public servant, focusing on legislative matters that impact his constituents and the broader community. His extensive experience and commitment to service continue to shape his contributions to the state legislature.
