Stephanie Grund is a Democratic member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough District 34. She assumed office on December 4, 2024, and her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Stephanie Grund has a rich background in community leadership and public service. She graduated from Case Western Reserve University with a Bachelor of Science in Accounting and began her professional journey as an auditor with Arthur Andersen. Over the years, she transitioned into the corporate sector, where she worked with multinational firms, focusing on financial consolidations, audits, and SEC filings. Her expertise in accounting and finance has been a cornerstone of her career.

After moving to Amherst, New Hampshire, in 2004, Stephanie became deeply involved in her local community. She volunteered extensively in schools, served on the Amherst School District Ways & Means Committee, and led the Amherst Junior Women's Club. Her dedication to youth development was evident in her role as a Girl Scout Leader, a position she held for over twelve years. In 2020, she was elected to the Souhegan Cooperative School Board, where she served as Chairperson for the fiscal years 2024 and 2025.

In her role as a state representative, Stephanie Grund is a member of the Municipal and County Government Committee. Her work in this committee involves addressing matters related to town, city, and county governance, including local revenue, property taxes, and municipal boundaries. Her commitment to public service and her extensive experience in both professional and community settings make her a dedicated and effective representative for her constituents in Hillsborough District 34.
