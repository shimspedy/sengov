Pam Brown is a member of the Republican Party serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She represents Rockingham District 14 and assumed office on December 4, 2024. Her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Pam Brown has a strong academic background and professional experience that complements her role in public service. She graduated from South Kingstown High School and went on to earn a bachelor's degree from the University of Rhode Island in 1981. She furthered her education by obtaining a Ph.D. from George Mason University in 1987. Professionally, she has worked as an economist, a role that has undoubtedly shaped her approach to policy-making and governance.

In her capacity as a state representative, Pam Brown has been actively involved in legislative matters, particularly those that impact her constituents in Rockingham District 14. Her dedication to public service is evident in her commitment to addressing the needs and concerns of her community. Beyond her legislative duties, she has also been a member of various local organizations, including the Country Pond Fish & Game Club and the Country Pond Lake Association, reflecting her engagement with community and environmental issues.

Pam Brown's journey into politics was marked by her successful election campaign in 2024, where she ran as a Republican candidate and secured her position in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. Her tenure is characterized by a focus on fiscal responsibility, community development, and education, among other key areas. Her efforts aim to create a positive impact on the lives of her constituents and contribute to the overall progress of her district and the state of New Hampshire.
