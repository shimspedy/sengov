Sue Newman is a distinguished member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing the Hillsborough 4 district. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term ends on December 4, 2024. 

Born in Rochester, New York, Sue Newman moved to Nashua, New Hampshire in 1981 after living in various places including Germany, Florida, Georgia, and Virginia. Her journey has been diverse and enriching, contributing to her broad perspective and understanding of different cultures and communities.

Before her political career, Sue Newman worked as a realtor, gaining valuable experience in the real estate industry. Her career experience extends beyond real estate, as she has served on the Nashua Board of Education and the Nashua Board of Selectmen. These roles allowed her to engage directly with her community, understand its needs, and work towards its betterment.

On November 8, 2016, Newman was elected to the New Hampshire House of Representatives where she represents the Hillsborough 29 district. She assumed office later in 2016. As a Democrat, she has been an active participant in the political sphere, contributing to the development and implementation of various policies and initiatives.

In her personal life, Sue Newman resides in Nashua, New Hampshire. She is married to fellow state representative Ray Newman. Together they have three children and six grandchildren. Her family life brings her joy and provides her with a strong support system.

Throughout her political career, Sue Newman has been assigned to the Public Works and Highways Committee multiple times, indicating her interest and expertise in public works and infrastructure. Her work in these committees has been instrumental in shaping the infrastructure policies of New Hampshire.

Sue Newman's journey from Rochester to Nashua, from a realtor to a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, is a testament to her dedication, hard work, and commitment to serving her community. As she continues her political career, she remains a strong advocate for her constituents, working tirelessly to represent their interests and improve their lives.
