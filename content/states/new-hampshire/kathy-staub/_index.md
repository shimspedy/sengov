Kathy Staub is a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough 25. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term ends on December 4, 2024. Staub ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Hillsborough 25 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Staub is a lifelong resident of Manchester, New Hampshire. She graduated from Central High School in 1975. She earned a B.A. in anthropology from the University of New Hampshire in 1979 and an M.A. in American studies from Boston College in 1992. 

Staub's career experience includes working as a museum educator and a community organizer. She co-founded the Manchester Coalition for Quality Education and the Manchester Foundation for Education and served on the Manchester Board of School Committee. 

In addition to her work in education and community organizing, Staub has been involved in local government. She served on the Manchester Board of School Committee from 2012 to 2015. 

As a legislator, Staub has been assigned to the House Transportation Committee. She has sponsored several bills during her tenure, although the specific bills she has sponsored are not listed.

Staub is a member of the Democratic Party. She and her husband have two grown children. She is now retired.
