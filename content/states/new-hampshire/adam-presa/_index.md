Adam Presa, a member of the Republican Party, serves in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough District 12. He assumed office on December 4, 2024, and his current term is set to conclude on December 2, 2026.

Born and raised in Merrimack, New Hampshire, Adam Presa has deep roots in the community he now represents. He pursued higher education at New England College, earning a bachelor's degree in 2024 and completing his MBA in 2025. His academic background in business administration has equipped him with the skills to navigate the complexities of governance and public service.

Before entering politics, Presa built a career as a realtor, working with prominent firms like eXp Realty and Realty One Group. His professional experience has given him a unique perspective on the housing market and the economic challenges faced by his constituents. This insight has been instrumental in shaping his legislative priorities, which include economic development, consumer protection, and fostering a business-friendly environment in New Hampshire.

In the House of Representatives, Presa serves on the Commerce and Consumer Affairs Committee. This role allows him to address issues related to commerce, banking, insurance, and the licensing of businesses. His work on the committee reflects his commitment to supporting local businesses and ensuring fair practices in the marketplace.

Adam Presa's journey into public service is marked by a dedication to his community and a desire to make a meaningful impact. As a first-term representative, he has already begun to establish himself as a thoughtful and proactive legislator, focused on addressing the needs of his district and contributing to the broader goals of the state. His leadership and vision continue to resonate with the people of Hillsborough District 12, who look to him as a voice for their concerns and aspirations.
