Sanjeev K. Manohar is a member of the Democratic Party serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He represents Hillsborough District 9, having assumed office on December 4, 2024. His current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Sanjeev Manohar has a distinguished academic and professional background. He earned a graduate degree from Southern Illinois University, Carbondale, in 1986, followed by a Ph.D. from the University of Pennsylvania in 1992. His career spans academia and industry, with significant contributions as a chemical engineer. He has been actively involved with professional organizations such as the American Institute of Chemical Engineers and the American Chemical Society.

Manohar's journey into public service is rooted in his commitment to community and advocacy. He has lived in Nashua, New Hampshire, since 2006, where he has been an active member of the local community. His professional expertise includes nanotechnology, biotechnology, and polymer chemistry, with numerous publications and patents to his name. Beyond his technical achievements, he has served in leadership roles, including as an Associate Dean of Engineering and Department Chair at UMass Lowell. His dedication to education and innovation has been a cornerstone of his career.

In addition to his professional accomplishments, Manohar has a passion for public engagement and fostering dialogue across diverse perspectives. He believes in the power of collaboration and open-mindedness to address complex societal challenges. His campaign emphasized affordable housing, quality education, economic growth, and protecting individual rights, reflecting his commitment to creating a better future for his constituents.

Sanjeev Manohar's unique blend of scientific expertise, leadership experience, and dedication to public service positions him as a valuable representative for Hillsborough District 9. His approach to governance is informed by his belief in evidence-based decision-making and his desire to bridge divides for the greater good.
