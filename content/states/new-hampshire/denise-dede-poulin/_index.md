Denise DeDe-Poulin is a member of the Republican Party serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She represents Strafford District 6. Denise assumed office on December 4, 2024, and her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Denise DeDe-Poulin has been a long-time resident of Rochester, New Hampshire, where she has lived for over three decades. Her deep roots in the community are reflected in her active involvement in local affairs. She has dedicated years to volunteering with the local Parent-Teacher Association (PTA) and has spent countless hours supporting youth sports, including working at local softball fields and concession stands. Her commitment to civic engagement extends to her role at the local polling station, where she has served during every election and primary for the past eight years.

In addition to her public service, Denise works full-time at the Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) office in Rochester, where she is a valued member of the clerical team. Her professional and personal experiences have shaped her dedication to keeping New Hampshire tax-free and ensuring the safety and well-being of its citizens.

Outside of her professional and political life, Denise enjoys hiking with her three dogs and cycling around Rochester during the warmer months. Her love for the outdoors and her community is evident in her daily life and her approach to public service. As a mother, wife, and artist, Denise brings a multifaceted perspective to her role in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, striving to represent her constituents with integrity and dedication.
