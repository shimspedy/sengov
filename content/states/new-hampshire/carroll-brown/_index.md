Carroll Brown Jr. is a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Grafton 10. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024. He ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Grafton 10 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Carroll Brown Jr. was born in Hampton, New Hampshire, and currently resides in Bristol, New Hampshire. His career experience includes working with the New Hampshire Department of Environmental Services and as a musician. He has served on the Town of Bristol Conservation Commission, as the chair of the Town of Bristol Selectboard, and as a little league coach and umpire.

In the general election for New Hampshire House of Representatives Grafton 10, Carroll Brown Jr. defeated Taylor Largmann. Carroll Brown Jr. received 59.2% of the votes, amounting to 1,255 votes. Taylor Largmann received 40.7% of the votes, amounting to 863 votes.

In addition to his legislative duties, Brown was assigned to the Fish and Game and Marine Resources Committee in 2023-2024. His work in the legislature and his commitment to his constituents demonstrate his dedication to public service.
