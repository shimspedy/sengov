Hoyt is a member of the Democratic Party and ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Grafton 7. His victory in the general election on November 8, 2022, was a significant achievement. 

Hoyt's life is rooted in New Hampshire. He is a magna cum laude graduate in philosophy and anthropology from Plymouth State College, class of 1980. This educational background has undoubtedly contributed to his understanding of society and his constituents.

In addition to his political career, Hoyt has a diverse professional background. He has worked as a licensed real estate broker, providing him with a deep understanding of the housing market and property laws. This experience is invaluable in his role as a representative, where he often deals with legislation related to housing and property rights.

Hoyt's commitment to his community extends beyond his political and professional life. He has served as an NCAA soccer referee for 25 years, with five final four assignments. His involvement in sports demonstrates his dedication to fair play and community engagement.
