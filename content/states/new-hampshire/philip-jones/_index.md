Philip Jones is an American politician and a member of the Democratic Party. He serves as a representative for the Cheshire 3rd district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives.

Jones assumed office on December 7, 2022. His current term is set to end on December 4, 2024. Prior to his election, Jones ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Cheshire 3.

In the general election held on November 8, 2022, Jones emerged victorious. He defeated Jerry Sickels, securing 69.9% of the votes which amounted to 1,484 votes. Jerry Sickels, the Republican candidate, secured 30.0% of the votes, amounting to 636 votes. There were also a few write-in votes.

Before the general election, Jones had advanced from the Democratic primary for New Hampshire House of Representatives Cheshire 3 on September 13, 2022. In the primary, he secured 99.6% of the votes, amounting to 550 votes.

As a legislator, Jones has sponsored several bills. However, the specific bills he sponsored are not listed due to an error in retrieving the bills.

In terms of committee assignments for the period 2023-2024, Jones was assigned to the House Transportation Committee.

Jones's work as a representative is characterized by his commitment to his constituents in the Cheshire 3rd district and his dedication to the principles of the Democratic Party.
