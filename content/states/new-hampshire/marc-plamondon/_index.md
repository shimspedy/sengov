Marc Plamondon is an American politician and a member of the Democratic Party. He represents the Hillsborough 3rd district in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. 

Marc assumed office on May 31, 2023, and his current term is set to end on December 4, 2024. Prior to his current role, Marc served as a Nashua Alderman, where he was known for his practical leadership and focus on commonsense solutions. He was at the forefront of issues that mattered to residents, such as parking and parks, to enhance the quality of life in Nashua.

In 2023, Marc ran in a special election for the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Hillsborough 3. He won the special general election on May 16, 2023, defeating David Narkunas and securing 71.6% of the votes. This victory marked the beginning of his tenure in the New Hampshire House of Representatives.

Marc's political journey reflects his commitment to serving his community and his dedication to improving the lives of the residents of Nashua and the Hillsborough 3rd district. His practical approach to problem-solving and his experience as a local leader continue to guide his work in the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
