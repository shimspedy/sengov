William Infantine, also known as Will, is an American politician who serves as a Republican member for the Hillsborough 16th district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He is currently the chair of the Labor, Industrial and Rehabilitative Services Committee.

Infantine has been a resident of Manchester, New Hampshire, since 1997 and is a proud father of two children. He graduated from Milford Area Senior High School in 1982 and later earned a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration from Saint Michael's College in 1986.

His political career in the New Hampshire House of Representatives began in 2002 and continued until 2016. After a brief hiatus, he returned to serve again in 2020 and continues to do so. His current term is set to end on December 4, 2024.

In addition to his political career, Infantine is also a business owner. He co-founded Aspen Insurance Agency, demonstrating his entrepreneurial spirit.

Throughout his tenure, Infantine has been assigned to various committees. However, his most notable assignment has been to the Labor, Industrial and Rehabilitative Services Committee, where he currently serves as the chair.

Infantine's dedication to his role is evident in his re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Hillsborough 16. He won in the general election held on November 8, 2022.

His contributions to the New Hampshire House of Representatives and his commitment to serving the Hillsborough 16th district make him a notable figure in American politics.
