Before joining the New Hampshire House of Representatives, Hemingway had a distinguished career in the New Hampshire Army National Guard, where he retired with the rank of Master Sergeant (MSG/E8). His military service instilled in him a strong sense of discipline, leadership, and a deep understanding of the value of teamwork.

In addition to his military background, Hemingway has been actively involved in local governance. He served as a Claremont City Councilor at Large, where he worked on various initiatives to improve the quality of life for residents in his community. As a state representative, Hemingway focuses on issues that matter most to the people of Sullivan County District 6. He is known for his pragmatic approach to problem-solving and his ability to work collaboratively with colleagues across the political spectrum. His legislative priorities include supporting veterans, enhancing public safety, and promoting economic development in his district.

Hemingway's personal life reflects his strong ties to his community. He resides in Claremont, New Hampshire, with his family. He is married and has three children, and he values the support and encouragement of his loved ones as he navigates the responsibilities of public office.
