Lisa Mazur is a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough 44. She is a member of the Republican Party. Her tenure began in 2022 and her current term is set to end in 2024.

Lisa Mazur lives in Goffstown, New Hampshire. Before her political career, she had a diverse professional background. She owned Piper's Cove and worked as the outreach director of Manchester Christian Church. As an outreach director, she organized, led, and project managed a yearly city and statewide outreach event where hundreds of volunteers gathered to serve thousands in their community. She also led, organized, and participated in several disaster relief trips throughout New Hampshire and the USA, as well as over sixty global outreach trips to third world countries, working alongside community leaders and women's co-ops.

In recognition of her efforts, she was chosen as one of New Hampshire's 20 Outstanding Women in 2016. Currently, she serves as a Venue Operations Director.

Lisa Mazur ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Hillsborough 44 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. During her time in office, she has been assigned to the Health, Human Services, and Elderly Affairs Committee.

In addition to her political and professional roles, Lisa Mazur has been actively involved in local and state candidate campaigns and other election activities. She became a US citizen in 2020, naturalized from Canada.

Lisa Mazur is dedicated to serving her community and state, and she continues to contribute to the betterment of New Hampshire through her role in the House of Representatives.
