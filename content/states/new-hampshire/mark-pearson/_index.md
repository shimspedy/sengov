Mark Pearson is an American politician who serves as a Republican member for the Rockingham 34th district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He was born on June 5, 1949, in Leominster, Massachusetts, U.S. 

Pearson resides in Hampstead, New Hampshire. He is a graduate of Leominster High School (1967). He earned his B.A. Cum Laude and with Honors in History from Williams College, Williamstown, MA, in 1971. He was elected to Phi Beta Kappa during his time at Williams College. He then went on to earn his M.A. with Honours from Oxford University, England, in 1973. He also holds a Doctorate in Theology from Boston University, Boston, MA which he received in 1999.

He is married to Dr. Mary Pearson and they have three adult children, two grandchildren, and one basset hound. Pearson serves as the Rector of Trinity Church, Kingston, NH. He is also the Chief Executive Officer of New Creation Healing Center, Kingston, NH.

In his political career, Pearson serves as the Chairman of the Children and Family Law Committee. In 2023, Pearson supported Ron DeSantis' campaign to be re-elected as the Governor of Florida.
