Susan DeRoy is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She represents Strafford District 3 and assumed office on December 4, 2024. Her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Born in York, Maine, Susan DeRoy graduated from R.W. Traip Academy. Her professional journey has been diverse and impactful, including roles as a banking executive, part owner of a multinational multimedia company, and small business owner. Her career reflects a commitment to both economic development and community engagement.

DeRoy has been actively involved in numerous civic and community organizations. She has served as a member of the Rochester Rotary, Sanford Chamber of Commerce, and Kiwanis. Her dedication to education and healthcare is evident through her affiliations with the Monarch School of New England and Frisbie Hospital. Additionally, she has contributed to the Rochester Economic Development initiatives, showcasing her focus on fostering growth and opportunities within her community.

Her leadership extends to her local government involvement, where she has held positions on the New Durham Budget Committee and the Capital Improvement Program (CIP) Committee. These roles underline her dedication to fiscal responsibility and strategic planning for her district.

Susan DeRoy's journey into politics is marked by her commitment to serving her constituents and addressing the needs of her community. Her diverse professional background and active civic engagement make her a dynamic and dedicated representative for Strafford District 3.
