Kevin Scully is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He represents Hillsborough District 8, having assumed office on December 4, 2024. His current term is set to conclude on December 2, 2026.

Born in Everett, Massachusetts, Kevin Scully has a rich educational and professional background. He graduated from Boston College High School and later earned a bachelor's degree in Geology and Geophysics from Boston College in 1978. Over the course of his career, he accumulated three decades of experience in hazardous waste site investigations and cleanups. Initially, he worked hands-on in the field, but as his career progressed, he transitioned into management roles, overseeing complex environmental projects.

Scully has been a resident of Nashua, New Hampshire, for many years, where he has been actively involved in his community. His dedication to public service is evident in his commitment to addressing environmental and agricultural issues as part of his legislative responsibilities. He serves on the Environment and Agriculture Committee, where he focuses on matters such as land use, waste management, and the protection of the state's natural resources.

In addition to his professional and legislative work, Kevin Scully is a family man. He is married and has two adult children. Now retired from his professional career, he devotes his time and energy to being an effective lawmaker and contributing to the betterment of his community and state. His journey reflects a blend of technical expertise, community involvement, and a steadfast commitment to public service.
