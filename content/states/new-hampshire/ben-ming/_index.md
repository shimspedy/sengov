Ben Ming is an American politician who serves as a Democratic member for the Hillsborough 35th district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, which comprises the town of Hollis. He is the first Chinese American elected to the New Hampshire General Court.

Ming assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024. He ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Hillsborough 35 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Born and raised in Hollis, New Hampshire, Ming graduated from Buckingham Browne & Nichols in 1999. He earned a B.S. in computer science from The George Washington University in 2003, an A.L.M. in information technology with a concentration in software engineering from Harvard University, and a J.D. from Suffolk University Law School.

His career experience includes working as an attorney. As a legislator, he has sponsored several bills, demonstrating his commitment to serving his constituents and making a positive impact on his community.

In addition to his political career, Ming is also known for his contributions to the field of law and his dedication to public service. His work has earned him recognition and respect from his peers and constituents alike.
