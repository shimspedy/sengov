Vicki Wilson is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Rockingham District 9. She assumed office on December 4, 2024, and her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Vicki Wilson's journey into public service reflects her deep commitment to her community and her dedication to addressing the needs of her constituents. Before joining the New Hampshire House of Representatives, she actively engaged with her local community in Sandown, New Hampshire. Her experience as a ten-year member of the Sandown Planning Board provided her with valuable insights into the town's development needs and the importance of sustainable growth. During this time, she worked tirelessly to ensure that the voices of residents were heard and that decisions were made with their best interests in mind.

In addition to her work on the Planning Board, Vicki spent two years as a political canvasser, connecting directly with residents across New Hampshire. This role allowed her to listen to the concerns, hopes, and ideas of people from diverse communities, further deepening her understanding of the unique challenges faced by each neighborhood. Her dedication to accessible and responsive representation has been a cornerstone of her political career.

As a state representative, Vicki Wilson has focused on several key issues, including education freedom, fiscal responsibility, and the protection of constitutional rights. She is a strong advocate for reducing taxes, cutting wasteful spending, and opposing sanctuary city policies. Her legislative priorities reflect her commitment to creating a better future for her constituents and ensuring that New Hampshire remains a great place to live and work.

Vicki's dedication to public service and her unwavering commitment to her community have earned her the trust and respect of her constituents. She continues to work diligently to represent the interests of Rockingham District 9 and to make a positive impact on the lives of the people she serves.
