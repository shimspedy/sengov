Skip Rollins is a dedicated and experienced politician from the state of New Hampshire. He is a member of the Republican Party and represents the Sullivan 3 district in the New Hampshire House of Representatives.

Rollins began his political journey on November 6, 2012, when he was elected to the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He assumed office on December 5, 2012, and has been serving the people of New Hampshire ever since. His current term began on December 7, 2022, and is set to end on December 4, 2024.

In addition to his role as a representative, Rollins also holds the position of Vice Chair on the Committee on State-Federal Relations and Veterans Affairs. This committee is responsible for considering all matters pertaining to the National Guard and other military or veterans organizations within the state, potential actions by the U.S. Congress which will have a major impact on the state and the New England region, and all proposed resolutions petitioning the Congress on any matter. The committee also serves as a liaison between the New Hampshire House and the United States Congress and oversees emergency management.

Rollins resides in Newport, New Hampshire, a town he also represents along with Unity and Charlestown. He is currently in his 7th year in the New Hampshire House representing these towns.

Throughout his political career, Rollins has shown a strong commitment to his constituents and has worked tirelessly to represent their interests in the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
