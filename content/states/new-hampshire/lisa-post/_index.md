Lisa Post is an American politician who serves as a Republican member for the Hillsborough 42nd district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term ends on December 4, 2024.

Before her current tenure, she served as a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives from the Hillsborough 4th district from 2020 until December 7, 2022. She has been a part of the Republican party and has made significant contributions to her party and the state.

Lisa Post's career experience includes working as an executive assistant in the presidential scheduling office during the presidency of Ronald Reagan. She has also worked for Fortune 500 companies, public and private nonprofit schools, and as a small business owner. Her diverse career experience has equipped her with a unique perspective and understanding of both the private and public sectors.

In the 2023-2024 term, Post was assigned to the Commerce and Consumer Affairs Committee. In the previous term (2021-2022), she was assigned to the Resources, Recreation and Development Committee. These assignments reflect her commitment to various aspects of governance and public service.

In the general election for the New Hampshire House of Representatives Hillsborough 42 on November 8, 2022, Lisa Post emerged victorious, securing 17.0% of the votes. Her victory underscored the trust and confidence that the people of Hillsborough 42 have in her leadership.
