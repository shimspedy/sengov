Amy Bradley is an American politician who serves as a Democratic member for the Hillsborough 41st district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She has been a leader in Cambria County for three decades and is known for her values of education, hard work, positivity, and compassion. She is a married mother of three and understands the region and its families well.

Amy Bradley was a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives from the Hillsborough 43rd district from 2020 until December 7, 2022. After that, she assumed office for the Hillsborough 41st district. In 2023, Bradley took on the role of New Hampshire State Director at Run for Something, an organization dedicated to supporting young diverse progressives to run for down-ballot races in order to build a bench for the future.

Amy is a life-long resident of New Hampshire and lives in Manchester with her husband, two daughters, and two dogs. During her career, she has worked to shape policies and actions for over 11 years in areas such as Women's Rights, Gun Violence Prevention, Health Care, and Education. She holds a Bachelor's Degree in Human Services and a Masters Degree in Public Policy.

Amy is committed to showcasing positive solutions for communities, fighting for values, promoting workers and local jobs, and delivering for the county both at home and in Harrisburg. She stands for supporting safe and strong communities, backing state and local police and first responders. She also fights for local jobs, supports job creators, and works to attract new jobs to the county. In the field of education and healthcare, Amy aims to give parents more control of their children's education and fight for quality health care for families.

Amy Bradley's dedication to her community and her commitment to positive change make her a respected and influential figure in New Hampshire politics.
