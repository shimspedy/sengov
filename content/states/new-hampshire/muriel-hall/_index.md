Muriel Hall is a dedicated public servant and a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She resides in Bow, New Hampshire. 

Hall has a rich educational background. She earned an Associate of Arts (A.A.) degree in foreign languages from Mt. San Antonio College. She then pursued a Bachelor of Science (B.S.) degree in elementary education from Keene State College. Furthering her education, she obtained a Master of Education (M.Ed.) degree in computer technology from Plymouth State University.

Hall has had a long and fulfilling career as an educator. Her passion for teaching and her commitment to shaping young minds is evident in her four-decade-long career in education. After her retirement, she decided to serve her community in a different capacity by entering politics.

As a representative, she has been assigned to the Committee on Education. The committee is responsible for considering subjects relating to the regulation of school districts and schools, the postsecondary college system, the University System of New Hampshire, and other matters concerning education.

Hall's dedication to education and her extensive experience make her an invaluable asset to the committee and to the New Hampshire House of Representatives. Her work continues to impact her community and the state of New Hampshire positively.
