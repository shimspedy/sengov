Sean Durkin is an American politician who serves as a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He represents the Coos 1st district and is a member of the Republican Party.

Durkin assumed office on January 31, 2024. His current term is set to end on December 4, 2024. Prior to his current position, the Coos 1st district was represented by Troy Merner.

Durkin's journey to the New Hampshire House of Representatives was marked by a special election. He ran as a Republican candidate and emerged victorious in the special general election held on January 23, 2024.

In the special general election for New Hampshire House of Representatives Coos 1, Durkin faced off against Cathleen Fountain. Durkin secured a decisive victory, garnering 60.2% of the votes, which amounted to 1,289 votes. Fountain, on the other hand, received 39.8% of the votes, totaling 851 votes.

Before the general election, Durkin also participated in the special Republican primary for New Hampshire House of Representatives Coos 1. The primary was held on December 5, 2023, and Durkin defeated Pamela Kathan, securing 68.2% of the votes (122 votes) against Kathan's 31.8% (57 votes).

Durkin's victory in the special general election marked the beginning of his tenure in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. As a representative, Durkin is committed to serving the people of the Coos 1st district and upholding the principles of the Republican Party.
