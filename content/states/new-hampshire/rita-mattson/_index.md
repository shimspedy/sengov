Rita Mattson is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Cheshire District 18. She assumed office on December 4, 2024, and her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Rita grew up in Atkinson, New Hampshire, and graduated from Timberlane Regional High School in 1973. She has spent her career as a blue-collar worker, embodying the values of hard work and dedication. Currently, she resides in Dublin, New Hampshire, with her husband. Rita has been an active member of her community, serving as the Chair of the Dublin Area GOP and Vice Treasurer of the Cheshire County Republican Committee. Her involvement in local government reflects her commitment to representing the interests of her constituents.

Rita's journey into politics began in 2019 when she attended her first town GOP meeting. Since then, she has been a vocal advocate for conservative values and has worked tirelessly to bring common sense to Concord. Her campaign emphasized the importance of preserving freedom and maintaining the unique character of New Hampshire. Rita is known for her straightforward approach and dedication to thoroughly understanding legislative matters before casting her vote.

In her personal life, Rita enjoys hobbies such as building dollhouses, sewing, painting, and traveling in her trailer. She cherishes time spent with her family, including her married daughter and two granddaughters. Her diverse interests and strong community ties make her a relatable and approachable representative for the people of Cheshire District 18.
