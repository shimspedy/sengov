Lori Korzen, a Republican, serves in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Coos County District 7. She assumed office on December 4, 2024, and her current term is set to conclude on December 2, 2026.

From a young age, Lori Korzen was instilled with a deep sense of service and compassion. Her journey into public service began with her dedication to helping others, which she demonstrated through her work in emergency medical services. As an Advanced Emergency Medical Technician and a Wilderness Emergency Medical Technician on Mt. Washington, she devoted herself to saving lives and supporting those in need. Her passion for advocacy extended to youth leadership and child welfare, where she worked tirelessly to ensure that every child had the opportunity to thrive. This commitment led her to open her home to foster children, further exemplifying her dedication to making a positive impact.

In addition to her work in public service, Lori Korzen is a small business owner in the North Country. Her entrepreneurial spirit and community involvement have made her a respected figure in her district. She has also been actively involved in local government, serving as a Planning Board Alternate. Her love for animals, particularly dogs, has driven her to participate in dog rescue organizations, fostering and training therapy dogs that bring comfort to nursing homes, hospitals, and schools. Her Newfoundland dogs have even earned titles in competitions such as Obedience, Rally, and Dock Dogs.

As a mother and grandmother, Lori Korzen draws inspiration from her family to continue her work in creating meaningful change. Her role in the New Hampshire House of Representatives allows her to advocate for the rights and well-being of her constituents, particularly in areas related to children and family law. Her dedication to service, both in her personal and professional life, underscores her commitment to building a stronger and more compassionate community.
