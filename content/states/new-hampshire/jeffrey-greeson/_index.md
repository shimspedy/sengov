Jeffrey Greeson is a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Grafton 6. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024. Greeson ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Grafton 6 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Greeson is a member of the Republican Party. He also ran for election to the U.S. House to represent New Hampshire's 2nd Congressional District, but he did not appear on the ballot for the Republican primary on September 13, 2022. 

Greeson lives in New Hampshire. He served in the U.S. Navy and reached the rank of lieutenant commander. He obtained a B.S. in meteorology from the University of Oklahoma and an M.S. in meteorology and oceanography from the Naval Postgraduate School. His career experience includes working as a meteorologist, a minister, and a lecturer at Plymouth State University.

In the 2023-2024 session, Greeson was assigned to the House Judiciary Committee. In the 2021-2022 session, he was assigned to the Commerce and Consumer Affairs Committee.

Greeson is a retired Navy officer, having served 8 years enlisted in the Navy's nuclear power program prior to commissioning and then 12 years as a surface warfare officer, meteorologist, and oceanographer. Since retiring from active duty, he has served in ministry work throughout New England and across the country, taught at Plymouth State University for three semesters, and has utilized his own small business experience to work with a friend to help grow his business into the largest of its kind in the state.

Representative Greeson has been married for over 30 years and has two children and five grandchildren. The 2023-2024 session will be Representative Greeson's second term of office having thoroughly enjoyed his freshman term.
