Nicholas Bridle is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Rockingham District 29. He assumed office on December 4, 2024, and his current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Nicholas Bridle was born in Exeter, New Hampshire, and grew up in the Hampton area. He attended Winnacunnet High School, where he balanced academics with extracurricular activities, including sports and community service. After graduating, he pursued higher education at ECPI University, earning a bachelor's degree in 2012.

Bridle's professional journey has been diverse and community-focused. He began his career as a communications specialist, a role that honed his skills in public engagement and problem-solving. Over the years, he has been actively involved in local governance and community initiatives. His commitment to public service is evident in his previous roles, including serving on the Hampton Municipal Budget Committee from 2015 to 2017. These experiences provided him with a deep understanding of the needs and aspirations of his constituents.

In addition to his professional endeavors, Bridle has been a passionate advocate for community development. He has played a significant role in organizing local events and fostering a sense of unity among residents. His leadership in various community projects has earned him respect and admiration from his peers and constituents alike.

Nicholas Bridle resides in Hampton with his wife, Kelley, and their daughter. The family is deeply rooted in the community, cherishing the support and camaraderie of their neighbors and friends. Bridle's dedication to his family and community continues to inspire his work as a legislator, where he strives to represent the interests and values of Rockingham District 29 with integrity and commitment.
