Joe Schapiro is a dedicated and experienced public servant who is currently serving his second term in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He represents the Cheshire 16th district and is a member of the Democratic Party.

In his role as a representative, Schapiro serves on the Health, Human Services, and Elderly Affairs Committee. During his first term, he was actively involved in efforts to increase access to telemedicine services and to implement dental benefits for adult Medicaid recipients. His commitment to public service and the welfare of his constituents is evident in his work.

Before his political career, Schapiro had a long and distinguished career as a Social Worker. He worked as a clinician and administrator in the mental health field, practicing in inpatient, community mental health, educational, and private practice settings. He worked with children, adults, and families, bringing compassion and understanding to his role.

Schapiro is a resident of Keene, New Hampshire, where he has lived, worked, and raised a family for the last 32 years. He is married and has two adult daughters, one an attorney in Boston and the other a Social Worker in New York City. He is also the proud grandfather of two.

In addition to his work as a representative and social worker, Schapiro serves on the board of Monadnock Family Services, the local designated community mental health center. He is also a founding member of the Keene Immigrant and Refugee Partnership (KIRP), further demonstrating his commitment to his community.

Schapiro's dedication to public service, his community, and his family are clear indicators of his character and his commitment to making a positive impact on the lives of those he serves.
