Richard Tripp is an American politician who serves as a Republican member for the Rockingham 13th district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024.

Tripp ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Rockingham 13 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. Prior to this, Tripp represented Rockingham 6 in the New Hampshire House of Representatives from 2016 to 2018.

In addition to his political career, Tripp has a rich personal history. He lives in New Hampshire and served in the U.S. Navy. Tripp also volunteered as a Derry town councilor from 2015 to 2021.

During his tenure, Tripp has been assigned to several committees. For the 2023-2024 term, he was assigned to the House Judiciary Committee. In the 2021-2022 term, he was assigned to the Municipal and County Government Committee. At the beginning of the 2017 legislative session, he served on the Municipal and County Government Committee.

Tripp's dedication to public service is evident in his commitment to his constituents and his active participation in the legislative process. His work in the New Hampshire House of Representatives continues to have a significant impact on the Rockingham 13th district and the state of New Hampshire.
