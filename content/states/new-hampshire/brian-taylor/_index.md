Brian R. Taylor is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Carroll District 8. He assumed office on December 18, 2024, and his current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Born and raised in New Hampshire, Brian Taylor has deep roots in the community he serves. He is a veteran of the United States Army, where he developed a strong sense of discipline and commitment to public service. After his military service, Taylor pursued higher education, earning degrees in Business Administration. His professional career spanned several decades in a large engineering and manufacturing company, where he held key roles in manufacturing, human resources, facilities management, and aftermarket support.

Taylor has also been actively involved in local governance and community development. He served on the Freedom, New Hampshire Planning Board, contributing to the town's growth and planning initiatives. Beyond his professional and political endeavors, Taylor has dedicated significant time to volunteering and serving as a board officer for numerous nonprofit organizations, reflecting his commitment to giving back to his community.

As a legislator, Taylor is a member of the Resources, Recreation, and Development Committee, where he focuses on issues related to natural resources, water pollution control, parks, recreational areas, and property development. His work in the House of Representatives is guided by his dedication to improving the quality of life for his constituents and ensuring sustainable development in the region.

Taylor's leadership and experience make him a respected figure in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, where he continues to advocate for policies that benefit his district and the state as a whole.
