Tim McGough is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Republican Party. He is currently serving as a representative in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough 12. His tenure began on December 7, 2022, and his current term is set to end on December 4, 2024.

McGough lives in Merrimack, New Hampshire. His career experience includes working as a chief commercial officer for a publicly held medical device company. In addition to his professional career, McGough also became a licensed paramedic, showcasing his commitment to serving his community in multiple capacities.

Before his current term, McGough had already made significant contributions to the New Hampshire State Legislature. He was first elected in 1996, serving his first two terms from 1996 to 2000. During his second term, he was appointed as the Republican Caucus Whip by the Speaker. He served both terms on the House Committee on Commerce.

In the most recent election for the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Hillsborough 12, McGough ran and won, securing his current position. This victory is a testament to his dedication and the trust placed in him by the residents of his district.

Throughout his political career, McGough has been involved in various committees and initiatives. He served two terms as the NH State Representative for Merrimack and was the Majority Caucus Whip during his second term. He spent four years on the House Commerce Committee and was a part of the Council on Applied Science and Technology, the American Legislative Exchange Council, and the leadership committees of Senator Bob Dole and President George W. Bush.

As a 30-year resident of his district, the Town of Merrimack, Hillsborough County, District 12, McGough's commitment to his community is unwavering. His diverse experience in both the public and private sectors, coupled with his dedication to public service, makes him a valuable asset to the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
