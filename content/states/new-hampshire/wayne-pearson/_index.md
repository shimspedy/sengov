Wayne Pearson is a Democratic representative serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He represents Strafford District 12, which includes Rollinsford and Somersworth Wards 1 through 5. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term is set to end on December 4, 2024.

Wayne Pearson has had a remarkable journey leading up to his role in public service. Before entering politics, he enjoyed a successful career as a commercial photographer in New York City, where he owned and operated a photography studio for over 30 years. His work in photography not only showcased his artistic talents but also his dedication to his craft and his ability to connect with people from all walks of life.

Five years ago, Wayne and his wife, Anne, decided to move to New Hampshire to be closer to their family, particularly their grandson, Liam. The couple, who have been married for 47 years, found joy in being actively involved in their grandson's life, attending his little league and soccer games. Anne, a retired teacher, shares Wayne's commitment to community and education.

As a lifelong Democrat, Wayne Pearson has been passionate about advocating for issues that matter to his constituents. His priorities include improving public schools, ensuring affordable healthcare, protecting reproductive rights, and addressing the need for attainable housing. He also emphasizes the importance of providing dignified care for seniors in county nursing homes with modern amenities.

Wayne's dedication to his community extends beyond his legislative work. He has taken on a more artistic direction in his personal life, exploring creative pursuits while remaining deeply engaged in local initiatives. His journey from a thriving career in photography to a meaningful role in public service reflects his commitment to making a positive impact on the lives of those around him.
