Gail Pare is a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Strafford 16. She is a member of the Democratic Party. Her tenure began in 2022 and her current term is set to end in 2024.

Gail Pare lives in Dover, New Hampshire. She has been married for over 50 years and has two grown sons and three granddaughters. She has served on the boards of the Seacoast Land Trust and the Wentworth-Gardner House Association.

In her first term in the New Hampshire Legislature, she has been assigned to the Fish and Game and Marine Resources Committee. The committee is responsible for considering all matters concerning conservation, improvement, and preservation of fish, game, and non-game species and marine resources within the state.

Before her election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives, Gail Pare was involved in local government. She served as a Library Trustee, a member of the Historic District Commission, and the Budget Committee.

Gail Pare ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Strafford 16. She won in the general election on November 8, 2022. She assumed office on December 7, 2022. Her current term ends on December 4, 2024.
