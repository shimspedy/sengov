Jay Markell is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Rockingham District 18. He assumed office on December 4, 2024, and his current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Jay Markell's journey into politics is rooted in his dedication to public service and his commitment to the people of Rockingham District 18. A resident of Atkinson, New Hampshire, since 1985, Markell has built a life centered around community involvement and professional excellence. He earned his law degree from the Massachusetts School of Law and has since established himself as a practicing attorney. His legal expertise and problem-solving skills have been instrumental in addressing the needs of his constituents.

Beyond his professional achievements, Markell is a devoted family man. He is the father of two adult sons, one of whom serves in the Coast Guard Reserve, while the other faces the challenges of a severe disability. These personal experiences have shaped Markell's perspective and fueled his passion for advocating for policies that support families, veterans, and individuals with disabilities.

In the New Hampshire House of Representatives, Markell serves on the Children and Family Law Committee as well as the Health, Human Services, and Elderly Affairs Committee. His work in these committees reflects his dedication to improving the lives of children, families, and the elderly in his district and beyond. Markell's approach to governance is characterized by his focus on practical solutions and his ability to navigate complex issues with empathy and determination.

Markell's election to the House of Representatives in 2024 marked the beginning of a promising political career. His victory in the general election demonstrated the trust and confidence that the people of Rockingham District 18 have in his leadership.
