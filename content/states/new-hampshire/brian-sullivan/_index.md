Brian M. Sullivan is a dedicated and accomplished politician from New Hampshire. He is currently serving his 4th term in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing the Sullivan 1st district. His political journey began in 2017 when he was elected in a special election following the resignation of Andy Schmidt.

Sullivan's educational background is rooted in science. He earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Biology from Northland College. Before his political career, Sullivan was a science teacher for 10 years at Berlin High School in Berlin, New Hampshire. His passion for education extended beyond the classroom as he served as a teacher advocate for the National Education Association - New Hampshire for 25 years.

In the New Hampshire House, Sullivan has held significant roles including the chair and ranking member of the Labor, Industrial and Rehabilitative Services Committee. His commitment to public service is evident in his tireless work and dedication to his constituents.

On a personal note, Sullivan resides in Grantham, New Hampshire. He is married and the proud father of three adult children. His life is a testament to his commitment to public service, education, and family.
