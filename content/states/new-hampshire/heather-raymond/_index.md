Heather Raymond is a dedicated public servant and a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough 5. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term ends on December 4, 2024.

Heather Raymond lives in New Hampshire and has a rich educational background. She earned a B.S. in psychology from Worcester State University in 2004. Her career experience includes working as a child and family social worker.

In addition to her role in the House of Representatives, Raymond is also a member of the Nashua Board of Education At-large in New Hampshire. She has served as the president of the Nashua Board of Education, demonstrating her commitment to education and the well-being of children.

Raymond ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Hillsborough 5 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. Her campaign was focused on advocating for strong public schools, sound fiscal policies, environmental protections, and maintaining rights for women, LGTBQ+ Persons, and minorities.

Heather Raymond is an expert on child development and family dynamics who has spent her career advocating for children in need. A large part of Mrs. Raymond's professional experience has been visiting local schools, participating in IEP meetings, and navigating Special Education Law.

In addition to her work on the Board of Education, Mrs. Raymond volunteers with local community service agencies that focus on serving underprivileged children and families in Nashua. Her dedication to public service and advocacy for children and families is evident in her work and contributions to her community.
