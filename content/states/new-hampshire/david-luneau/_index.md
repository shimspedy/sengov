David Luneau, born on February 11, 1965, is a dedicated public servant and a current member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He is one of the three members representing the Merrimack 10th District, which includes Hopkinton and Concord. His journey in the House of Representatives began in 2014, and he has been serving his constituents with commitment and integrity ever since.

David Luneau is not just a politician; he is also a successful business executive. He is the co-founder and president of ClassCo Inc., a technology company based in Concord that develops and markets universal-design telecom products. His business acumen and leadership skills have been instrumental in the growth and success of the company.

Education has played a significant role in shaping David's career and life. He is a proud graduate of Hopkinton High School. His thirst for knowledge didn't stop there. He went on to earn a degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from the prestigious Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1987. This strong educational background laid the foundation for his successful career in technology and politics.

David's commitment to his community extends beyond his professional life. He served on the Hopkinton School Board from 2007 until 2019, including a stint as the chairman of the board from 2007 to 2016. He also serves on the advisory board for NHTI, Concord's Community College, and on the industrial advisory board for the University of New Hampshire at Manchester.

In his current term, which began on December 7, 2022, and ends on December 4, 2024, David continues to work tirelessly for his constituents. His dedication to public service and his passion for making a difference in his community make him a valued member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
