Lucy McVitty Weber, born on August 5, 1952, is a Democratic member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She has been representing the Cheshire 1st District since 2006. 

Born in New York City, New York, U.S., Weber pursued her education at Tufts University where she earned her Bachelor of Arts degree. She later attended Vermont Law School, earning both a Master's degree and a Juris Doctor degree. 

In addition to her political career, Weber has a diverse professional background. She owns the Walpole Inn and has experience working as a teacher and attorney. Her legal practice is based in Vermont.

Weber has held significant positions in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. From December 5, 2018, to December 2, 2020, she served as the Speaker pro tempore of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She succeeded Barbara Griffin and was succeeded by Kimberly Rice in this role.

Throughout her political career, Weber has demonstrated a strong commitment to her constituents and has made significant contributions to the state of New Hampshire.
