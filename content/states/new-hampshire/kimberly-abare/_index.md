Kimberly Abare is an American politician who serves as a Republican member for the Hillsborough 1st district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term ends on December 4, 2024.

Born in Vermont, Kimberly Abare has had a diverse career experience. She owns a business and has worked as a real estate manager of 97 Shoppes. She is also the president of New England Die Cutting, Inc./NEDC Sealing Solutions. Her leadership roles extend beyond her professional career as she has served as the chair of the Pelham Water Commission.

In the political arena, Kimberly Abare ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Hillsborough 1. She emerged victorious in the general election held on November 8, 2022. As a legislator, she has sponsored several bills, contributing to the legislative process. She is also a member of the House Executive Departments and Administration Committee for the 2023-2024 term.

In the 2022 general election for New Hampshire House of Representatives Hillsborough 1, Kimberly Abare secured 17.7% of the votes, amounting to 4,240 votes. This election saw her and three other Republican candidates, Tom Mannion, Sandra Panek, and Jeffrey Tenczar, win the four available seats.

Kimberly Abare's commitment to her role as a representative and her diverse experience in business and real estate management make her a notable figure in the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
