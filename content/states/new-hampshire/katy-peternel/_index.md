Katy Peternel is a dedicated and accomplished member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing the Carroll 6 district. She is a member of the Republican Party and assumed office on December 7, 2022. Her current term is set to end on December 4, 2024.

Katy Peternel earned a bachelor's degree in nursing from the University of Akron. Her career experience includes working as a homeschool coop coordinator and a caregiver. She has been affiliated with the New Hampshire Homeschool Coalition, the Wolfeboro Agricultural Commission, and the New Hampshire Farm Bureau.

In the 2022 elections, Katy Peternel ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Carroll 6. She won in the general election on November 8, 2022. In this election, Katy Peternel and incumbent John MacDonald defeated Carrie Duran and Gogi Millner.

As a legislator, Katy Peternel has sponsored several bills, demonstrating her commitment to serving her constituents and making a positive impact on her community. She is currently assigned to the House Education Committee, where she considers subjects relating to the regulation of school districts and schools, the postsecondary college system, the University System of New Hampshire, and other matters concerning education.

Katy Peternel's dedication to public service, her community, and her constituents make her a valued member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
