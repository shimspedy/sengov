Susan Treleaven is a dedicated and experienced public servant who is currently serving her fourth term in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She represents Strafford 21, which includes Dover Ward 1, Ward 2, Ward 3, Ward 5, Ward 6.

Born and raised in New Hampshire, Susan graduated from Oyster River High School. She then pursued higher education at the University of New Hampshire, where she earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Child and Family Studies in 1980. 

In addition to her political career, Susan has a diverse professional background. She has worked as a real estate broker and realtor, demonstrating her business acumen and commitment to her community. 

Susan is a member of the Democratic Party and assumed office on December 7, 2022. Her current term ends on December 4, 2024. She ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Strafford 21 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Throughout her tenure, Susan has been assigned to the Municipal and County Government Committee multiple times, reflecting her expertise and interest in local governance. 

Beyond her professional and political life, Susan is also an active volunteer. She has volunteered with Seacoast Family Promise, a non-profit organization that serves homeless families in the Seacoast area.

Susan Treleaven is a committed representative who believes in a New Hampshire that works for everyone. She is dedicated to protecting women's rights, adopting domestic renewable energy to lower energy costs, and fighting to keep public money in public schools so every student in New Hampshire has the opportunity to succeed.
