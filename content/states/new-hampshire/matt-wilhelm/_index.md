Matthew Wilhelm, commonly known as Matt Wilhelm, is a prominent politician from New Hampshire. He is currently serving as the Minority Leader of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, a position he assumed on December 7, 2022. He is also a representative of the Hillsborough 42nd district in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, a role he has held since December 5, 2018.

Wilhelm's journey into politics was backed by a strong educational foundation. He earned a Bachelor's degree in Communication Studies and Political Science from Plymouth State University in 2004. He furthered his education by obtaining a Master's degree in Public Administration from the Carsey School of Public Policy at the University of New Hampshire in 2016.

His political career officially began on November 6, 2018, when he was elected to the New Hampshire House of Representatives. As a member of the Democratic Party, Wilhelm has made significant contributions to the political landscape of New Hampshire.

In addition to his political career, Wilhelm has an impressive professional background. He has worked as a managing member of Make It Better, LLC, the director of Old Sol Alliance, Inc., and a lighting director/tour manager with DISPATCH. He has also served on the board of Volunteer NH, the NH alumni board of City Year, and the NH chapter board of AmeriCorps Alums.

Wilhelm's personal life is rooted in Manchester, New Hampshire, where he resides with his family. He is married and is a proud father of two children.

Throughout his political career, Wilhelm has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 term, he was assigned to the Rules Committee. In the 2021-2022 term, he was assigned to the Special Committee On Redistricting and the State-Federal Relations and Veterans Affairs Committee. In the 2019-2020 term, he was assigned to the Legislative Administration Committee.

Wilhelm's dedication to public service began even before his political career, as an AmeriCorps team leader. He supported diverse young Americans through a demanding year of full-time AmeriCorps service in under-resourced public schools.

His commitment to public service, coupled with his strong leadership skills, continues to shape his political career and his contributions to the state of New Hampshire.
