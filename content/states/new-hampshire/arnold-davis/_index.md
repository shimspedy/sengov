Arnold Davis is an American politician who is currently serving as a Republican member for the Coos 2nd district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He assumed office on December 2, 2020, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024.

Davis ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Coos 2 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. He lives in Milan, New Hampshire and graduated from Berlin High School. 

Before his political career, Davis worked as a home inspector and owned and operated a tree and landscape service. His commitment to public service and his community is evident in his work in the New Hampshire House of Representatives.

As a legislator, Davis has been assigned to several committees, including the Environment and Agriculture Committee for the years 2023-2024 and 2021-2022. His work in these committees reflects his dedication to addressing environmental issues and supporting agriculture in his district and throughout New Hampshire.

In the general election for New Hampshire House of Representatives Coos 2 on November 8, 2022, Davis defeated Katherine Doherty, securing 65.2% of the votes. His victory reaffirmed the trust and confidence his constituents have in his leadership and representation.

Throughout his tenure, Davis has demonstrated a strong commitment to his constituents and has worked tirelessly to represent their interests in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. His dedication to public service continues to drive his work as a representative, and he remains a respected figure in New Hampshire politics.
