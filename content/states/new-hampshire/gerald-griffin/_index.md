Gerald Griffin, also known as Gerry, is a member of the Republican Party and a representative of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Hillsborough 42. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024.

Before his current tenure, Griffin had served in the New Hampshire House of Representatives for Hillsborough 5. His career in politics has been marked by dedication and service to his constituents. He ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Hillsborough 42 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

In addition to his political career, Griffin has a distinguished background in the military and business. He served in the U.S. Air Force and reached the rank of Captain, demonstrating leadership and commitment to his country. 

In terms of education, Griffin earned a B.S. in business administration from Boston College and an M.B.A. from Boston University. These academic credentials laid a strong foundation for his subsequent career in business. He has worked as a chief financial officer in public and private companies, showcasing his expertise in financial management. 

Moreover, Griffin is not just a politician and a business leader; he is also a patron of the arts. He owns a museum, indicating his interest in culture and history.

Throughout his tenure, Griffin has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 session, he was assigned to the House Finance Committee and the Finance - Division I Committee. In the 2021-2022 session, he served as the Vice Chair of the Finance - Division I Committee.
