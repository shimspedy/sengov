Arlene Quaratiello is a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Rockingham 18. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term ends on December 4, 2024. Quaratiello ran for election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Rockingham 18 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Arlene Quaratiello lives in Atkinson, New Hampshire. She earned a bachelor's degree in English from Holy Cross College, a master's degree in library science from Simmons College, and a master's degree in English literature from the University of New Hampshire. Her career experience includes working as an academic reference librarian, an adjunct college English instructor, and an author.

In addition to her professional accomplishments, Quaratiello has been involved in local government and community activities. She has served as a school board member for St. Joseph Regional Catholic School in Salem, NH from 2011 to 2016. She is also a parishioner and choir member at St. Anne's Church in Hampstead, NH. In her free time, she enjoys reading, writing, gardening, and yoga.

As a legislator, Quaratiello has been assigned to the House Education Committee, where she serves as a Clerk. Her work in the committee involves considering subjects relating to the regulation of school districts and schools, the postsecondary college system, the University System of New Hampshire, and other matters concerning education.

Quaratiello's dedication to her community and her commitment to education make her a valuable member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
