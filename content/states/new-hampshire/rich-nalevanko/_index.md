Representative Rich Nalevanko, a member of the Republican Party, serves in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Cheshire District 9. He assumed office on December 4, 2024, and his current term is set to conclude on December 2, 2026.

Rich Nalevanko brings a wealth of experience to his role as a legislator. Before entering politics, he enjoyed a distinguished career as a Fortune 100 executive in the energy industry, where he held various international and domestic roles over three decades. His career highlights include serving as a Director of Mobil Sekiyu in Tokyo, Japan, and Mobil Oil Hong Kong, where he oversaw the construction of a $400 million oil terminal project. Rich's academic background is equally impressive, with a degree in Industrial Engineering and Operations Research from Cornell University and an MBA in Finance.

After retiring from the corporate world, Rich and his wife settled in Alstead, New Hampshire, where they have lived since 2000. He continued to contribute to his community by serving as Chief Financial and Operations Officer for a small packaging manufacturing company. Rich has also been deeply involved in local governance, spending nine years on the Alstead Planning Board and advocating for informed and sensible governance at the local level.

Rich's commitment to education is evident through his leadership of the Fall Mountain Scholarship Fund, Inc., which awards over $200,000 annually in scholarships to local students. A passionate advocate for quality education, he works tirelessly to support opportunities for young Granite Staters. Beyond his professional and civic engagements, Rich is an avid golfer, bowler, chess enthusiast, and competitive cribbage player. He is also a life member of several organizations, including the Alstead Historical Society and the NRA.

Rich Nalevanko's dedication to his constituents and his extensive professional and community experience make him a valuable asset to the New Hampshire House of Representatives. His focus on education, local governance, and community involvement underscores his commitment to serving the people of Cheshire District 9.
