Daniel Eaton is a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Cheshire 9. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024. Eaton is a member of the Democratic Party.

Before his current term, Eaton served in the House of Representatives previously, representing Cheshire 2 from 1976 to 1990 and 2002 to 2010. He was also a candidate for the House in 2000. 

Eaton's career experience includes working as the director of Southwestern N.H. Fire Mutual Aid and a police officer with the Town of Stoddard, New Hampshire. He has also served as the town moderator and school district moderator of Stoddard, the president of the Cheshire City Peace Officers Association, and the vice chair of the NCSL Standing Committee on Communications, Financial Services, and Interstate Commerce.

In terms of committee assignments, Eaton has been assigned to the Public Works and Highways Committee for the 2023-2024 term. He was also assigned to the same committee for the 2021-2022 and 2019-2020 terms. At the beginning of the 2017 legislative session, Eaton served on the Finance - Division II and Finance committees. At the beginning of the 2015 legislative session, Eaton served on the Finance and Finance - Division II committees. In the 2013-2014 legislative session, Eaton served on the Finance and Finance - Division II committees. In the 2009-2010 legislative session, Eaton served on the Finance and Finance - Division II committees.

Eaton ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Cheshire 9 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. Incumbent Daniel Eaton defeated Richard Nalevanko in the general election for New Hampshire House of Representatives Cheshire 9 on November 8, 2022.

Daniel Adams Eaton was born on April 15, 1956, in Keene, New Hampshire, U.S. He is a businessman and consultant by profession. He resides in Stoddard, New Hampshire, U.S.
