Mary Ford is a member of the Republican Party serving in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She represents Rockingham District 3. Mary assumed office on December 4, 2024, and her current term is set to end on December 2, 2026.

Mary Ford has dedicated her career to education and public service. With a background as a teacher and educator, she has worked tirelessly to improve the quality of education in her community. Her professional experience includes roles as a special education teacher, coordinator, and director in various educational institutions. She has also served as a college professor and held leadership positions in educational programs aimed at fostering innovation and compliance with regulatory standards.

In addition to her professional achievements, Mary has been actively involved in local government and community activities. She has served on the school board in Hollis, New Hampshire, and has been a delegate to the Rockingham County Republican Committee. Her commitment to public service extends to her role in the House of Representatives, where she is a member of the Ways and Means Committee. This committee focuses on examining state revenue and taxation policies, ensuring fiscal responsibility and transparency.

Mary Ford is also a devoted family person. She is married to Oliver J. Ford and is the proud mother of two children, Ross and Charlotte. She cherishes her role as a grandmother to her five grandchildren and is an active participant in church and community events. An avid hunter and a passionate advocate for her constituents, Mary embodies the spirit of New Hampshire's "Live Free or Die" ethos.

Her journey in public service reflects her dedication to making a positive impact on her community and the state of New Hampshire. Through her work in education and government, Mary Ford continues to strive for a better future for her constituents and the generations to come.
