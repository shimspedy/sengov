Lisa Freeman is a Republican member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Belknap District 8. She assumed office on December 4, 2024, and her current term will end on December 2, 2026.

Lisa Freeman has been an active participant in her community and has a history of public service. Before joining the New Hampshire House of Representatives, she served on the Manchester Board of School Committee, representing Ward 5. Her experience in education and local governance has shaped her approach to policymaking, emphasizing the importance of community engagement and practical solutions to local issues.

Freeman is a graduate of Winthrop Senior High School and pursued further education at Laboure College and North Shore Community College, where she studied occupational therapy. Her educational background has provided her with a unique perspective on healthcare and education policies, which are among her key areas of focus as a legislator.

In her role as a state representative, Lisa Freeman is committed to addressing the needs of her constituents in Belmont, Sanbornton, and Tilton. She serves on committees such as Education Policy and Administration, as well as Environment and Agriculture, where she works on issues ranging from agricultural development to environmental protection.

Freeman's dedication to public service and her ability to connect with her constituents have made her a respected figure in New Hampshire politics. She continues to advocate for policies that promote economic growth, education reform, and environmental sustainability, ensuring that the voices of her district are heard in the state legislature.
