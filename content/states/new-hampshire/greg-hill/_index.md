Greg Hill is a multifaceted individual with a diverse career spanning across radio, philanthropy, entrepreneurship, and politics. 

In the realm of radio, Greg Hill is a well-known personality, having served as the morning show host at WEEI Boston for over three decades. He currently hosts The Greg Hill Morning Show alongside Jermaine Wiggins and Courtney Cox. Over the years, Hill has interviewed numerous celebrities and sports personalities, including Boston Bruins President Cam Neely, New England Patriots quarterback Tom Brady, and Patriots wide receiver Julian Edelman.

Beyond his radio career, Hill is also a successful entrepreneur. He is a partner at Alipes Inc. and founder/partner at Right Coast Hospitality Group, LLC. He co-owns and operates several Boston-area eateries, including Howl at the Moon and Battle Road Brewing.

Philanthropy is another area where Hill has made significant contributions. He established the Greg Hill Foundation in 2010, which matches philanthropic donations from his fans. The foundation has raised over $10 million in donations for over 7500 local families and organizations, helping them face emergencies in the New England area.

In addition to his radio and philanthropic work, Hill is also a dedicated runner. He has participated in numerous 5k charity runs, raising over $23,000 for a youth group.

On the political front, Greg Hill serves as a Republican member for the Merrimack 2nd district of the New Hampshire House of Representatives. He currently is the chair of the Legislative Administration Committee.

In terms of his education, Hill graduated from Nashoba Regional High School in Bolton, Massachusetts, in 1985. He earned a B.A. in political science from Texas A&M University in 1993 and a J.D. from the South Texas College of Law in 2004.

Throughout his career, Hill has demonstrated a commitment to serving his community, whether through his radio show, his foundation, or his role in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. His diverse career and contributions make him a notable figure in both the media and political landscape.
