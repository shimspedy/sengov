Carol McGuire is an American politician who is a member of the Republican Party. She represents the Merrimack 27 district in the New Hampshire House of Representatives. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term ends on December 4, 2024.

Carol McGuire grew up in Connecticut and now resides in Epsom, New Hampshire. She has a rich educational background, having earned a B.S. in mechanical engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and an M.B.A from Boston University.

Before her current tenure, she had served in the New Hampshire House of Representatives, representing Merrimack 29. She ran for re-election to the New Hampshire House of Representatives to represent Merrimack 27 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

In addition to her political career, Carol McGuire has an impressive professional background. She is an engineer by profession. She has lived in various places including Boston, Silicon Valley, Washington state, and New Hampshire.

Carol McGuire has been actively involved in local government. She was a former member of the Epsom budget committee and served as an Epsom Cemetery trustee for 9 years.

Throughout her political career, Carol McGuire has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 legislative session, she was assigned to the House Executive Departments and Administration Committee as Chair. In the 2021-2022 legislative session, she served as Vice-Chair of the Administrative Rules Committee and Chair of the House Executive Departments and Administration Committee. She was also part of the Special Committee On Redistricting.
