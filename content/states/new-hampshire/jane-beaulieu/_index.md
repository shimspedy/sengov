Jane Beaulieu is a dedicated and experienced public servant who has made significant contributions to the state of New Hampshire. She is currently serving as a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives from the Hillsborough 19 district. Her tenure began in 2022 and her current term will end in 2024.

Born and raised in Manchester, New Hampshire, Jane's early life was greatly influenced by her father, Emile Beaulieu, who was a former state legislator and mayor of Manchester. This exposure to public service at an early age sparked Jane's interest in politics and community service.

Jane is not just a politician, she is also a successful businesswoman. She is a licensed cosmetologist and owns a small business. In addition to her political career, she has also made a name for herself in the business world. She was an unsuccessful candidate in the 2003 Manchester Mayoral Election.

Jane's educational background is also noteworthy. She graduated from Manchester Memorial High School and later earned a bachelor's degree from the University of New Hampshire. She also holds a certificate from the Nackey Loeb School of Communications.

Before her current tenure, Jane served as a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives where she represented the Hillsborough 17 district from 2004 to 2010. She also served on the Manchester Board of School Committee, representing Ward 10 from 2020 to 2022.

In her personal life, Jane is a resident of Manchester. She is divorced, has one daughter, and one grandson. Despite her busy professional life, Jane always finds time for her family and is a devoted mother and grandmother.

Throughout her career, Jane Beaulieu has shown a commitment to serving her community and making a difference in the lives of the people of New Hampshire. Her dedication to public service, combined with her business acumen, makes her a valuable asset to the New Hampshire House of Representatives.
