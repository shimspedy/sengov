State Sen. Justin Busch has been representing northeastern Indiana in the Senate since 2018, serving Senate District 16, which includes Whitley County and a portion of Allen County.

In the Senate, Busch holds the position of Assistant Caucus Chair and serves as the ranking member of the Senate Committee on Health and Provider Services. He is also an active member of the Senate Committees on Appropriations and Public Policy.

Before his tenure in the Senate, Busch contributed to public service through various roles. He worked for the George W. Bush administration, was appointed to the Indiana State Real Estate Commission by former Gov. Mike Pence, and served as an at-large member of the Allen County Council.

Busch is an alumnus of Indiana University-Purdue University Fort Wayne, where he received a bachelor’s degree in interpersonal and organizational communication from Purdue University and a bachelor’s degree in history from Indiana University.

Outside of the Senate, Busch serves as the Vice President of Fort Wayne Garmong Construction.
