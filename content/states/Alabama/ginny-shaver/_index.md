Ginny Shaver is a member of the Alabama House of Representatives, representing District 39. She assumed office on November 7, 2018, and her current term ends on November 4, 2026. Shaver ran for re-election to the Alabama House of Representatives to represent District 39 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Shaver is a member of the Republican Party. She is known for her active involvement in the community. She is the president of the Cherokee County Republican Women and vice-chair of the Cherokee County Republican Executive Committee. She was also appointed to the Cherokee County Industrial Development Board.

Before her political career, Shaver worked with the Gadsden Airport Authority and served as Town Clerk of Leesburg. She is a certified municipal clerk. Shaver hails from Leesburg, Alabama, and currently works for the City of Gadsden.

In terms of education, Shaver attended Gadsden State Community College and Chattahoochee Valley Community College, majoring in accounting and computer science. She continued her education at Jacksonville State University.

During her tenure, Shaver has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 term, she served as the Chair of the Children and Senior Advocacy Committee and Vice Chair of the Economic Development and Tourism Committee. She was also a member of the Legislative Committee on Public Accounts. In the previous terms, she served on the Transportation Committee and the Agriculture and Forestry Committee.

In the general election for Alabama House of Representatives District 39 held on November 8, 2022, Shaver won with a significant majority, securing 99.0% of the votes.
