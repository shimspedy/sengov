Bobby Singleton is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Democratic Party. He has been serving as a member of the Alabama State Senate, representing District 24, since 2005. His current term is set to end on November 4, 2026.

Before his tenure in the Senate, Singleton served in the Alabama House of Representatives from 2002 to 2005. His commitment to public service is evident in his long-standing involvement in state politics.

Singleton received his Bachelor of Science degree from Alabama State University and his Juris Doctor degree from Miles Law School. Apart from his political career, he has professional experience in consulting, demonstrating his versatility in different fields.

As a legislator, Singleton has been assigned to several committees. In the 2023-2024 term, he served on the Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry Committee, Banking and Insurance Committee, Confirmations Committee, Finance and Taxation Education Committee, Finance and Taxation General Fund Committee, Senate Judiciary Committee, Tourism Committee, Reapportionment Committee, Joint Legislative Prison Committee, and Housing Finance Authority License Plates Legislative Oversight Committee.

Singleton's base salary as a senator is $53,913 per year. However, per diem is not paid to legislators whose permanent residence is less than six hours away. Legislators who are 6-12 hours from their permanent residence receive $12.75 per day, and those who are over 12 hours away with no overnight receive $34 per day.

In addition to his legislative duties, Singleton is a business owner. He is a Christian and a member of the Baptist denomination. His dedication to his faith, his profession, and his public service role make him a well-rounded individual committed to serving his community.
