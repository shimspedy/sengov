Chris Blackshear, born on March 11, 1974, is a Republican American politician who has been serving in the Alabama House of Representatives from the 80th district since his victory in a special election in 2016. He was elected to a full term in 2018.

Blackshear's political journey began with his service on the Phenix City Council, where he also had a stint as mayor pro tempore. His commitment to public service and his community was evident during his tenure on the council.

In addition to his political career, Blackshear has a diverse professional background. He has been employed since 1994 with the credit card processing firm, TSYS. He also worked as a reporter covering the football team for Troy University in Troy, Alabama. His wife, Krista, is an employee of the Phenix City School Board.

Blackshear ran for re-election to the Alabama House of Representatives to represent District 80 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. His current term ends on November 4, 2026.

Throughout his legislative career, Blackshear has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 term, he was assigned to the Financial Services Committee (as Chair), Lee County Legislation Committee, and Urban and Rural Development Committee. In the previous terms, he served on the House County and Municipal Government Committee and Urban and Rural Development Committee.

Blackshear's dedication to his constituents and his commitment to public service continue to guide his work in the Alabama House of Representatives.
