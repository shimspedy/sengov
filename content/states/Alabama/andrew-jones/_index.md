Andrew Jones is an American politician who serves as a Republican member of the Alabama Senate for the 10th District. He represents District 10 in the Alabama Senate, which includes Etowah, Cherokee, and Southern DeKalb counties. Jones serves as the chairman of the Veterans, Military Affairs, & Public Safety committee.

Jones was elected to the Senate in 2018 in his first run for public office. Prior to this, he served as chairman of the Children, Youth, & Human Services committee.

Beyond his political career, Jones is the founder and owner of Deep South Coffee Factory, a craft coffee roasting company. He is also a fourth-generation cattle farmer and shareholder in Paul Jennings Farms, Inc., which is named after his late grandfather.

Jones holds a Masters in Public Policy from the College of William and Mary in Virginia, and a B.A. from Berea College in Kentucky. Before his political career, Jones spent several years working in South Korea and later Japan as part of the Japan Exchange and Teaching (JET) Program.

Jones and his wife, Summer, reside in Centre and are members of Centre First Baptist Church, where he serves as pianist.
