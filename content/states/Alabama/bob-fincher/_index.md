Bob Fincher, born in 1949 or 1950, is an American politician and a member of the Republican Party. He represents District 37 in the Alabama House of Representatives. He assumed office on November 5, 2014, and his current term ends on November 4, 2026.

Before his political career, Fincher worked as an educator, teaching at Woodland High School and New Hope Christian School. His dedication to education has been a defining aspect of his career, shaping his perspectives and policy decisions.

Over the years, Fincher has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 term, he served on the Agriculture and Forestry Committee, the Transportation, Utilities and Infrastructure Committee, the Tuscaloosa County Legislation Committee, and the Water Policy And Management Joint Legislative Committee.

In the 2021-2022 term, he was assigned to the Water Policy And Management Joint Legislative Committee, the Constitution, Campaigns and Elections Committee (as Chair), the Agriculture and Forestry Committee, the House Education Policy Committee, and the House Local Legislation Committee.

In the 2019-2020 term, he served on the Agriculture and Forestry Committee, the Constitution, Campaigns and Elections Committee (as Vice chair), the House Education Policy Committee, and the House Local Legislation Committee.

Fincher ran for re-election to the Alabama House of Representatives to represent District 37 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. He continues to serve his constituents with dedication and commitment, striving to make a positive impact through his legislative work.
