Edward Banks Oliver, known as Ed Oliver, is an American politician who has been serving as a member of the Alabama House of Representatives from the 81st district since 2018. He is a member of the Republican Party.

Born between 1955 and 1956 in Dadeville, Alabama, Oliver graduated from Dadeville High School in 1974. He then pursued higher education at the University of Alabama, graduating in 1979.

Before his political career, Oliver served as an aviator in the United States Army. He also worked as an air ambulance helicopter pilot. His experiences in these roles have undoubtedly shaped his perspective and approach to his work in the House of Representatives.

In 2018, Oliver decided to run for the Alabama House of Representatives in the Republican primary for the 81st district. He advanced to a runoff against Terry Martin, which he won. In the general election, Oliver faced Democratic nominee Jeremy Jeffcoat. Oliver campaigned on industrial development and economic growth, as well as education. His efforts paid off when he won against Jeffcoat in the November general election and was sworn in as state representative.

As a member of the Alabama House of Representatives, Oliver has been assigned to several committees, including the Agriculture and Forestry Committee, Financial Services Committee, and Health Committee. His work in these committees reflects his commitment to serving his constituents and the state of Alabama.

Oliver ran for re-election to the Alabama House of Representatives to represent District 81 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. His current term ends on November 4, 2026.

Throughout his political career, Oliver has demonstrated a dedication to public service and a commitment to representing the interests of his constituents. His work in the Alabama House of Representatives continues to have a significant impact on the state of Alabama.
