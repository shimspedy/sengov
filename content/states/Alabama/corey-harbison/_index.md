Corey Harbison is a member of the Republican Party and represents District 12 in the Alabama House of Representatives. He assumed office on November 5, 2014, and his current term is set to end on November 4, 2026. Harbison ran for re-election to the Alabama House of Representatives to represent District 12 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Before his tenure in the Alabama House of Representatives, Harbison served as the Mayor of Good Hope, Alabama. His professional experience includes various roles in public service, demonstrating his commitment to his community.

Throughout his political career, Harbison has been assigned to several committees. In the 2023-2024 term, he was assigned to the Economic Development and Tourism Committee, Ethics and Campaign Finance Committee, and the Technology and Research Committee, where he served as Vice Chair. He also served on the Housing Finance Authority.

In the 2021-2022 term, Harbison was assigned to the Constitution, Campaigns and Elections Committee, Technology and Research Committee (as Vice Chair), and the Economic Development and Tourism Committee. In the 2019-2020 term, he served on the Constitution, Campaigns and Elections Committee, Economic Development and Tourism Committee, and the Technology and Research Committee as Vice chair.

At the beginning of the 2015 legislative session, Harbison served on the Boards, Agencies and Commissions, Constitution, Campaigns and Elections, and Economic Development and Tourism committees.

In the general election for Alabama House of Representatives District 12 on November 8, 2022, incumbent Corey Harbison defeated James Fields Jr. Harbison secured 85.3% of the votes, amounting to 11,958 votes. James Fields Jr. received 14.5% of the votes, amounting to 2,030 votes. Other write-in votes accounted for 0.2% of the votes, amounting to 23 votes.

Corey Harbison was born on April 11, 1986, in Good Hope, Alabama, U.S. He continues to serve his community and state with dedication and commitment.
