Tashina Morris is an American politician and former cosmetologist who is currently serving as a member of the Alabama House of Representatives from the 77th district. She assumed office on November 7, 2018.

Born and raised in Alabama, Morris has a strong educational background. She earned a Master of Science in leadership management from Amridge University and a Ph.D. in criminal justice and safety studies from Capella University. Her education has played a significant role in shaping her political career and her approach to leadership.

Before entering politics, Morris had a diverse career. She worked as a cosmetology instructor and floor manager at Virginia College and a program director at the SouthEastern School of Cosmetology. These experiences gave her a unique perspective on the needs and challenges of her constituents, particularly those related to education and workforce development.

In addition to her work in cosmetology, Morris has also worked as a nonprofit director. This role allowed her to make a direct impact on her community and further developed her leadership skills.

Morris's political career began when she was elected to the Alabama House of Representatives, assuming office on November 7, 2018. She succeeded John Knight and has since been a dedicated and effective representative for her district.

In November 2020, Morris was a candidate for the Alabama Senate, where she placed fourth in the Democratic primary. Despite not winning the Senate seat, Morris continues to serve her district in the House of Representatives and remains committed to advocating for her constituents.
