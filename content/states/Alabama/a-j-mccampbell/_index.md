Artis J. McCampbell, also known as A.J., is a member of the Alabama House of Representatives, representing District 71. He assumed office in 2006 and his current term ends on November 4, 2026. McCampbell ran for re-election to the Alabama House of Representatives to represent District 71.

McCampbell was born on March 11, 1953, in Greene County, Alabama, United States. He graduated from Eutaw High School in 1971 and earned a B.A. in Political Science from the University of Alabama in 1976.

McCampbell's professional experience includes working as an insurance agent, serving as an investigator for the district attorney's office in the 17th Judicial Circuit, working as a juvenile probation officer for Green County, and working as an investigator and patrolman for the City of Tuscaloosa.

He began his career in law enforcement in 1975 with the City of Tuscaloosa as a patrolman. Two years later he was promoted to investigator.

Over the years, McCampbell has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 legislative session, he was assigned to the Agriculture and Forestry Committee and the Health Committee, where he served as Vice Chair. In the 2021-2022 legislative session, he was assigned to the Transportation Committee, Water Policy And Management Joint Legislative Committee, Transportation, Utilities and Infrastructure Committee, Tuscaloosa County Legislation Committee, and Agriculture and Forestry Committee.

McCampbell won re-election in the general election for Alabama House of Representatives District 71 on November 8, 2022.
