Neil Rafferty is an American politician and current member of the Alabama House of Representatives, representing District 54. Winning his seat in the 2018 elections, Rafferty assumed office on November 7, 2018. He was re-elected to his position in 2022.

Rafferty is a member of the Democratic Party. Prior to his political career, Rafferty served as a corporal in the United States Marine Corps and is a veteran of the Global War on Terrorism. After his military service, he worked as director of research and development for Birmingham AIDS Outreach.

Rafferty resides in Birmingham, Alabama with his husband and former United States Marine, Michael Rudulph. His commitment to public service and his community is evident in his work both in the military and in the Alabama House of Representatives.

In addition to his legislative duties, Rafferty has served on several committees, including the Economic Development and Tourism Committee, Health Committee, Jefferson County Legislation Committee, and the Transportation, Utilities and Infrastructure Committee.

Rafferty's tenure in the Alabama House of Representatives has been marked by his dedication to his constituents and his commitment to improving the lives of the people of Alabama. His work in the House has focused on a range of issues, from economic development and tourism to health and infrastructure.

Rafferty's dedication to public service, his experience in the military, and his work in the field of research and development for Birmingham AIDS Outreach all contribute to his effective leadership in the Alabama House of Representatives.
