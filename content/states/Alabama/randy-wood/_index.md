Randy Wood, born on March 25, 1947, is a member of the Republican Party and a representative of the Alabama House of Representatives, representing District 36. He first assumed office in 2002 and his current term is set to end on November 4, 2026.

Wood's professional experience includes owning Wood's Auto Body Shop and serving in the Alabama National Guard. He also served as a commissioner in Calhoun County from 1998 to 2002.

Over the years, Wood has been assigned to various committees. For the 2023-2024 term, he was assigned to the Military and Veterans' Affairs Committee, House Rules Committee, and Reapportionment Committee. In the 2021-2022 term, he was part of the Reapportionment Committee, House Rules Committee, Economic Development and Tourism Committee, and Military and Veterans' Affairs Committee. In the 2019-2020 term, he served on the Economic Development and Tourism Committee, Military and Veterans' Affairs Committee, and House Rules Committee.

Wood ran for re-election to the Alabama House of Representatives to represent District 36 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. He received 98.2% of the votes, a total of 12,449 votes.

Before his political career, Wood was a businessman and a graduate of Saks High School and Southern Union College.
