Arnold Mooney, born on December 8, 1950, is an American politician affiliated with the Republican Party. He has been serving in the Alabama House of Representatives from the 43rd district since 2014.

Mooney was born and raised in Montgomery, Alabama. He pursued his education at Samford University, where he earned both his Bachelor's and Master's degrees.

Before his political career, Mooney spent over 40 years working as a businessman in commercial real estate. His commitment to family values and conservative principles have been a guiding force in his life and work.

In May 2019, Mooney announced that he would seek the Republican nomination for the U.S. Senate seat in Alabama in the 2020 election. However, he received 1 percent of the vote in the March 3, 2020 primary.

Apart from his political career, Mooney was an at-large delegate to the 2016 Republican National Convention from Alabama. He was one of 13 delegates from Alabama bound by state party rules to support Ted Cruz at the convention. Cruz suspended his campaign on May 3, 2016.

Mooney ran for re-election to the Alabama House of Representatives to represent District 43 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. His current term ends on November 4, 2026.

Arnold Mooney continues to serve his constituents with dedication and commitment, upholding the principles and values that have guided him throughout his life and career.
