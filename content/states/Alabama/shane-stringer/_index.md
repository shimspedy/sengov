Shane Stringer is a member of the Republican Party and represents District 102 in the Alabama House of Representatives. He assumed office on November 7, 2018, succeeding Jack Williams. His current term is set to end on November 4, 2026.

Born in Citronelle, Alabama, Representative Stringer has had a diverse career. Prior to his political career, he served as the Satsuma Police Chief. He later resigned from his position to serve as a captain in the Mobile County Sheriff's Office. However, in May 2021, Stringer was fired from his position in the sheriff's office for sponsoring constitutional carry legislation.

In terms of his political career, Stringer ran for re-election to the Alabama House of Representatives to represent District 102 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. As a legislator, he has been assigned to several committees over the years.

For the 2023-2024 term, Stringer was assigned to the following committees:
- Mobile County Legislation Committee
- Public Safety and Homeland Security Committee
- Urban and Rural Development Committee
- Baldwin County Legislation Committee

In the previous term (2021-2022), he was assigned to the following committees:
- Urban and Rural Development Committee
- House Education Policy Committee
- Mobile County Legislation Committee
- Public Safety and Homeland Security Committee

And for the 2019-2020 term, he was assigned to the following committees:
- House Education Policy Committee
- Mobile County Legislation Committee
- Public Safety and Homeland Security Committee
- Urban and Rural Development Committee

As a legislator, Representative Stringer has sponsored several bills, although the specific details of these bills were not available at the time of this writing.

In the most recent election for the Alabama House of Representatives District 102, held on November 8, 2022, incumbent Shane Stringer won with a staggering 98.8% of the votes, totaling 10,472 votes. The remaining 1.2% of the votes were write-ins.
