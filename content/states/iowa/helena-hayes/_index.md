Helena Hayes is a member of the Republican Party and represents District 88 in the Iowa House of Representatives. She assumed office on January 1, 2023, and her current term ends on January 1, 2025.

Hayes was born in Harrison County, Iowa. She graduated from Tri-Center High School and later earned a B.A. in environmental science and biology from Simpson College.

Before her political career, Hayes worked as a homeschool tutor, a naturalist with the Mahaska County Conservation Board, and a poultry superintendent. She has also served as the chair of Protect My Innocence and a Mahaska County 4-H organizational leader.

Hayes ran for election to the Iowa House of Representatives to represent District 88 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

In addition to her political career, Hayes has been involved in various community activities. She has sponsored numerous bills as a legislator, although the specific details of these bills are not available at this time.

Hayes was assigned to the following committees in 2023-2024: Environmental Protection Committee, Natural Resources Committee, and International Relations Committee.

In the general election for Iowa House of Representatives District 88, Helena Hayes defeated Lisa Ossian with a significant margin. She received 73.7% of the votes, amounting to 8,455 votes in total.

Hayes' dedication to her community and her commitment to public service are evident in her work. She continues to serve her constituents with integrity and dedication.
