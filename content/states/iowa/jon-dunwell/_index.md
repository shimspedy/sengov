Jon Dunwell is an American politician who is currently serving as a member of the Iowa House of Representatives from the 38th district. He assumed office on October 12, 2021, succeeding Wes Breckenridge.

Dunwell's political journey began with a special election in October 2021. His victory over Newton City Councilman Steve Mullan flipped the 29th Iowa House district seat to Republican from Democrat. This win marked a significant shift in the political landscape of the district.

Before entering politics, Dunwell had a diverse career. He worked as a pastor in Orlando, Florida, and Minnesota, providing spiritual guidance to his community. His professional journey also includes roles in the financial sector. He served as the vice president of two financial services firms, showcasing his leadership and strategic planning skills.

In addition to his pastoral and financial roles, Dunwell founded Legacy Park Wealth and owned a business. He also worked as a registered representative with International Assets Advisory. His experience in these roles demonstrates his entrepreneurial spirit and commitment to economic growth.

Dunwell also served as a senior chaplain with the Orange County Sheriff's Office. This role allowed him to provide support and counseling to law enforcement officers, further showcasing his dedication to community service.

Despite his extensive career outside of Iowa, Dunwell has strong ties to the state. He relocated to his wife's hometown of Newton, Iowa, embedding himself in the local community.

As a representative, Dunwell continues to serve his community and state, bringing his diverse experience to the political arena. His journey from pastor to politician showcases his commitment to public service and his ability to adapt to new challenges.
