David Blom is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Iowa House of Representatives, representing District 52. Born and raised in Marshalltown, Iowa, Blom has deep roots in his community. He attended Marshalltown High School, where he developed a strong work ethic and a commitment to serving others.

Blom's career began as a Union sheet metal worker, a profession that instilled in him the values of hard work, precision, and teamwork. His experience in this field has given him a unique perspective on the challenges faced by working-class families and the importance of supporting local industries.

In 2024, Blom decided to run for the Iowa House of Representatives to represent District 52. His campaign focused on issues such as job creation, education, and healthcare. Blom's dedication to these causes resonated with voters, and he won the general election on November 5, 2024, defeating incumbent Sue Cahill.

Blom assumed office on January 1, 2025, and his current term will end on January 1, 2027. As a state representative, he serves on several important committees, including Appropriations, Commerce (where he is the Vice Chair), Local Government, and Veterans Affairs. His work on these committees allows him to address a wide range of issues that impact his constituents.

Throughout his tenure, Blom has remained committed to improving the lives of the people in his district. He is known for his approachable demeanor and his willingness to listen to the concerns of his constituents. Blom's dedication to public service and his strong work ethic make him a valuable asset to the Iowa House of Representatives.
