Monica Kurth is an American educator and politician hailing from Davenport, Iowa. She has had a significant impact in the field of education, having taught at Scott Community College. Her journey into politics began in 2017 when she won a special election to the Iowa House of Representatives. This was to fill the seat vacated by Jim Lykam for the 89th district. Her dedication and commitment to her role have seen her win reelection in 2018, 2020, and 2022.

In addition to her role as a representative, Monica Kurth is a member of the Democratic Party. Her political career has been marked by several noteworthy achievements. In the 2017 Iowa State House district 89 special election, she secured a decisive victory with 72.4% of the votes. In the subsequent years, she ran unopposed in both the primary and general elections in 2018. In the 2020 special election for the same district, she garnered 54.8% of the votes.

Monica Kurth's full name is Monica Hosch Kurth. She was born in Cascade, Iowa. She is an alumnus of Aquin High School. She furthered her education at Mount Mercy College where she earned a bachelor’s degree in mathematics. She also holds a master’s degree in counseling from Northeast Missouri State University.

Her career experience is diverse and includes working as a counselor/therapist with the Rock Island Youth Guidance Council and a faculty member with Scott Community College. She has served on the Scott County Democratic Central Committee and has been affiliated with Quad Cities Interfaith and Progressive Action for the Common Good.

As a legislator, Monica Kurth has been assigned to several committees. These include the House Agriculture Committee, House Education Committee, House Ethics Committee, and House Ways and Means Committee. Her dedication to her role and her constituents is evident in her active participation in these committees.

Monica Kurth's tenure as a representative has been marked by her commitment to her role and her constituents. She assumed office on January 1, 2023, and her current term ends on January 1, 2025.
