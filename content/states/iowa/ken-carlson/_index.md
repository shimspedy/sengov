Ken Carlson is a member of the Republican Party and represents District 13 in the Iowa House of Representatives. He assumed office on January 1, 2023, and his current term is set to end on January 1, 2025.

Carlson's journey to the Iowa House of Representatives began with his election campaign for District 13. He emerged victorious in the general election held on November 8, 2022. Prior to his political career, Carlson gained valuable experience working as a farmer and a public school teacher.

In terms of legislative work, Carlson has been assigned to several committees during his tenure from 2023 to 2024. These include the House Agriculture Committee, Economic Growth Committee, Natural Resources Committee, where he serves as the Vice Chair, and the House Veterans Affairs Committee.

Carlson's election journey was marked by significant victories. In the general election for the Iowa House of Representatives District 13, he defeated Amy Janowski, securing 85.4% of the votes, which amounted to 10,157 votes. In the Republican primary for the same district, Carlson defeated Mark Peters with 56.4% of the votes, totaling 1,488 votes.

Representative Carlson hails from Onawa and his base salary as a legislator is $25,000 per year. Legislators who live outside of Polk County receive a per diem of $175 per day, while those who live within Polk County receive $131.25 per day.

In conclusion, Ken Carlson's political career is marked by his commitment to his constituents in District 13 and his contributions to the various committees he has been a part of. His background as a farmer and a public school teacher adds a unique perspective to his legislative work.
