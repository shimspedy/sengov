Jeff Edler, born on August 17, 1976, is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Republican Party. He is currently serving as the Iowa State Senator from the 26th District. His journey in the Iowa Senate began when he defeated the Democratic incumbent Steve Sodders in 2016.

Edler hails from State Center, Iowa, where he resides with his wife Stephanie and their six children. Apart from his political career, he is also a farmer and serves as the Consumers Energy Rural Electric Cooperative board president.

His political career has been marked by his commitment to various committees. As of February 2020, Edler serves on the Local Government (Chair), Agriculture, Education, Human Resources, Veterans Affairs, and Ways and Means committees. He also serves on the Health and Human Services Appropriations Subcommittee (Vice Chair), as well as the International Relations Committee, the Children's Behavioral Health System State Board, Education Commission of the States, and the Mental Health and Disability Services Commission.

In the past, Edler has served on the Senate Agriculture Committee, Senate Appropriations Committee, Senate Human Resources Committee, Chair Senate Veterans Affairs Committee, and many more. His dedication to these committees reflects his commitment to serving the people of Iowa.

Edler's electoral history is also noteworthy. In the Iowa Senate 36th District election in 2018, he secured 52.91% of the votes, defeating his opponent. He ran for re-election to the Iowa State Senate to represent District 36 and won in the general election on November 3, 2020.

In his current term, which began on January 3, 2023, and ends on January 2, 2025, Edler continues to serve the people of Iowa with dedication and integrity. His work in the Senate is a testament to his commitment to public service and the betterment of his community.
