Matt W. Windschitl was born on December 30, 1983, in Marshalltown, Iowa. He studied gunsmithing at the Colorado School of Trades. Windschitl is a member of the Republican Party and has been serving in the Iowa House of Representatives since 2007.

Before his political career, Windschitl worked in various roles including a conductor for the Union Pacific Railroad, a gunsmith, and for Doll Distributing in Council Bluffs, Iowa. He also served in the United States Marine Corps Reserve from 2001 to 2009, including a six-month tour in Iraq.

In the Iowa House of Representatives, Windschitl first represented the 17th District. He defeated the incumbent Republican Paul Wilderdyke in a primary election to win his seat. Currently, he is serving as a representative from the 15th District and holds the position of Majority Leader of the Iowa House. He assumed this office on January 1, 2023.

Throughout his tenure, Windschitl has held several key positions. He served as the Speaker Pro Tempore from 2014 to 2020. He was elected by his caucus to serve as House Majority Leader in 2019. In addition to these leadership roles, Windschitl has served on several committees in the Iowa House including Judiciary, Local Government, Veterans Affairs, and Ways and Means committees.

Windschitl's dedication to public service extends beyond his political career. His military service in the U.S. Marine Corps is a testament to his commitment to his country. His diverse professional background, from gunsmithing to railroad conducting, shows his wide range of skills and experiences. As a legislator, Windschitl continues to serve his constituents and the state of Iowa with the same dedication and commitment.
