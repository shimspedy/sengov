Daniel Gosa is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Iowa House of Representatives, representing District 81. He assumed office on January 1, 2025, and his current term ends on January 1, 2027. Gosa is also a member of the Davenport Community Schools school board At-large in Iowa, a position he has held since 2015. His current term on the school board ends in 2027.

Gosa's career experience includes working as a journeyman heat and frost insulator. He has also served as a labor representative for the executive board of the United Way of the Quad-Cities. His commitment to public service and community involvement is evident in his various roles and responsibilities.

Gosa attended schools in the Davenport Community School District, which has given him a deep understanding of the local education system and the needs of students and educators. His background in labor and community service has equipped him with the skills and knowledge to advocate for the interests of his constituents effectively.

In the 2024 general election, Gosa ran for election to the Iowa House of Representatives to represent District 81. He won the election with a significant majority, securing 94.5% of the votes. His victory in the election is a testament to his dedication and the trust that the community has placed in him.

As a state representative, Gosa serves on several standing committees, including Labor and Workforce, Local Government, Public Safety, and Transportation. He is also a member of the Appropriations Subcommittee on Federal and Other Funds. His work on these committees allows him to address various issues and contribute to the development of policies that benefit the people of Iowa.
