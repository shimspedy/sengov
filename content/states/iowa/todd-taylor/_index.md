Todd Taylor, born on May 21, 1966, in Ames, Iowa, is a prominent figure in the Iowa State Senate. He represents the 40th District as a member of the Democratic Party. His political journey began in the Iowa House of Representatives, where he served from 1995 to 2019, representing the 70th District.

Taylor's educational background includes a Bachelor of Arts degree from Graceland College and a Bachelor of Science degree in Political Science from the University of Northern Iowa. His career experience includes working as a staff representative with AFSCME. He has also served on the Bowhay Institute for Legislative Leadership Development Selection Board and the executive board of the Hawkeye Labor Council.

In the Iowa House, Taylor served on several committees, including the Appropriations, Labor, and State Government committees. He also served as the ranking member of the Justice System Appropriations Subcommittee and as a member of the Iowa Law Enforcement Academy Council and the Statewide Fire and Police Retirement Board of Trustees.

Taylor was first elected in a June 27, 1995 special election, succeeding fellow Democrat Richard Running in the 54th District. Over the years, he has been re-elected multiple times, demonstrating his commitment to public service and the trust placed in him by his constituents.

In 2018, Taylor was elected to his first term in the Iowa Senate, after serving in the Iowa House of Representatives. He represents Senate District 35, which includes portions of Cedar Rapids and the southwest corner of Linn County, including Fairfax, Covington, and East Walford.

As a member of the Iowa State Senate, Taylor has continued to serve on various committees, contributing his expertise and leadership. His current term ends on January 2, 2025.

Throughout his political career, Todd Taylor has demonstrated a strong commitment to public service and has made significant contributions to the state of Iowa. His dedication and leadership continue to have a positive impact on his constituents and the state as a whole.
