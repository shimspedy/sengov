Jeff Reichman is an American politician serving as a member of the Iowa Senate from the 50th district. He was elected in November 2020 and assumed office on January 11, 2021.

Reichman was born on May 6, 1966, in Keokuk, Iowa. After graduating from Keokuk High School, he pursued higher education and earned an associate degree from Southeastern Community College. He furthered his studies at Iowa Wesleyan University, where he received a Bachelor of Business Administration.

Before entering politics, Reichman served in the United States Marine Corps, retiring with the rank of lieutenant colonel. During his military career, he served two tours in Iraq. His dedication to service did not end with his military career. After retiring from the military, he founded Tri-State Home Inspection, demonstrating his entrepreneurial spirit.

As a state senator, Reichman has been active in various committees. He serves as vice chair of the state Senate Veterans Affairs Committee and is a member of the Iowa Innovation Council. He also holds the position of Assistant Majority Leader.

Reichman's political journey has been marked by his commitment to his constituents and his dedication to public service. His experiences, both in the military and in the business world, have shaped his approach to his role as a state senator. He continues to serve the people of Iowa with the same dedication and commitment he demonstrated during his military service.
