Kimberly Landry Coates is an American politician currently serving as a member of the Louisiana House of Representatives from the 73rd district. She is a member of the Republican Party and represents parts of Tangipahoa Parish. She has been in office since January 8, 2024.

Before her political career, Kimberly Coates was a small business owner. She earned her bachelor's degree from Southeastern Louisiana University. Her professional experience includes working as a small business owner.

Kimberly Coates won the election to the Louisiana House of Representatives to represent District 73 outright in the primary on October 14, 2023, after the general election was canceled. She assumed office on January 8, 2024. Her current term ends on January 10, 2028.

In the primary for Louisiana House of Representatives District 73 held on October 14, 2023, Kimberly Landry Coates won the election outright against Braville LeBlanc and Michael Chatellier. She secured 53.9% of the votes, amounting to 6,244 votes in total.

As a representative, Kimberly Coates is committed to serving her constituents and making a positive impact on her community. Her tenure in the Louisiana House of Representatives has been marked by her dedication to public service and her commitment to the principles of the Republican Party.
