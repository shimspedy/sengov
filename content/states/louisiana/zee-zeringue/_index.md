Jerome Zeringue, also known as Zee, is a member of the Louisiana House of Representatives, representing District 52. He assumed office on January 11, 2016, and his current term ends on January 10, 2028.

Zeringue is a member of the Republican Party. He was first elected to the chamber in 2015 and has since been re-elected in 2019 and 2023 to continue his representation of District 52.

Born in Thibodaux, Louisiana, Zeringue graduated from Thibodaux High School and went on to earn both a bachelor's and a master's degree from Louisiana State University, with degrees in zoology and fisheries biology, respectively.

In addition to his political career, Zeringue has worked as a principle with Progressive Coastal Consultants. His career experience includes a strong background in coastal and environmental issues, which has been a significant asset in his role as a representative.

Throughout his tenure, Zeringue has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 term, he served as the Chairman of the Appropriations Committee and the Joint Legislative Budget Committee. He was also a member of the Technology and Cybersecurity Committee and the Select Committee on Louisiana Economic Recovery.

In the 2021-2022 term, Zeringue served as the Chair of the Appropriations Committee and the Vice chair of the Joint Legislative Budget Committee. He was also a member of the Joint Capital Outlay Committee.

In the 2019-2020 term, Zeringue was assigned to the Joint Legislative Budget Committee, the Appropriations Committee, the Judiciary Committee, and the Natural Resources and Environment Committee.

Zeringue's dedication to his district and his commitment to addressing the issues that matter most to his constituents have been evident throughout his time in office. His work in the Louisiana House of Representatives continues to have a significant impact on District 52 and the state of Louisiana as a whole.
