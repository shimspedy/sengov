Shaun Mena, a member of the Democratic Party, represents District 23 in the Louisiana House of Representatives. He assumed office on January 8, 2024, and his current term is set to end on January 10, 2028.

Born and raised in Louisiana, Mena graduated from Warren Easton Fundamental High School. He furthered his education at Louisiana State University, where he earned a Bachelor's degree in Political Science and Government in 2004. He then pursued a Juris Doctor degree from Southern University Law Center, which he obtained in 2011.

Before his political career, Mena worked as a policy analyst for former Governor Kathleen Babineaux Blanco. He also has experience as an entrepreneur and as an attorney at The Mena Law Firm.

Mena ran for election to represent District 23 in the Louisiana House of Representatives and won in the general election held on November 18, 2023. He defeated Tammy Savoie, securing 57.9% of the votes. This victory marked the beginning of his political career as a representative.

As a representative, Mena is committed to serving his constituents and making a positive impact in his community. His work in the Louisiana House of Representatives is a testament to his dedication to public service.
