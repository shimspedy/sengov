C. Travis Johnson is an American politician who is currently serving as a member of the Louisiana House of Representatives from the 21st district. He was elected on November 16, 2019, and assumed office on January 13, 2020.

Johnson is a native of Ferriday, Louisiana. He earned a Bachelor of Science degree in economics and political science from Louisiana Tech University and a Master of Arts in public administration from Belhaven University.

Before his political career, Johnson served as the regional political director for Senator Mary Landrieu. In September 2020, Johnson became the vice chair of the Louisiana's Democratic State Central Committee. He also owns a restaurant in Vidalia, Louisiana.

Johnson ran for re-election to the Louisiana House of Representatives to represent District 21 and won in the general election on November 18, 2023. His current term ends on January 10, 2028.

During his tenure, Johnson has been assigned to various committees including the Agriculture, Forestry, Aquaculture and Rural Development Committee, Health and Welfare Committee, and the Transportation, Highways and Public Works Committee.

Johnson's work in the Louisiana House of Representatives is marked by his commitment to his constituents and his dedication to public service.
