Larry Selders is an American politician serving as a member of the Louisiana House of Representatives from the 67th district. He was elected in November 2020 and assumed office on January 13, 2021.

Education:
After graduating from Louisiana State University Laboratory School, Selders earned a Bachelor of Science degree in social work from Southern University.

Career:
Before his political career, Selders served as a commissioner of BREC, the parks and recreation agency in East Baton Rouge Parish, Louisiana. He has also worked in the healthcare industry.

Political Career:
Selders was elected to the Louisiana House of Representatives in November 2020 and assumed office on January 13, 2021. He is a member of the Democratic Party. His current term ends on January 10, 2028.

Committee Assignments:
In the years 2023-2024, Selders was assigned to the following committees: Agriculture, Forestry, Aquaculture and Rural Development Committee, Health and Welfare Committee, and Transportation, Highways and Public Works Committee.

Controversies:
Selders was arrested for DWI, reckless operation, and driving under suspension in the early morning hours of Sunday August 21, 2022. He was accused of speeding and doing burnouts while over the legal limit near the LSU campus, in his legislative district. Selders subsequently issued an apology for the arrest on social media.
