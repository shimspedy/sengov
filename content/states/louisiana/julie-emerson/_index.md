Julie Cathryn Emerson, born in 1988, is an American small business owner and politician hailing from Carencro, Louisiana. She is a Republican member of the Louisiana House of Representatives for District 39, which covers Lafayette and St. Landry parishes in South Louisiana.

Emerson's political journey began in earnest on January 11, 2016, when she succeeded incumbent Democratic Representative Stephen Ortego, whom she unseated in the primary election held on October 24, 2015. In this closely contested election, Emerson polled 6,149 votes (51 percent) to Ortego's 5,902 (49 percent).

In 2019, Emerson was re-elected for a second term, winning re-election with an impressive 70% of the vote. Her political career continued to flourish as she was re-elected for a third term in 2023, garnering 72% of the vote against Mckinley James Jr., a Democrat who received 28% of the vote.

Beyond her political career, Emerson is also known for her work as a small business owner. Her commitment to her community, her dedication to public service, and her entrepreneurial spirit have all contributed to her reputation as a respected figure in Louisiana politics.

Emerson's education includes Westminster Christian Academy of Opelousas, Louisiana, the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, and the University of South Carolina. Her diverse educational background has equipped her with a broad perspective and a deep understanding of the issues that matter most to her constituents.

Throughout her political career, Emerson has demonstrated a steadfast commitment to serving her community and working towards a better future for all residents of District 39. Her leadership, dedication, and passion for public service continue to inspire those around her and make a meaningful impact on the lives of the people she serves.
