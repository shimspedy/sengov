Chad Michael Brown, born in February 1970, is a Democrat from Plaquemine, Louisiana. He is a member of the Louisiana House of Representatives for District 60, which covers Iberville and Assumption parishes in the southern portion of his state.

Brown's political journey began when he assumed office on January 11, 2016. He has been serving the people of District 60 ever since. His current term is set to end on January 10, 2028. Brown has shown his dedication and commitment to his role by winning re-election to the Louisiana House of Representatives to represent District 60 outright in the primary on October 14, 2023.

Before his political career, Brown was an employee of the Louisiana Department of Insurance. His educational background includes a Bachelor of Science from Louisiana State University, which he earned in 1992. He furthered his education by obtaining a law degree from Southern University Law Center in 1996.

Throughout his tenure, Brown has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 term, he served on the Commerce Committee and the House Insurance Committee. In the 2021-2022 term, he chaired the House Insurance Committee. He also served on the Commerce Committee, Judiciary Committee, and House Insurance Committee during the 2019-2020 term.

Brown's career experience includes working as an attorney. As of March 2020, he resided in Plaquemine, Louisiana. He graduated from Plaquemine High School in 1988. His personal life includes his spouse, Hayley Mendoza Brown, and their children, Clayton and Christian Brown.
