Robert "Bob" Owen is a distinguished politician currently serving as a member of the Louisiana Senate from the 1st district. He assumed office on January 8, 2024, succeeding Sharon Hewitt. His current term is set to end on January 10, 2028.

Before his tenure in the Senate, Owen served in the Louisiana House of Representatives, representing the 76th district from 2020 to 2024. He was succeeded by Stephanie Berault in this role. Owen's political journey has been marked by his dedication to public service and his commitment to the Republican Party.

Owen's victory in the October 14, 2023 election for District 1 of the Louisiana State Senate was a significant milestone in his political career. The general election was canceled, and Owen won the seat outright in the primary.

Beyond his political roles, Owen has also made significant contributions in the healthcare sector. He has worked as a healthcare executive, leveraging his expertise to improve healthcare services and policies. Owen's educational background includes a bachelor's degree from Louisiana State University, further equipping him with the knowledge and skills needed for his professional and political endeavors.

In his role as a senator, Owen has been assigned to several committees, including the Health and Welfare Committee, Labor and Industrial Relations Committee, and Transportation, Highways and Public Works Committee. These assignments allow him to influence policies and legislation in a variety of areas, reflecting his diverse interests and expertise.

Owen's political philosophy is marked by his support for term limits. This stance reflects his belief in the importance of fresh perspectives and diverse representation in political office.
