Kerri Seekins-Crowe is an American politician who is currently serving as a member of the Montana House of Representatives from the 43rd district. She was elected in November 2020 and assumed office on January 4, 2021. Born as Kerri Seekins, she is a member of the Republican party. She is married to Michael Crowe and they have two children. She holds a BBA from the University of Alaska Fairbanks and an MBA from Campbell University.

Before her political career, Seekins-Crowe worked as a realtor, a customer service representative for Delta Air Lines, and an aide to Alaska Senator Ted Stevens. She has also been an adjunct professor of business at several institutions including Montana State University Billings, Athens Technical College, Truett McConnell University, Bauder College, and Coastal Carolina Community College.

In April 2023, Seekins-Crowe created controversy with comments she made on the Montana House floor about her reaction to her daughter's suicidal ideation.
