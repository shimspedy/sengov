Steve Fitzpatrick is a prominent American politician and attorney, currently serving as the Majority Leader of the Montana House of Representatives. A member of the Republican Party, he represents District 24. Fitzpatrick's political career is marked by his dedication to public service and his commitment to addressing the needs of his constituents.

Born on December 2, 1978, in Helena, Montana, Fitzpatrick grew up with a strong sense of community and a passion for making a difference. He pursued higher education at Montana State University, where he earned a bachelor's degree in biology. His academic journey continued at Arizona State University, where he obtained a Juris Doctor degree, equipping him with the legal expertise that would later define his professional career.

Before entering politics, Fitzpatrick worked as a law clerk for Judge Thomas Honzel of the Montana First Judicial District Court. This experience provided him with valuable insights into the judicial system and reinforced his commitment to justice and fairness. He later joined the law firm of Browning, Kaleczyc, Berry & Hoven, P.C., where he established himself as a respected attorney.

Fitzpatrick's political career began in the Montana House of Representatives, where he represented District 20 from 2011 to 2017. During this time, he demonstrated strong leadership skills and a deep understanding of legislative processes. His dedication to public service and his ability to navigate complex political landscapes earned him the trust and respect of his colleagues.

In 2017, Fitzpatrick transitioned to the Montana Senate, representing District 10. His tenure in the Senate was marked by his focus on economic development, education, and healthcare. He served as the Senate Majority Leader from 2023 to 2025, where he played a pivotal role in shaping legislative priorities and advancing policies that benefited the people of Montana.

In January 2025, Fitzpatrick returned to the Montana House of Representatives, assuming the role of Majority Leader.
