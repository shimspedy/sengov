Tony Tezak is a dedicated public servant and a proud Montanan, deeply rooted in the values and traditions of his home state. Born and raised in Sheridan, Montana, Tony has spent his life immersed in the rugged beauty and resilient spirit of Beaverhead and Madison counties. These communities have shaped his character, instilling in him a strong sense of grit, compassion, and independence—qualities that define true Montanan values.

Tony's life has been a testament to hard work and perseverance. Over the years, he has been actively involved in various industries that are the backbone of Montana's economy, including mining, logging, ranching, construction, education, and tourism. His hands-on experience in these fields has given him a deep understanding of the challenges and opportunities faced by Montanans. Alongside his family, Tony owns and operates two small businesses and a fifth-generation Montana ranch. These ventures have taught him the importance of making tough decisions, embracing sacrifice, and celebrating the rewards of a life built on hard work.

Tony's commitment to his community extends beyond his professional endeavors. He has been a steadfast advocate for agriculture, small businesses, and natural resources, recognizing their vital role in Montana's way of life. He is passionate about protecting water rights, promoting career and technical education programs, and ensuring transparency in the education system. Tony believes that parents should have a voice in their children's education and that local governments should have the autonomy to make decisions that best serve their communities.

As a member of the Montana State Senate representing District 35, Tony brings his wealth of experience and unwavering dedication to the table. He is a strong proponent of limiting federal government interference in local affairs, supporting law enforcement, and holding government actions accountable. His vision for Montana is one of self-reliance, community strength, and a commitment to preserving the state's unique heritage.

Tony's journey is deeply intertwined with his family. He and his wife, Connie, have been married for 37 years and have raised five sons and seven grandchildren in the heart of Montana. Their shared love for their home state and its people has been the driving force behind Tony's public service. For Tony Tezak, Montana is more than just a place—it's a way of life, a legacy, and a promise for future generations.
