Jane Gillette is a Republican member of the Montana House of Representatives, representing District 77. She assumed office on January 6, 2025, and her current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Jane Gillette has a diverse and accomplished background that reflects her dedication to public service and her community. Before entering politics, she served in the U.S. Air Force from 1999 to 2013, where she gained valuable leadership and organizational skills. Her academic journey is equally impressive, with a bachelor's degree in biology from Pacific Lutheran University, a doctorate in dental surgery from the University of Washington, and a master's degree in public health from the University of Montana. These qualifications have equipped her with a unique perspective on healthcare and public policy.

Professionally, Jane has made significant contributions to the field of dentistry and public health. She has worked as a dental director with Community Health Partners, served as a director with Sprout Oral Health, and is the owner of Mint Dental Studio. Her career reflects her commitment to improving access to quality healthcare and addressing the needs of underserved populations.

In the Montana House of Representatives, Jane Gillette is known for her evidence-based approach to policymaking and her dedication to addressing complex challenges facing her state. She has been an advocate for economic opportunities, healthcare reform, and preserving Montana's values and traditions. Her legislative work is informed by her extensive experience in healthcare and her passion for serving her constituents.

Jane's commitment to her community and her state is evident in her work and her dedication to making Montana a better place for future generations. Her leadership and expertise continue to make a positive impact in the Montana House of Representatives.
