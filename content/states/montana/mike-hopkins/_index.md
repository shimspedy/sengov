Mike Hopkins is an American politician and a member of the Republican Party. He serves in the Montana House of Representatives, representing District 92, which includes parts of Missoula, Montana. Hopkins assumed office in 2017 and his current term is set to end on January 6, 2025. He successfully ran for re-election to the Montana House of Representatives to represent District 92 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

Over the years, Hopkins has been assigned to various committees. In the 2023-2024 legislative period, he was assigned to the Appropriations Committee and the Legislative Consumer Committee. In the 2021-2022 period, he was assigned to the Appropriations Committee. In the 2019-2020 period, he served as the Chair of the Long-Range Planning Committee and was also a member of the Appropriations Committee. At the beginning of his tenure in the 2017 legislative session, he served on the Appropriations and Long-Range Planning committees.

Hopkins has sponsored several bills during his tenure. Some of these include bills on long-range building appropriations, long-range information technology financings and appropriations, revising marijuana laws, Montana historic preservation grants, renewable resource grants, and more. His work in the legislature reflects a commitment to various issues of importance to his constituents.
