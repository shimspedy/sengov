Greg Kmetz is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Montana House of Representatives, representing District 36. Born in Cheyenne, Wyoming, Greg has a strong connection to the values and traditions of the American West. He pursued higher education at the University of Wyoming, where he earned a bachelor's degree in industrial education. This academic foundation laid the groundwork for his professional journey and his commitment to serving his community.

Before entering politics, Greg built a successful career as a business owner. He owned and operated a welding and machine shop, showcasing his entrepreneurial spirit and hands-on expertise. Additionally, he worked as a welding instructor, sharing his knowledge and skills with others and contributing to workforce development in his region.

Greg's political career is marked by his dedication to representing the interests of his constituents. He assumed office in the Montana House of Representatives for District 36 on January 6, 2025. His tenure is characterized by a focus on issues that matter most to the people of his district, including agriculture, energy, technology, and legislative administration. Greg's committee assignments reflect his commitment to addressing these critical areas and ensuring that Montana remains a great place to live and work.

Throughout his career, Greg has been a strong advocate for policies that promote economic growth, education, and community well-being. His leadership and vision have earned him the trust and respect of his constituents, who re-elected him to continue serving their interests.

Greg resides in Miles City, Montana, where he enjoys the close-knit community and the natural beauty of the region. He is a family-oriented individual, married to his wife, Alice, since 1979. Together, they have raised three children, instilling in them the values of hard work, integrity, and service to others.

As a legislator, Greg Kmetz remains committed to making a positive impact on the lives of Montanans. His dedication to public service, combined with his professional experience and personal values, makes him a respected and effective representative for District 36.
