Connie Keogh is a member of the Democratic Party serving in the Montana House of Representatives. She represents District 92 and assumed office on January 6, 2025. Her current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Born in Red Lodge, Montana, Connie Keogh has dedicated her life to education and public service. She graduated from Absarokee High School and went on to earn a bachelor's degree from Eastern Montana College in 1977. Later, she pursued further education and obtained a graduate degree from Western Governors University in 2002. Her academic achievements laid the foundation for a career as a teacher, where she influenced countless students and contributed to her community's growth.

Keogh's journey into politics was driven by her passion for education and her desire to make a difference at the legislative level. She has been actively involved in various committees, including the Appropriations Committee, where she has worked on critical issues affecting her constituents. Her legislative efforts reflect her commitment to improving education, supporting local communities, and fostering economic development in Montana.

Throughout her career, Connie Keogh has been recognized for her dedication, leadership, and ability to connect with people from all walks of life. Her work in the Montana House of Representatives continues to be guided by her values and her vision for a better future for her district and the state as a whole.
