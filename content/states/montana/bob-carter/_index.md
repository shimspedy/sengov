Bob Carter is a member of the Democratic Party serving in the Montana House of Representatives. He represents District 96 and assumed office on January 6, 2025. His current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Born and raised in Great Falls, Montana, Bob Carter has deep roots in the state. He earned a bachelor's degree from the University of Montana in 2021, showcasing his commitment to education and personal growth. Before stepping into politics, Carter built a career as a scientist and also served as a reserve police officer, demonstrating his dedication to both scientific inquiry and public service.

Carter's journey into politics was driven by his passion for addressing pressing issues faced by Montanans. As a legislator, he has been actively involved in committees such as Agriculture, Business and Labor, and Transportation, where he has worked to create policies that benefit his constituents. His legislative priorities include tackling property taxes, improving healthcare access, supporting public education, and protecting the environment.

Outside of his professional life, Carter is known for his community involvement. He has volunteered for various civic and nonprofit organizations, contributing to causes related to agriculture, childhood development, and education. His multifaceted experiences and dedication to public service make him a respected figure in Montana's political landscape.
