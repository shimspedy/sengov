Peter Strand is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Montana House of Representatives, representing District 63. Born in San Francisco, California, in 1965, Peter grew up with a strong sense of community and a passion for education. He pursued his undergraduate studies at Occidental College, earning a Bachelor of Arts degree in 1987. His commitment to learning and teaching led him to further his education at Lesley University, where he obtained a Master of Education degree in 1993.

Before entering politics, Peter devoted his career to education, working as a teacher in public schools. His professional experience includes years of service with Bozeman Public Schools, where he became a respected figure in the community. He has also been actively involved with the Montana Federation of Public Employees, advocating for the rights and well-being of educators and public workers.

Peter's journey into politics was driven by his desire to make a broader impact on his community. In the 2024 general election, he ran as a Democratic candidate for the Montana House of Representatives and won a decisive victory. He officially assumed office on January 6, 2025, and his current term is set to end in January 2027. As a legislator, Peter is committed to addressing the needs of his constituents and ensuring that government works for everyone.

Throughout his career, Peter has been known for his dedication to public service and his ability to connect with people from all walks of life. Whether in the classroom or the legislature, his priority has always been to help Montanans achieve the highest quality of life possible. He continues to live in Bozeman, Montana, where he remains deeply connected to the community he serves.
