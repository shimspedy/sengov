Jill Cohenour is a Democratic Party member of the Montana House of Representatives, representing District 83. She assumed office on January 6, 2025, and her current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Born in Havre, Montana, Jill Cohenour has dedicated much of her life to public service and community engagement. She graduated from Havre High School and went on to earn a bachelor's degree from the University of Montana. Her professional background as a chemist has equipped her with a unique perspective on environmental and scientific issues, which she has brought into her legislative work.

Cohenour's political career spans several years and includes a wealth of experience in both the Montana House of Representatives and the Montana State Senate. She has previously represented multiple districts, including Districts 78 and 84 in the House and District 42 in the Senate. During her tenure in the Senate, she served as the Minority Leader from 2021 to 2023, showcasing her leadership skills and commitment to her party's values.

Throughout her career, Cohenour has been actively involved in various legislative committees, focusing on issues such as natural resources, taxation, and local government. Her dedication to these areas reflects her passion for creating policies that benefit her constituents and the state of Montana as a whole.

Jill Cohenour resides in East Helena, Montana, where she continues to be an advocate for her community. Her extensive experience and unwavering commitment to public service make her a respected figure in Montana's political landscape.
