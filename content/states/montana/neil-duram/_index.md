He pursued higher education at the University of Montana, where he laid the foundation for his future endeavors. His early career was rooted in public safety, serving as a Montana Highway Patrol trooper. This role allowed him to engage directly with the community, ensuring the safety and security of the people he served.

Duram's dedication to public service extended beyond law enforcement. He later became the chief of the Eureka Police Department, where he continued to demonstrate his leadership and commitment to his hometown of Eureka, Montana. His experience in law enforcement provided him with a unique perspective on the challenges and opportunities facing his community, which he carried into his political career.

His tenure in the legislature has been characterized by a focus on issues that matter most to his constituents, including public safety, economic development, and preserving Montana's natural resources. Over the years, he has served on various committees, contributing to discussions and decisions that shape the state's policies.

Following redistricting after the U.S. Census, Duram transitioned to representing District 1 in the Montana House of Representatives.
