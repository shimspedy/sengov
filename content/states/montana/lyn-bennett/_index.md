Lyn Bennett is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Montana House of Representatives, representing District 4. A member of the Republican Party, she assumed office on January 6, 2025, following her victory in the general election held on November 5, 2024. Her current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Bennett's journey into politics is marked by her commitment to addressing the pressing issues faced by her community. She has been a strong advocate for policies aimed at reducing property taxes, ensuring affordable housing, and promoting energy independence. Her legislative priorities also include supporting law enforcement, protecting public lands, and advancing pro-veteran initiatives. As a staunch supporter of the Second Amendment, she has worked to uphold the rights of her constituents while fostering safe and thriving communities.

Beyond her political career, Bennett has a rich background in healthcare. She is a registered nurse with extensive experience in high-risk obstetrics, neonatal care, and maternal-fetal medicine. Her professional expertise has given her a unique perspective on healthcare policy, enabling her to advocate effectively for improved healthcare services and resources.

Bennett is also deeply involved in her community. She serves as the president of Glacier Country Pachyderm, an organization dedicated to promoting active citizen involvement and education in government and politics. Additionally, she is a board member of the Flathead Republican Women's group and actively participates in various youth and community activities across the Flathead Valley.

Her dedication to public service is rooted in her belief in the power of community and the importance of preserving the values and traditions that define Montana. Through her work in the legislature and her engagement with local organizations, Lyn Bennett continues to make a meaningful impact on the lives of her constituents and the broader Montana community.
