Christopher Pope is a dedicated public servant and a prominent figure in Montana's political landscape. Born on December 4, 1952, in Biloxi, Mississippi, he has built a career marked by commitment to his community and a passion for addressing critical issues. Currently serving as a member of the Montana State Senate representing District 33, Pope has a long history of public service, having previously represented District 65 in the Montana House of Representatives.

Pope's educational journey reflects his diverse interests and intellectual curiosity. He earned a bachelor's degree in Spanish literature from the University of Oregon in 1975, showcasing his appreciation for language and culture. Later, he pursued a master's degree in public and private management from Yale University School of Management, graduating in 1988. This combination of humanities and management education has equipped him with a unique perspective on governance and problem-solving.

Before entering politics, Pope gained valuable experience in various professional roles. He worked as a reporter for the Brunswick Times Record in Maine, where he honed his communication skills and developed a deep understanding of community issues. He later transitioned into the private sector, serving as a manager for Solarex Corporation in Maryland and working in his family's retail business. Additionally, Pope has been a commercial realtor, further diversifying his professional portfolio.

Pope's commitment to community service extends beyond his legislative duties. He has been actively involved with the Montana Conservation Corps, serving as its treasurer. His dedication to environmental conservation and sustainable development is evident in his legislative priorities and community engagement.

Throughout his political career, Pope has been a strong advocate for issues such as renewable energy, economic development, and education. His leadership and vision have earned him respect among his peers and constituents alike. Residing in Bozeman, Montana, with his wife Maddy and their two children, Pope continues to be a steadfast representative of his district, working tirelessly to improve the lives of Montanans.
