Kelly Kortum is a Democratic Party member serving in the Montana House of Representatives. He represents District 64, having assumed office on January 6, 2025. His current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Kelly Kortum resides in Bozeman, Montana, and has a strong connection to his community. He earned a bachelor's degree in computer science from Montana State University in 2008, which laid the foundation for his career in information technology. Beyond his professional work, Kortum has been deeply involved in grassroots activism. He co-founded the Gallatin Progressive Action Network, an organization dedicated to fostering civic engagement and progressive values in the region.

Kortum's legislative focus reflects his commitment to energy, technology, and local governance. He has served on various committees, including the Energy, Technology, and Federal Relations Committee, the House Local Government Committee, and the House State Administration Committee, where he has also held the position of Vice Chair. His work in these committees underscores his dedication to addressing critical issues that impact both his district and the state of Montana.

His leadership style is characterized by a pragmatic approach to problem-solving and a genuine desire to improve the lives of his constituents.
