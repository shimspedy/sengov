Larry Brewster is a dedicated public servant and a prominent figure in Montana's political landscape. Representing District 43 in the Montana House of Representatives, Brewster has built a reputation as a committed advocate for his constituents and a leader in his community.

Born in Idaho Falls, Idaho, Larry Brewster's early years were marked by a strong sense of duty and service. He served in the United States Army from 1969 to 1972, during which he demonstrated resilience and leadership. Following his military service, Brewster pursued vocational training at Idaho State University, equipping himself with the skills and knowledge to excel in his professional endeavors.

Brewster's career spanned several decades at NorthWestern Energy, where he worked from 1982 until his retirement in 2017. His tenure at the company showcased his dedication and work ethic, qualities that would later define his political career. Beyond his professional achievements, Brewster has been deeply involved in his local community. He served six years on the Billings Public Schools District Board, where he worked to improve educational opportunities for students. Additionally, he spent eleven years on the Billings City Council, contributing to the development and governance of the city.

In 2020, Larry Brewster was appointed to the Montana House of Representatives, stepping into the role with a commitment to making a positive impact. His legislative work has been characterized by a focus on energy, technology, and local government issues. As a member of various committees, including the Energy, Technology, and Federal Relations Committee and the House Local Government Committee, Brewster has played a pivotal role in shaping policies that address the needs of Montanans.

Throughout his career, Brewster has been guided by a deep sense of responsibility and a desire to serve. His contributions to his community and state reflect his unwavering commitment to public service and his belief in the power of effective governance.
