Rhonda Knudsen, born in 1958/1959, is an American politician and retired civil engineer. She is currently serving as a member of the Montana House of Representatives from the 34th district. She assumed office on January 7, 2019. Her current term ends on January 6, 2025.

Knudsen was born and raised in Culbertson, Montana. She earned a Bachelor of Science degree from Montana State University. Prior to entering politics, Knudsen worked as a civil engineer for the Natural Resources Conservation Service and Bureau of Indian Affairs.

She was elected to the Montana House of Representatives in 2018 and took office in 2019, succeeding her son, House Speaker Austin Knudsen, who was unable to seek re-election due to term limits and later announced his candidacy for Attorney General of Montana in the 2020 election.

Knudsen and her husband have two children, including Austin. She is a member of the Republican Party.
