Jonathan Karlen is a Democratic Party member serving in the Montana House of Representatives for District 98. He assumed office on January 6, 2025, and his current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Jonathan Karlen has a strong academic background, having earned a bachelor's degree in Wildlife Biology with a minor in Climate Change Studies in 2021, followed by a master's degree in Public Administration in 2022, both from the University of Montana. His career has been deeply rooted in environmental and wildlife research, where he has worked as a Wildlife Research Fellow. His passion for conservation and public service has been evident throughout his career.

Before his tenure in the Montana House of Representatives, Karlen was actively involved in local governance and environmental advocacy. He served on the Climate and Energy Board of the City of Missoula, where he contributed to initiatives aimed at addressing climate change and promoting sustainable energy practices. His commitment to these causes has been a driving force in his political career.

In addition to his legislative duties, Karlen has been a dedicated community member, volunteering his time and expertise to various local organizations. His work reflects a deep commitment to the well-being of his constituents and the preservation of Montana's natural resources. As a legislator, he has focused on issues such as environmental conservation, public policy, and community development, aiming to create a sustainable and prosperous future for his district and the state of Montana.
