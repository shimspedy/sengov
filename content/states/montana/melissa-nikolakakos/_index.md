Melissa Nikolakakos is a dedicated public servant and educator who represents District 20 in the Montana House of Representatives. A member of the Republican Party, she assumed office on January 6, 2025, after winning the 2024 election. Her district encompasses east-central Great Falls, Montana, where she has been a prominent figure in the community.

Born and raised in Montana, Melissa is a fourth-generation Montanan who takes pride in her roots and the values instilled in her by her upbringing. She has a deep commitment to education and community service, which has been a driving force throughout her career. Before entering politics, Melissa worked as a preschool teacher, where she developed a passion for fostering young minds and advocating for quality education.

Melissa's journey into politics was fueled by her desire to address the pressing issues facing her community and state. She is a strong advocate for fiscal responsibility, low taxes, and policies that promote economic growth. Her legislative priorities include improving the quality of education, supporting veterans and military families, and addressing the challenges posed by inflation and rising property taxes. She has been a vocal supporter of initiatives like the TEACH Act, which aims to increase starting teacher pay and invest in technical and trade education.

In addition to her professional achievements, Melissa is a devoted wife and mother. She is married to George Nikolakakos, who also serves as a representative in the Montana House for District 22. Together, they have four children and share a commitment to public service and community involvement. Melissa has been actively involved in various community organizations, including serving as a PTA president, a Girl Scout Service Unit Leader, and a leader in the "Raising Readers" program.
