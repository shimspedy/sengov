Matt Regier is an American businessman and politician who has made significant contributions to the state of Montana through his public service. Born in 1980 in Kalispell, Montana, he grew up in a family deeply rooted in public service. His father, Keith Regier, is also a member of the Montana Legislature, and his sister, Amy Regier, serves as a state representative. This familial connection to politics undoubtedly influenced Matt's path into public service.

Regier pursued higher education at the University of Montana, where he earned a Bachelor of Science degree in business in 2005. His academic background laid the foundation for his entrepreneurial ventures. He is the owner of Stillwater Sod Corporation and has also established himself as a real estate investor, showcasing his business acumen and commitment to Montana's economic development.

Matt Regier's political career began in earnest in 2016 when he was elected as a Republican member of the Montana House of Representatives for District 4. His leadership skills and dedication to his constituents quickly became evident, and he was re-elected in subsequent elections. During his tenure in the House, he served on various committees, including the Appropriations Committee and the House Rules Committee, where he played a pivotal role in shaping legislative priorities.

In 2023, Regier was elected as the Speaker of the Montana House of Representatives. His tenure as Speaker was marked by significant legislative activity, including navigating complex and often contentious issues. His leadership style emphasized decorum and integrity, qualities that earned him respect from colleagues across the political spectrum.

In 2025, Matt Regier transitioned to the Montana State Senate, representing District 5. As the President of the Senate, he continues to advocate for policies that align with his vision of freedom, liberty, and respect for the people of Montana.
