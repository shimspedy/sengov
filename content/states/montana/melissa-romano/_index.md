Melissa Romano is a Democratic Party member serving in the Montana House of Representatives for District 80. She assumed office on January 6, 2025, and her current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Melissa Romano has dedicated her career to education and public service. She resides in Helena, Montana, and has a strong academic background, holding an associate degree in photography from Northwest College, as well as a Bachelor of Science in social sciences and a Master of Science in elementary education from the University of Bridgeport. Her professional journey includes working as an elementary school teacher in the Helena School District, where she has been a passionate advocate for quality education. Additionally, she has served as a facilitator at Illustrative Mathematics and as a special project coordinator for the Montana Federation of Public Employees.

Beyond her professional roles, Romano has been actively involved in educational leadership. She served a three-year term on the Montana Council of Teachers of Mathematics, where she held the position of president. Her commitment to education and her community has been a driving force in her political career.

In the 2024 general election, Romano successfully ran for re-election to represent District 80, securing her position with a strong voter mandate. Her legislative focus includes education, taxation, and agricultural policies, reflecting her dedication to addressing the needs of her constituents.

Melissa Romano's journey is a testament to her unwavering commitment to public service and her belief in the power of education to transform lives. Her work continues to inspire those around her as she strives to make a positive impact in Montana.
