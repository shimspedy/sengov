Russ Miner is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Montana House of Representatives. Representing District 26, he assumed office on January 6, 2025, and his current term is set to conclude on January 4, 2027. A proud Republican, Miner has been actively involved in Montana's political and agricultural landscape for decades.

Born and raised in Great Falls, Montana, Miner has deep roots in the community he serves. He graduated from C.M. Russell High School and went on to earn a bachelor's degree in agricultural production operations from Montana State University-Bozeman in 1976. His educational background laid the foundation for a lifelong career in agriculture, a field he is deeply passionate about.

Miner's professional journey is marked by significant contributions to Montana's agricultural sector. He has over 45 years of experience in the industry, including owning and operating his own agricultural consulting business. Additionally, he has held key roles such as fertilizer director and manager at the Wilbur-Ellis Company. His hands-on experience and extensive knowledge make him a strong advocate for Montana's farmers and ranchers, who play a crucial role in feeding the world.

Beyond his professional achievements, Miner has been an active member of his community. Since 2018, he has served as a board member of the Cascade County Farm Bureau Federation, where he has worked to support and promote the interests of local agricultural producers. His commitment to public service extends to his legislative work, where he focuses on issues that matter most to his constituents, including natural resources, taxation, and wildlife management.

Miner's journey to the Montana House of Representatives began with his election in November 2024. His campaign emphasized his dedication to representing the values and needs of his district.
