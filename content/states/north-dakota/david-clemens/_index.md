David A. Clemens is an American politician who has made significant contributions to the state of North Dakota. He is a member of the Republican Party and has been serving as a senator for the 16th district of the North Dakota Senate.

Born and raised in North Dakota, Clemens has always had a deep connection with his home state. He attended North Dakota State University, where he earned his Bachelor of Science degree in Industrial Engineering. His education laid a strong foundation for his future political career.

Before entering politics, Clemens served in the US Army and is a proud Vietnam Veteran. His military service instilled in him a sense of duty and commitment to his country, which he carries into his political career.

In 2012, Clemens became the Chairman of the Republican Party District 16, a position he held until 2016. During his tenure, he worked tirelessly to represent the interests of his party and constituents.

In 2016, Clemens won the election for the 16th district of the North Dakota Senate, succeeding politician Tyler Axness. He secured 4,457 votes, accounting for 54.1 percent of the total votes, while his opponent Axness secured 3,783 votes, accounting for 45.9 percent. Clemens assumed his office on December 1, 2016.

As a senator, Clemens has been a strong advocate for life issues and pregnancy resource centers. He is known for his dedication to his constituents and his commitment to upholding the principles of the Republican Party.

Clemens is also the owner of Clemens Transport. His experience in the business world gives him a unique perspective on economic issues, which he brings to his role as a senator.

On a personal note, Clemens is married to Jody Clemens and they have four children together. He is a member of several organizations including the VFW, American Legion, NRA, and Vietnam Veterans of America.

Throughout his career, Senator David A. Clemens has shown a steadfast commitment to serving his constituents and the state of North Dakota. His dedication to public service continues to make a significant impact on his community and state.
