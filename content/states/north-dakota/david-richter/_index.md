David Richter is an American politician and educator who has served as a member of the North Dakota House of Representatives since December 2018. He currently represents the 1st district alongside Patrick Hatlestad. Richter is a member of the Republican party.

Before his political career, Richter had a distinguished career in education. He is retired from his roles in Education Administration and Faculty at WSC and GNWEC. He also served as the Executive Director for the Williston Council for the Aging. Richter holds a BA degree from Minot State University and an MA degree from Wayne State College.

In February 2018, Richter announced that he would run for state representative. He came first in the June Republican primary, followed by incumbent Patrick Hatlestad. He and Hatlestad went on to win the November general election. Richter was sworn in as a representative on December 1, 2018, succeeding Gary Sukut.

Richter has been active in his community, serving on the Advisory Board for Northwest Human Services and the Williston Community Coalition. He has also been a board member for the Williston Public School, the State Board of Public Education, and the ND Career and Technical Education.

Richter is married to Debbie Richter and they have three children together. They reside in Williston, North Dakota.
