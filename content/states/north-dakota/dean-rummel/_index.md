Dean Rummel is an American politician serving as a member of the North Dakota Senate from the 37th district. He is a member of the Republican Party.

Rummel's journey in public service began with his tenure on the Dickinson, North Dakota school board from 1999 to 2010. His dedication to education was further demonstrated when he served as the Chairman of the North Dakota Education Fact Finding Commission.

In addition to his work in education, Rummel has been involved in various community services. He has served on the board of Badlands Crime Stoppers and has been a part of the Dickinson Rural Fire Department and Rescue Squad. His commitment to his community is evident in his diverse range of service.

Before entering politics, Rummel had a successful career in business. He worked in accounting, sales, and administrative positions with TMI Systems Design Corp. His business acumen and leadership skills were honed during this time, preparing him for his future role in the Senate.

Rummel ran for election to the North Dakota State Senate to represent District 37 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. His current term ends on December 1, 2026. As a senator, Rummel has sponsored numerous bills, demonstrating his active participation in legislative matters.

Rummel's educational background includes a Bachelor's degree in Business Administration and Accounting from Dickinson State University, which he earned in 1980. This solid foundation in business and finance has undoubtedly contributed to his effectiveness as a legislator.

In his personal life, Rummel is married to Danita and they have three children. His family life provides a grounding influence and offers him a personal understanding of the issues that families face, further informing his work in the Senate.

Senator Dean Rummel's career is a testament to his dedication to public service, his commitment to education, and his love for his community. His work in the North Dakota Senate continues to have a positive impact on his constituents and the state as a whole.
