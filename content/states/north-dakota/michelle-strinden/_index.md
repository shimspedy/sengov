Michelle Strinden is an American politician who is currently serving as a member of the North Dakota House of Representatives from the 41st district. She was elected in November 2018 and assumed office on December 1, 2018.

Born in Grand Forks, North Dakota, Strinden pursued her education diligently. She earned a Bachelor of Science degree in secondary education from the University of North Dakota in 1991. Furthering her academic pursuits, she obtained a Master of Science in counseling from Minnesota State University Moorhead in 1995.

Before her political career, Strinden worked as a school counselor in the West Fargo Public Schools. Her experience in the education sector has significantly influenced her political career, making her a strong advocate for school choice.

Strinden's political journey began with her election to the North Dakota House of Representatives in November 2018. Since assuming office on December 1, 2018, she has been actively serving the people of the 41st district. Her current term is set to end on December 1, 2026.
