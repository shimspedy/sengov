Fred Gee Jr. is a member of the Democratic Party and represents District 126 in the Connecticut House of Representatives. He assumed office on January 4, 2023, and his current term is set to end on January 8, 2025.

Fred Gee Jr. ran for election to the Connecticut House of Representatives to represent District 126 and won in the general election held on November 8, 2022. In the general election for Connecticut House of Representatives District 126, Fred Gee Jr. defeated Philip Flynn and Joseph Thompson. Fred Gee Jr. secured 77.3% of the votes, amounting to 3,240 votes. Philip Flynn (Republican Party) secured 18.8% of the votes, amounting to 789 votes. Joseph Thompson (New Movement Party) secured 3.9% of the votes, amounting to 163 votes. The total votes cast were 4,192.

During his tenure, Fred Gee Jr. has been assigned to several committees for the period of 2023-2024. These include the Commerce Committee, Finance, Revenue and Bonding Committee, and the Public Safety and Security Committee.

In addition to his legislative duties, Fred Gee Jr. has been actively involved in various initiatives and campaigns. He has been vocal about key issue areas including early voting, health and mental health, and online privacy and data protection. He has also shown support for arts grants for local organizations in Bridgeport and has encouraged Connecticut residents to take advantage of the ArrayRx program, a free program that can save Connecticut residents up to 80% off certain prescriptions at nearly all pharmacies in the state.

Fred Gee Jr.'s commitment to his constituents and his active involvement in various legislative and community initiatives reflect his dedication to public service and his commitment to the people of District 126.
