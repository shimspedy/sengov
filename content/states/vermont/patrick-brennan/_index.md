Patrick Brennan, a member of the Republican Party, serves in the Vermont State Senate representing the Grand Isle District. He assumed office on January 8, 2025, and his current term is set to end on January 6, 2027.

Born in Burlington, Vermont, Patrick Brennan has deep roots in the community he represents. He graduated from Rice Memorial High School and has spent much of his life contributing to local organizations and initiatives. Before joining the Vermont State Senate, Brennan served in the Vermont House of Representatives for over two decades, from 2003 to 2024. During his tenure in the House, he held various leadership roles, including Chair of the Transportation Committee, showcasing his commitment to improving infrastructure and transportation systems in Vermont.

Beyond his legislative work, Brennan has been actively involved in civic duties. He served as a justice of the peace and was a key communicator for Colchester High School. His dedication to community engagement is further reflected in his involvement with the Colchester Republican Committee and other local boards and committees. Brennan is also known for his passion for sports and youth development, having coached girls' softball and participated in activities that support young athletes.

In addition to his political and community endeavors, Brennan is a convenience store owner, which gives him a unique perspective on the challenges faced by small business owners in Vermont. His professional background has informed his legislative priorities, particularly in areas like economic development and support for local businesses.

Patrick Brennan's career is marked by a steadfast commitment to his constituents and a focus on practical solutions to the challenges facing Vermont. His leadership in the Grand Isle District continues to reflect his dedication to public service and his vision for a thriving and connected community.
