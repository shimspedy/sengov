Arthur J. L. Peterson, commonly known as Art Peterson, is a dedicated public servant and a prominent figure in Vermont politics. He represents the Rutland-2 District in the Vermont House of Representatives, which includes the towns of Clarendon, West Rutland, Wallingford, Tinmouth, and Proctor. Art is a member of the Republican Party and has been serving his community with distinction.

## Early Life and Education

Art Peterson was born and raised in Vermont, growing up in the Rutland area. His family has deep roots in the local community, fostering a strong sense of belonging and commitment to public service. After completing his high school education, Art attended the prestigious United States Military Academy at West Point. He graduated in 1973, earning a commission as an officer in the United States Army.

## Military Service

Following his graduation from West Point, Art served in the U.S. Army for several years. His military career instilled in him a sense of discipline, leadership, and dedication to duty. These values have been a cornerstone of his approach to public service and community involvement.

After his military service, Art transitioned to a career in the telecommunications industry. Art Peterson is deeply committed to his community. He has resided in Clarendon since 1978, where he has been actively involved in various local initiatives.
