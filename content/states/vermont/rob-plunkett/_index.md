Robert Plunkett, a member of the Democratic Party, serves in the Vermont State Senate representing the Bennington District. He assumed office on January 8, 2025, and his current term is set to conclude on January 6, 2027.

Born and raised in Bennington, Vermont, Plunkett has deep roots in his community. He graduated from Mount Anthony Union High School and pursued higher education at Amherst College, where he earned his bachelor's degree. He later attended Brooklyn Law School, obtaining his law degree and laying the foundation for his career in public service.

Before his tenure in the Vermont State Senate, Plunkett built a reputation as a dedicated Deputy State's Attorney in Bennington. His work in this role showcased his commitment to justice and his ability to navigate complex legal challenges. Beyond his professional achievements, Plunkett has been actively involved in community initiatives. He served on the board of the Bennington Rescue Squad, co-chaired the Bennington Charter Review Committee, and was a member of the Mount Anthony Union School Board. These roles reflect his passion for improving the lives of those in his district and his dedication to fostering community development.

In the Vermont State Senate, Plunkett has taken on significant responsibilities, including serving on the Senate Committee on Agriculture and the Senate Committee on Institutions, where he holds the position of Vice Chair. His legislative efforts focus on addressing the needs of his constituents, promoting sustainable agricultural practices, and enhancing infrastructure within his district.

Plunkett resides in Bennington with his two children and their tiny dog, embodying the values of family and community that drive his public service. His journey from a local high school graduate to a state senator is a testament to his unwavering dedication to his hometown and its residents.
