## Early Life and Education
William Notte is a lifelong resident of Rutland City, Vermont. He attended Castleton State College, where he developed a strong foundation in public service and community engagement.

## Career Before Politics
Before his election to the Vermont House of Representatives, William Notte dedicated eleven years to serving on the Rutland City Board of Aldermen. During his tenure, he held the position of board president for two terms, demonstrating his leadership and commitment to the community. Additionally, he contributed to the Rutland Redevelopment Authority, focusing on the city's growth and development.

## Political Career
William Notte was elected to the Vermont House of Representatives, representing the Rutland-7 District. He is currently serving his third term. In the House, he is a member of the House Judiciary Committee and the Joint Committee on Judicial Retention. He also holds the position of House Assistant Majority Leader for Development & Strategy.

## Community Involvement
Beyond his legislative duties, William Notte is actively involved in his local community. He serves on the Rutland Free Library Board of Trustees, reflecting his dedication to education and public resources. His commitment to community service extends to his professional life as well, where he manages a bookstore in downtown Rutland and works as a book editor.

## Personal Life
William Notte is married to Alison, and together they have three children. His family life and professional endeavors are deeply rooted in Rutland City, where he continues to contribute to the community's well-being and development.

## Conclusion
Representative William Notte's career is marked by his unwavering dedication to public service, both in his local community and at the state level.
