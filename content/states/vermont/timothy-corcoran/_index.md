Timothy R. Corcoran II was born and raised in Bennington, Vermont. He attended Mt. Anthony Union High School in Bennington, where he developed an early interest in public service and community involvement. After high school, he pursued higher education at Johnson State College in Johnson, Vermont, earning a Bachelor of Arts degree in Political Science.

Timothy Corcoran II has been a dedicated public servant for many years. He began his career in real estate, working with Mahar McCarthy Real Estate. His commitment to his community and passion for public service led him to run for office.

Corcoran was first elected to the Vermont House of Representatives in 2002, representing the Bennington 2-1 district. Over the years, he has been re-elected multiple times, demonstrating the trust and confidence his constituents have in his leadership. Throughout his tenure, he has served on various committees, including the House Committee on Transportation, where he is the Ranking Member, and the Canvassing Committee.

As a member of the Vermont House of Representatives, Corcoran has been actively involved in sponsoring and supporting numerous bills and resolutions aimed at improving the lives of Vermonters. His legislative efforts have focused on transportation, community development, and public safety.

Timothy Corcoran II is married to Corrie, and they reside in Bennington, Vermont. He continues to be an active member of his community, balancing his professional responsibilities with his commitment to public service.

Corcoran's long-standing dedication to his constituents and his impactful legislative work have made him a respected figure in Vermont politics. His efforts have contributed to the betterment of his community and the state as a whole, ensuring that the voices of Bennington residents are heard and represented in the Vermont legislature.
