## Early Life and Education
Daniel Noyes was born and raised in Vermont. He pursued his higher education at Johnson State College, where he earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Environmental Science. Furthering his education, he obtained a Certification of Nonprofit Management from Marlboro College.

## Professional Career
For over fifteen years, Daniel has been dedicated to serving the community through his work at the Central Vermont Council on Aging. He started as a coordinator and eventually became the Director of the RSVP program, which engages older Vermonters in various service activities. His role involves coordinating volunteers to provide essential services such as building wheelchair ramps, delivering meals, and offering companionship to older residents.

## Political Career
Daniel Noyes has been a member of the Vermont House of Representatives since 2016, representing the Lamoille-2 district. He is a member of the Democratic Party and has served on several important committees, including the House Committee on Human Services, where he is the Clerk. His legislative work focuses on improving the lives of children, youths, and families in Vermont.

## Community Involvement
In addition to his legislative duties, Daniel is actively involved in various community organizations. He serves on the board of directors at SerVermont, the Vermont Commission on National and Community Service, and the Vermont Commission on Alzheimer's and Related Disorders. He is also a board member at Copley Hospital.

## Personal Life
Daniel resides in Wolcott, Vermont, with his wife Amy and their two children. The family has lived in Wolcott for over 20 years, having previously resided in Hyde Park. Daniel is deeply committed to his community and continues to work tirelessly to improve the quality of life for all Vermonters.

## Achievements and Recognition
Throughout his career, Daniel has been recognized for his dedication to public service and community involvement. His efforts have made a significant impact on the lives of many Vermonters, particularly the elderly and vulnerable populations.

## Vision for the Future
Daniel Noyes remains committed to advocating for policies that support families, children, and the elderly. He aims to continue his work in the legislature, focusing on creating a more inclusive and supportive community for all residents of Vermont.
