## Early Life and Education

Lucy Boyden was born on May 1, 2000, in Cambridge, Vermont. She is a fifth-generation member of the Boyden family, who have been farming in Cambridge since 1914. Growing up on the family farm, Lucy developed a strong work ethic and a deep connection to her community. She attended Cambridge Elementary School and graduated from Lamoille Union High School in 2018. Lucy then pursued higher education at Roger Williams University in Bristol, Rhode Island, where she graduated summa cum laude with a Bachelor of Science in Marketing.

## Career Before Politics

Before entering politics, Lucy worked in various roles that showcased her versatility and dedication. During her formative years, she worked as a stable hand on the family farm and other farms within Lamoille County. After completing her degree, she gained experience in content management, working for Wex, a financial technology service provider. Her background in marketing and hands-on experience in agriculture provided her with a unique perspective on the needs and challenges of her community.

## Political Career

Lucy Boyden's interest in politics was sparked at a young age. As a middle school student, she served as a legislative page, which gave her an early glimpse into the workings of government. She later returned to the Capitol as a legislative intern with the Agriculture & Forestry Committee. These experiences fueled her passion for public service and inspired her to run for office.

In the general election on November 8, 2022, Lucy Boyden defeated the Republican nominee, Rebecca Pitre, to become the representative for Lamoille District 3 in the Vermont House of Representatives. She took office on January 4, 2023. As a member of the Democratic Party, Lucy has been actively involved in legislative efforts, serving on the House Committee on Government Operations and Military Affairs, where she holds the position of Clerk.

## Personal Life

Lucy Boyden resides in Cambridge, Vermont, where she continues to be an active member of her community. She enjoys a variety of hobbies, including gardening, biking, skiing, and baking. Her deep-rooted connection to her hometown and her commitment to public service make her a dedicated and effective representative for her constituents.
