Senator Virginia "Ginny" Lyons, a dedicated Democrat, represents Chittenden County in the Vermont Senate. Born and raised in Williston, Vermont, she has been a prominent figure in Vermont politics for many years. Her educational journey took her through Drew University and Rutgers University, culminating in a doctorate from the University of Vermont (UVM).

With over three decades of experience in teaching, research, and administration in the biological sciences, Senator Lyons has made significant contributions to academia. She has held positions at various institutions, including Trinity College in Vermont, where she has influenced countless students and colleagues with her expertise and dedication.

Senator Lyons' political career is marked by her commitment to environmental and health issues. She chaired the Senate Committee on Natural Resources and Energy from 2003 to 2013, where she played a pivotal role in Vermont's early responses to climate change, renewable energy development, stormwater protection, land use planning, and the reduction of toxic substances in the environment. Her leadership in these areas has helped shape Vermont's progressive environmental policies.

Currently, Senator Lyons chairs the Senate Committee on Health and Welfare. Her legislative efforts are deeply rooted in her commitment to improving the lives of Vermonters. She focuses on policies that reduce trauma and toxins affecting children and human development, linking medical care with community services to ensure comprehensive health care reform. Her work aims to provide universal access to high-quality, affordable health care for all.

In addition to her legislative duties, Senator Lyons serves on various community boards and organizations, reflecting her dedication to public service and community engagement. Her contributions extend beyond the Senate, as she actively participates in initiatives that promote the well-being of Vermonters.

Senator Lyons' career is a testament to her unwavering commitment to public service, education, and environmental stewardship. Her efforts continue to make a significant impact on the lives of those she serves, and she remains a respected and influential figure in Vermont politics.
