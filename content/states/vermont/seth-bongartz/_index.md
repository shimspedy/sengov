Seth Bongartz is a member of the Democratic Party serving in the Vermont State Senate. He represents the Bennington District, having assumed office on January 8, 2025. His current term is set to end on January 6, 2027.

Seth Bongartz has a long history of public service and professional accomplishments. Before his current role in the Vermont State Senate, he served in the Vermont House of Representatives, representing the Bennington 4 District. His career in public office has been marked by a commitment to addressing environmental issues, economic development, and community well-being. 

Beyond his legislative work, Bongartz has an extensive background in law and nonprofit leadership. He practiced law for over a decade before transitioning to become the president of Hildene, The Lincoln Family Home, in Manchester, Vermont. During his tenure, he transformed the historic site into a renowned cultural and educational destination. His leadership extended to various community initiatives, including serving as a principal in the consulting firm Gubb & Bongartz, which focuses on empowering nonprofits to achieve their goals.

Bongartz has also been deeply involved in education and community development. He served on the board of Burr and Burton Academy for nearly two decades, including 15 years as its chair. Additionally, he is a founder and the current chair of the Northshire Community Land Trust, an organization dedicated to affordable housing and sustainable development. His dedication to fostering economic opportunity is evident in his role as chair of Grow America, a nationwide nonprofit organization.

Throughout his career, Seth Bongartz has demonstrated a steadfast commitment to public service, community engagement, and environmental stewardship. His work continues to have a lasting impact on the people and communities he serves.
