## Early Life and Education
Casey Toof was born and raised in St. Albans, Vermont. He attended St. Albans City Elementary School and Bellows Free Academy. Toof pursued higher education at Castleton University, where he earned a degree in History and Education.

## Career and Political Involvement
Casey Toof has a diverse career background. He owns his own consulting firm, Toof Consulting, which specializes in social media and email marketing. His involvement in the community extends beyond his professional work. He has coached various youth programs around Franklin County and assisted his brother with the varsity boys' basketball program at Bellows Free Academy from 2011 to 2016.

## Political Career
In 2019, Casey Toof was elected to represent the Franklin-3-1 district in the Vermont House of Representatives, succeeding Corey Parent. His dedication and service led to his re-election in 2022, this time representing the Franklin-8 district. As a member of the Vermont House of Representatives, Toof serves on the House Committee on Education as the Ranking Member and is also a member of the House Rules Committee.

## Personal Life
Casey Toof is married to Ashley Lockerby, and they have two children, Callan and Lukas. The family resides on Clyde Allen Drive in St. Albans Town. Toof is actively involved in his community, serving as the Chair of the St. Albans Town Planning Commission and being a member of the Sons of the American Legion.

## Community Engagement
Toof's commitment to his community is evident through his various roles and contributions. He is a Co-Chair of the Vermont Future Caucus, which focuses on addressing issues pertinent to the future of Vermont. His work in the legislature and his community involvement highlight his dedication to public service and improving the lives of his constituents.

## Conclusion
Representative Casey Toof's journey from a local educator and coach to a respected member of the Vermont House of Representatives showcases his dedication to his community and his commitment to public service. His diverse experiences and active engagement in various community roles make him a valuable asset to the state of Vermont.
