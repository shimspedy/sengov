Tracy Miller, a member of the Republican Party, serves in the Utah House of Representatives, representing District 45. She assumed office on January 1, 2025, and her current term is set to conclude on January 1, 2027.

Born in California, Tracy Miller has built a life dedicated to public service and community engagement. She earned her bachelor's degree in Human Resource Management with a minor in Economics from Utah State University in 1992, followed by a master's degree in Organizational Behavior from Brigham Young University in 1994. Her academic background laid the foundation for her career and volunteer work, which has been marked by a commitment to education and community development.

Before her tenure in the Utah House of Representatives, Miller served on the Jordan Board of Education for seven years, including two terms as President. Her leadership extended to the Jordan Education Foundation Board of Directors and the Utah School Boards Association Joint Legislative Committee. She has also contributed to various committees and task forces, such as the Point of the Mountain Working Group and the State Office of Education Computer Literacy Task Force. Her dedication to education was further recognized when she received the Utah PTA Friend of Children Award and the Boy Scouts of America District Award of Merit.

Miller's approach to governance is characterized by her focus on good policy over politics. She is known for asking tough questions, listening intently, and collaborating with others to find solutions. Her track record of public service and legislative knowledge positions her as an effective voice for her community.

Tracy Miller's journey into politics was driven by her desire to make a meaningful impact. She left her career in healthcare administration to focus on her family and later transitioned into volunteering in schools and serving on various committees. Her election to the Utah House of Representatives in 2024 marked a new chapter in her commitment to serving her community and advocating for policies that reflect the needs and values of her constituents.
