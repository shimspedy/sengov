Clint Okerlund, a member of the Republican Party, serves in the Utah House of Representatives, representing District 42. He assumed office on January 1, 2025, and his current term is set to conclude on January 1, 2027.

Born and raised in Utah, Clint Okerlund has deep roots in the community he now represents. He pursued higher education at Brigham Young University, where he earned his bachelor's degree in 2001. He later advanced his academic journey by obtaining a graduate degree from the University of Notre Dame in 2004. His professional career has been marked by significant achievements in the financial sector, including his role as Chief Financial Officer at a biotech laboratory in Sandy, Utah. This experience has equipped him with a strong understanding of fiscal responsibility and strategic financial management.

Okerlund's decision to enter public service was driven by his commitment to the values and priorities of his community. As a dedicated family man, he and his wife are raising four children, with one attending Brigham Young University, another serving a mission in Albania, and two others still in school. His personal experiences as a parent and community member have shaped his legislative priorities, which include education, clean air, and fiscal accountability.

Throughout his career, Okerlund has been actively involved in the Republican Party, serving as a delegate for eight years. His dedication to public service is rooted in his belief in the importance of thoughtful policies that reflect Utah's compassionate and responsible values. He is committed to fostering a well-run government, a thriving economy, and an exceptional quality of life for all Utah residents.

Okerlund's leadership style is characterized by his ability to manage resources wisely, hold individuals accountable for their spending, and prioritize projects that have a meaningful impact. His vision for Utah is one of continued growth and prosperity, achieved through collaboration and a steadfast commitment to the principles that make the state a great place to live and work.
