Joseph Elison is a member of the Republican Party and represents District 72 in the Utah House of Representatives. He assumed office on January 1, 2023, and his current term is set to end on January 1, 2025.

Elison ran for election to the Utah House of Representatives to represent District 72 and won in the general election held on November 8, 2022. He lives in Washington County, Utah, and is a proud graduate of Hurricane High School.

Before his political career, Elison was a business owner and served as the president of the Hurricane Valley Rotary Club. His dedication to his community and his entrepreneurial spirit have been key aspects of his career.

In addition to his role as a representative, Elison has also been involved in various committees. During the 2023-2024 period, he was assigned to the Public Utilities, Energy, and Technology Committee, among others.

Elison's journey in the political arena has been marked by significant milestones. In the general election for Utah House of Representatives District 72, he defeated Ila Fica, securing 81.4% of the votes. Earlier, in the Republican primary for the same district, he had a close victory over Willie Billings, winning by a narrow margin.

Throughout his tenure, Elison has shown a commitment to his constituents and the issues that matter to them. His work in the Utah House of Representatives continues to have a significant impact on District 72 and the state of Utah.
