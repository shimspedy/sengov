Grant Miller is a Democratic Party member serving in the Utah House of Representatives. He represents District 24, having assumed office on January 1, 2025. His current term is set to end on January 1, 2027.

Grant Miller has dedicated his career to public service and the legal profession. Before entering politics, he worked as a public defender, advocating for individuals who could not afford legal representation. His commitment to justice and equality has been a cornerstone of his professional life. Miller's educational journey began at Snow College, where he earned an associate degree, and he later pursued a law degree at the S.J. Quinney College of Law at the University of Utah. During his time in law school, he was recognized for his leadership and service, serving as the President of the Student Bar Association and receiving the Pro Bono Initiative’s Certificate of Service.

In addition to his legal career, Miller has been an active leader in the legal community. He has served as the President of the Young Lawyers Division of the Utah State Bar and represented Utah at the American Bar Association's Young Lawyers Division House of Delegates. His dedication to community service is evident through his involvement in programs like Wills for Heroes and Expungement Day, where he has helped individuals navigate complex legal processes.

Miller's contributions extend beyond the legal field. Before pursuing a career in law, he was a professional trombonist and a member of the indie-folk band L’anarchiste, which received critical acclaim and was named "Band of the Year" by City Weekly in 2013. His diverse background and experiences have shaped his approach to leadership and public service.

As a legislator, Miller serves on several committees, including the Judiciary Committee and the Economic Development and Workforce Services Committee. He is committed to addressing issues such as criminal justice reform, economic development, and workforce training. His legislative efforts reflect his dedication to creating a more equitable and prosperous future for all Utahns.

Grant Miller's journey from a public defender to a state representative highlights his unwavering commitment to justice, community service, and effective leadership. His work continues to make a positive impact on the lives of his constituents and the broader Utah community.
