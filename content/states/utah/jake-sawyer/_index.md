Jake Sawyer is a member of the Republican Party serving in the Utah House of Representatives. He represents District 9 and assumed office on January 1, 2025. His current term is set to end on January 1, 2027.

Jake Sawyer's journey into public service is rooted in his deep connection to Weber County, Utah, where he grew up. From his early days at H. Guy Child Elementary and Bonneville High School, Jake developed a strong sense of community and the value of hard work. His professional career includes serving as the director of sales at Microworks, where he honed his leadership and organizational skills. Beyond his professional achievements, Jake has been actively involved in local politics, previously serving as the chairman of the Weber County GOP. In this role, he worked tirelessly to strengthen the party's presence and engage with the community.

Jake's commitment to conservative values and his vision for a better future have been central to his political career. He has been a vocal advocate for fiscal responsibility, education reform, and community development. His dedication to these causes has earned him the trust and support of his constituents.

As a representative, Jake Sawyer is focused on addressing the needs of his district and ensuring that the voices of his community are heard at the state level. He believes in the power of collaboration and is committed to working with his colleagues to create policies that benefit all Utahns. Jake's leadership and dedication continue to inspire those around him, and he remains steadfast in his mission to make a positive impact on his community and state.
