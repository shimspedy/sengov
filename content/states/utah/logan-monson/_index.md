Logan Monson is a member of the Republican Party serving in the Utah House of Representatives. He represents District 69 and assumed office on January 1, 2025. His current term is set to end on January 1, 2027.

Born in Provo, Utah, Logan Monson has dedicated his career to public service and healthcare administration. His professional background includes extensive experience in healthcare, where he has worked to address the unique challenges faced by rural communities. This commitment to improving healthcare access and mental health awareness has been a cornerstone of his career.

Monson's journey into politics was fueled by his deep connection to his community and his desire to amplify the voices of rural Utah. Before his tenure in the Utah House of Representatives, he served in various leadership roles, including positions on the City Council and as the Mayor of Blanding. These roles allowed him to gain a comprehensive understanding of the issues affecting his constituents and to develop key relationships that would later support his legislative efforts.

In addition to his political and professional endeavors, Monson is deeply rooted in his community's agricultural heritage. Coming from a multigenerational livestock-producing family, he has been actively involved in programs like the local Junior Livestock initiative and has contributed to the establishment of a local meat processing business. His passion for agriculture and rural development is evident in his efforts to preserve and promote these values for future generations.

Monson is also a devoted family man. He and his wife, Sara, who has been an educator for over 12 years, are raising their four children in the heart of rural Utah. His commitment to family values extends to his vision for a community where families can thrive, supported by strong educational systems and sustainable economic opportunities.

As a legislator, Logan Monson is dedicated to representing the unique needs of his district. He is passionate about ensuring that rural voices are heard and that the challenges faced by these communities are addressed with practical and effective solutions. His work in the Utah House of Representatives reflects his unwavering commitment to building a stronger, more resilient future for his constituents.
