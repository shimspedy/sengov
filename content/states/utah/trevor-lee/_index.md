Trevor Lee is a member of the Utah House of Representatives, representing District 16. He is affiliated with the Republican Party. He assumed office on January 1, 2023, and his current term ends on January 1, 2025.

Trevor Lee ran for election to the Utah House of Representatives to represent District 16 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. In this election, he defeated incumbent Stephen Handy and Brent Zimmerman. He secured 48.6% of the votes, amounting to 6,375 votes.

Born and raised in Layton, Utah, Trevor Lee completed his high school education at Davis High School. He further pursued his education at Weber State University, where he earned a bachelor's degree in Liberal Arts.

Before his political career, Trevor Lee worked in various sectors including business, management, finance, loans, and insurance. His professional background also includes a stint as a wealth manager. This diverse experience has equipped him with a unique understanding of the challenges faced by small businesses and Utah families.

In his role as a legislator, Trevor Lee has been assigned to several committees, including the Law Enforcement and Criminal Justice Committee and the Political Subdivisions Committee. However, the list of bills he has sponsored is not available due to an error in retrieval.

Trevor Lee lives in Layton with his wife, Kaitlin, and their growing family. He is committed to lowering taxes for working families and supporting law enforcement to ensure safe communities. He also advocates for protecting parental rights and supporting teachers to provide a quality education for children and grandchildren.
