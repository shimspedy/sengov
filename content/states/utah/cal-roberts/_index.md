Cal Roberts is a member of the Republican Party serving in the Utah House of Representatives. He represents District 46 and assumed office on January 1, 2025. His current term is set to conclude on January 1, 2027.

Cal Roberts has a strong educational background, having graduated from Brigham Young University with a degree in Political Science. His professional journey includes significant experience in the private sector, particularly in finance and real estate. He worked on Wall Street for several years, gaining expertise in mergers and acquisitions at Credit Suisse and Moelis & Company. Later, he transitioned into private equity and venture capital, serving as a director at Reef Capital Partners. His career also includes owning and operating multiple small businesses, which have contributed to local economic growth and provided employment opportunities in Salt Lake and Utah counties.

In addition to his professional achievements, Roberts has been actively involved in public service. Before joining the Utah House of Representatives, he served on the Draper City Council, where he focused on issues such as infrastructure development, land-use control, and public safety. His leadership roles have equipped him with a deep understanding of the challenges and opportunities facing his district.

Roberts is committed to addressing the dynamic needs of District 46, which includes areas like Draper and Bluffdale. He advocates for equitable funding to support infrastructure, champions local control over land-use decisions, and prioritizes safeguarding Utah's natural resources. His vision for the district includes fostering economic development, supporting entrepreneurial initiatives, and ensuring sustainable growth.

Throughout his career, Cal Roberts has demonstrated a dedication to conservative principles and a pragmatic approach to problem-solving. His blend of private sector expertise and public service experience positions him as a strong advocate for the residents of District 46.
