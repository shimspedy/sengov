Kristen Chevrier is a member of the Republican Party serving in the Utah House of Representatives. She represents District 54 and assumed office on January 1, 2025. Her current term is set to end on January 1, 2027.

Before her appointment to the Utah House of Representatives, Kristen Chevrier was deeply involved in local politics and community advocacy. She has been a prominent figure in the Republican Party leadership in Utah County, where she worked tirelessly to engage citizens in the political process and advocate for issues close to her heart. Her dedication to public service and her community has been a consistent theme throughout her career.

Kristen's political journey is marked by her commitment to education, responsible taxation, election integrity, and health freedom. She has been a vocal advocate for parental rights in education, emphasizing the importance of local control and competitive schools. Her approach to taxation reflects her belief in treating public funds with care and respect, ensuring that taxpayers' contributions are used effectively and responsibly.

In addition to her legislative work, Kristen has a background as a citizen lobbyist, where she championed issues related to individual liberty both in Utah and on a national level. She co-founded and serves as the executive director of a Utah-based advocacy group focused on informed consent and consumer protection. Her efforts have built strong relationships locally and nationally, enhancing her ability to lead and influence policy matters.

Kristen Chevrier's leadership style is characterized by her dedication to transparency and collaboration. As the Chair of House District 54, she pioneered Legislative Collaboration Committees to foster open communication between legislators and delegates on key issues. Her work reflects a deep commitment to the principles of limited government and individual liberty, which she consistently upholds in her legislative efforts.
