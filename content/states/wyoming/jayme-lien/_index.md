Jayme Lien is a Republican member of the Wyoming House of Representatives, representing District 38. She assumed office on January 6, 2025, and her current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Jayme Lien's journey to public service is deeply rooted in her Wyoming upbringing and her commitment to the values of her community. A graduate of Natrona County High School in 2002, she has been actively involved in various civic organizations, including 4H, the Relief Society, and the Thomas Jefferson Center. Her dedication to these groups reflects her passion for fostering community engagement and leadership.

Beyond her professional and civic endeavors, Jayme is a devoted family woman. She is married to Dustin, and together they have five children and one grandchild. Her family life is a cornerstone of her identity, and she takes pride in homeschooling her children, who are actively involved in sports, leadership education, and community activities. Her husband works in the coal mines, further connecting the family to Wyoming's rich industrial heritage.

Jayme's political career is driven by her commitment to preserving Wyoming's traditions and values. As a constitutional conservative, she focuses on minimizing property taxes, defending the Second Amendment, reducing government spending, and upholding individual liberties. Her vision for Wyoming includes maintaining the state's key industries, such as oil, gas, coal, agriculture, and tourism, while ensuring that the voices of her constituents are heard and respected.

In her role as a legislator, Jayme strives to be a true representative of the people, advocating for their rights and addressing their concerns. She is known for her willingness to ask tough questions, stand firm on her principles, and work tirelessly to protect the interests of her district. Her dedication to her community and her state is evident in her efforts to uphold Wyoming's heritage while pursuing a prosperous future for its residents.
