John Winter, a dedicated American politician, has been serving as the representative for the 28th District in the Wyoming House of Representatives since 2019. Let's delve into his life, career, and personal beliefs.

## Early Life and Education
John Winter was born in Cody, Wyoming, United States. He pursued his education at the University of Wyoming, graduating in 1972 with a B.S. in range management.

## Political Career
In November 2018, Winter secured his first election victory, winning the Wyoming House of Representatives seat for the 28th district with an impressive 75.1% of the vote. He officially assumed office on January 7, 2019, representing the Republican party. His commitment to public service continued, and in the subsequent election, he defeated Democrat Levi Shinkle with a resounding 81.6% of the vote. Notably, Winter supported an attempt to challenge the presidential election results in four states by joining Texas in a lawsuit (Texas v. Pennsylvania).

## Views on Wild Horses
Rep. Winter has expressed his stance on wild horse management. He believes that allowing the slaughter of federally protected wild horses is essential. According to him, these horses outcompete other species for grazing and water, necessitating a change to the Wild Horse and Burro Act of 1971. Winter advocates for best management practices that include equine slaughter and processing for shipment to both domestic and international markets.

## Personal Life
John Winter is a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. He shares his life with his wife, S. Diane Winter, and together they have ten children and a growing brood of 33 grandchildren.
