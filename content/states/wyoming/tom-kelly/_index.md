Thomas Kelly is a Republican member of the Wyoming House of Representatives, representing District 30. He assumed office on January 6, 2025, and his current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Born in Elk Grove Village, Illinois, Thomas Kelly has a rich academic background and a career rooted in education. He earned a bachelor's degree from Northern Illinois University in 1992, followed by a graduate degree from Roosevelt University in 1999. He further pursued his academic journey with another graduate degree from Loyola University Chicago in 2003 and ultimately achieved a Ph.D. from the University of Illinois Chicago in 2010. His professional experience includes serving as a university department chair, a role that highlights his dedication to fostering education and leadership.

Kelly's journey into politics began with his successful campaign for the Wyoming House of Representatives in 2024. He ran for the seat in District 30, previously held by Mark Jennings, who endorsed Kelly as his successor. Despite being a first-time politician, Kelly's campaign resonated with voters, emphasizing his commitment to representing the community's values and interests. His approachable demeanor and willingness to engage in meaningful conversations with constituents, even those who disagreed with him, earned him widespread respect.

Outside of his political and professional life, Kelly is known for his strong family values. He is married to Gina Kelly, and together they have six children and two grandchildren. His personal interests and community involvement reflect his dedication to serving others and making a positive impact in his district. As a legislator, Kelly aims to bring his educational expertise and fresh perspective to the Wyoming House of Representatives, contributing to the state's legislative process with integrity and a focus on the well-being of his constituents.
