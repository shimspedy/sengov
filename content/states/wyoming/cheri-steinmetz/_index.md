Senator Cheri E. Steinmetz, a dedicated Republican, has been a tireless advocate for Wyoming's 3rd district since January 7, 2019. Born and raised in Goshen County, she embodies the spirit of independent rural life, community, and stewardship of the land.

Early Life and Local Roots

Cheri and her husband, Corey, have deep ties to Wyoming. They've raised their family and built businesses within the close-knit fabric of Goshen County. Their appreciation for the rugged landscapes and the values of hard work and community resilience informs Cheri's legislative approach.

From the House to the Senate

Before joining the Wyoming Senate, Cheri served as a Republican member of the Wyoming House of Representatives, representing District 5 from 2015 to 2019. Her commitment to public service and her understanding of local needs led her to seek a broader platform in the Senate.

Committees and Contributions

As a Senator, Cheri Steinmetz actively participates in key committees:

- Appropriations Committee : Her fiscal acumen ensures responsible allocation of resources.
- Agriculture, State, and Public Lands & Water Resources : Cheri champions sustainable land use and water management.
- Transportation, Highways, & Military Affairs : She advocates for robust infrastructure and military support.
- Select Committee on Legislative Facilities, Technology, and Process : Cheri's attention to efficient governance benefits Wyoming.

Legacy and Community Impact

Cheri's legacy extends beyond legislative chambers. She embodies Wyoming's resilience, its ranching heritage, and the spirit of self-reliance. In the vast expanse of Senate District 3—encompassing Goshen, Niobrara, and Weston counties—Senator Steinmetz stands as a bridge between tradition and progress.
