Ann Lucas is a Republican member of the Wyoming House of Representatives, representing District 43. She assumed office on January 6, 2025, and her current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Born in Illinois, Ann Lucas has built a life dedicated to public service and community involvement. She earned her bachelor's degree from the University of Phoenix in 2010, showcasing her commitment to education and personal growth. Before entering politics, Lucas had a successful career as a banker, eventually retiring to focus on civic engagement and her family. She is married to Patrick Lucas, and together they have four children and five grandchildren. Her faith as a Presbyterian has been a guiding force in her life, influencing her values and approach to governance.

Lucas's journey into politics began with her active participation in local organizations, including the Conservative Coalition 307 and the Laramie County Republican Party Central Committee. Her dedication to conservative principles and her community's well-being earned her the trust and support of her constituents. In the 2024 elections, she defeated the incumbent, Dan Zwonitzer, in the Republican primary and went on to win the general election with an overwhelming majority.

As a legislator, Lucas is committed to representing the interests of her district and upholding the values of her party. Her priorities include fostering economic growth, supporting education, and preserving the unique character of Wyoming. She is known for her pragmatic approach to problem-solving and her ability to connect with people from all walks of life.

Ann Lucas's story is one of perseverance, dedication, and a deep commitment to her community. Her work in the Wyoming House of Representatives reflects her passion for making a positive impact and ensuring a bright future for her constituents.
