Forrest Chadwick, a Republican member of the Wyoming House of Representatives, represents District 62. Born in Casper, Wyoming, Chadwick has a diverse background that includes working in agriculture and managing Pesco Inc., a supply company. Additionally, he has served as a county commissioner.

## Political Career
Chadwick assumed office on January 2, 2023, and his current term will end on January 6, 2025. He successfully ran for election to the Wyoming House of Representatives in the general election held on November 8, 2022. In the Republican primary, Chadwick defeated Stan Mitchem, securing his candidacy for District 62.

## Committee Assignments
During the 2023-2024 legislative session, Chadwick was assigned to the following committees:
- Joint Corporations, Elections, and Political Subdivisions Committee
- Joint Labor, Health, and Social Services Committee
- Indian Child Welfare Act Task Force
- Small Business Assistance Program Advisory Panel

Forrest Chadwick's commitment to public service and his background in agriculture make him a valuable representative for District 62. His work in the Wyoming House of Representatives reflects his dedication to serving the people of Wyoming.
