Nina Webber is a Republican member of the Wyoming House of Representatives, representing District 24. She assumed office on January 6, 2025, and her current term is set to end on January 4, 2027. 

Born and raised in Wyoming, Nina Webber has spent most of her life deeply connected to the values and traditions of her home state. She earned a bachelor's degree in marketing from the University of Wyoming in 1993 and later pursued an MBA from the University of Mary in 2006. Her educational background laid the foundation for a career that spans public service, business, and civic engagement.

Before her tenure in the Wyoming House of Representatives, Webber was actively involved in the Republican Party, serving as a national committeewoman for Wyoming. Her dedication to conservative principles and her commitment to representing the interests of her constituents have been central to her political journey. She has also been affiliated with organizations such as Wyoming Right to Life, Gun Owners of America, and Wyoming Gun Owners, reflecting her advocacy for issues she holds dear.

In addition to her political career, Webber has a diverse professional background. She has worked as a Land Accounting Manager, owned a well-servicing company, and served as a Nursing Home Administrator. These roles have equipped her with a unique perspective on the challenges and opportunities facing Wyoming's communities.

Webber's personal life is equally rich and varied. She enjoys activities such as yoga, photography, elk hunting, and training bird dogs. Her love for the outdoors and her connection to Wyoming's rugged landscapes are evident in her hobbies and lifestyle. She is also an active member of her local church community, attending services at the Cowboy Church on the South Fork.

As a legislator, Nina Webber is committed to upholding the values of her district and working tirelessly to address the needs of her constituents. Her blend of professional experience, political dedication, and personal passion makes her a dynamic and effective representative for District 24.
