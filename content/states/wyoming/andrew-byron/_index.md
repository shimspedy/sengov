Andrew Byron, born and raised in Jackson, Wyoming, has dedicated his life to public service. After graduating from Jackson Hole High School in 2003, he pursued higher education at the University of Wyoming, where he earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Political Science in 2008.

## Early Years and Education

Growing up in Teton County, Andrew developed a deep appreciation for the natural beauty and rugged landscapes of Wyoming. His passion for community and public affairs led him to pursue a career in politics. His time at the University of Wyoming provided a solid foundation in political theory, governance, and policy analysis.

## Real Estate and Volunteer Work

Following his studies, Andrew embarked on a career as a real estate agent. His understanding of local markets and commitment to ethical business practices made him a respected figure in the industry. But Andrew's dedication extended beyond the professional realm. He volunteered as a lieutenant for Jackson Hole Fire/EMS, demonstrating his commitment to community safety and well-being.

## Political Aspirations

In 2022, Andrew Byron decided to take his passion for public service to the next level. When incumbent independent representative Jim Roscoe announced his retirement, Andrew seized the opportunity. He declared his candidacy for the Wyoming House of Representatives and won the Republican primary on August 16, 2022, unopposed. His campaign focused on fiscal responsibility, community development, and preserving Wyoming's unique heritage.

## Elected to Serve

The general election on November 8, 2022, saw Andrew Byron emerge victorious, securing 57% of the vote. His victory marked the beginning of a new chapter in Wyoming politics. As a representative for the 22nd district, Andrew now serves on the Revenue Committee and the Travel, Recreation, Wildlife, and Cultural Resources Committee. His dedication to his constituents and his state remains unwavering.
