J.R. Riggins is a Republican member of the Wyoming House of Representatives, representing District 59. He assumed office on January 6, 2025, and his current term is set to end on January 4, 2027.

Born in Kadoka, South Dakota, J.R. Riggins graduated from Kadoka High School. His professional background includes working as a project coordinator, a role that has equipped him with organizational and leadership skills. Riggins has been actively involved in his community and is known for his commitment to conservative principles, including limited government, property tax reform, and the defense of constitutional rights.

Riggins' journey into politics was driven by his desire to address the concerns of his constituents and ensure that their voices are heard in the legislative process. He has been a vocal advocate for policies that support Wyoming's energy industry, protect individual liberties, and promote rational governance. His dedication to these causes has earned him the trust and support of his district.

Outside of his political career, Riggins is a family man. He and his wife, Leslie, have been married for over four decades and have two grown children and five grandchildren. His personal experiences and values deeply influence his approach to public service, as he strives to create a better future for the next generation.

Riggins' tenure in the Wyoming House of Representatives reflects his unwavering commitment to his constituents and his belief in the importance of preserving Wyoming's unique way of life. Through his work, he continues to champion the values and priorities of the people he represents.
