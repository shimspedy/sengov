# Biography of Senator Fano Frank Shimasaki

Senator Fano Frank Shimasaki is a distinguished member of the American Samoa Senate, representing District 6. His journey to the Senate is marked by a deep commitment to public service and a rich heritage that blends Japanese and Samoan cultures.

## Early Life and Education

Fano Frank Shimasaki was born in Faga'alu, American Samoa, to Masaitchido Shimasaki, a Japanese businessman, and Fano Solinuu Shimasaki, a prominent Samoan chieftess. His mother, Fano Solinuu, was a trailblazer in her own right, being the first woman to serve in the American Samoa Senate. Growing up in a family that valued both tradition and progress, Fano was instilled with a strong sense of duty and community service from an early age.

## Career Beginnings

In 1937, Fano began his career in public service as a bus driver for the government. His dedication and hard work quickly earned him a promotion to head mechanic of the government motor pool. His technical skills and leadership abilities were further honed during a two-year stint at the Pearl Harbor shipyard in Hawaii. Upon returning to American Samoa, he took on the role of managing the motor pool, a position he held with distinction until his retirement in 1975.

## Political Career

Fano's political career began in earnest in 1968 when he was elected to the American Samoa Senate from Maoputasi County. His tenure in the Senate was marked by a focus on infrastructure development and community welfare. He was re-elected in 1970, serving until 1972. His contributions to the Senate were significant, and he was known for his ability to bridge cultural divides and work collaboratively with his colleagues.

## Community Involvement

Beyond his political career, Fano was deeply involved in his community. He was given the chiefly title "Fano" in 1947, a testament to his leadership and respect within the Samoan community. He also served as a deacon in the Christian Congregational Church at Faga'alu, where he was known for his compassion and dedication to his faith.
