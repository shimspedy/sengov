Julio Marenco, an American attorney and Democratic Party politician, currently serves as a member of the New Jersey General Assembly for the 33rd legislative district. He assumed office on January 9, 2024.

**Background and Early Life:**
Raised in Union City, New Jersey, Marenco has a diverse background. He served on nuclear submarines in the United States Navy from 1990 to 1994. After completing his service, he utilized the GI Bill to pursue higher education.

**Education:**
- Bachelor's degree from Montclair State University
- Juris Doctor (J.D.) degree from Seton Hall University School of Law
- Master's degree from Fairleigh Dickinson University

**Career Highlights:**
1. **North Bergen School District:** Marenco served as a trustee on the board of education of the North Bergen School District from 2002 to 2015. His dedication led to him being chosen as board president during his tenure.
2. **North Bergen Commissioner:** In 2015, he ran alongside Nicholas Sacco on a majority Latino slate as a commissioner in North Bergen, serving from 2015 to 2023.
3. **New Jersey State Parole Board:** Marenco served on the New Jersey State Parole Board from 2010 to 2024.
4. **Election to the General Assembly:** Following the 2021 apportionment, the Hudson County Democratic Organization selected Marenco and Gabe Rodriguez to run for the two Assembly seats in the 33rd district. They emerged victorious in the 2023 New Jersey General Assembly election, with Marenco being one of 27 members elected for the first time that year.

**Committees:**
For the 2024-2025 Legislative Session, Marenco serves on the following committees:
- Public Safety and Preparedness
- Science, Innovation, and Technology
- Telecommunications and Utilities

**Electoral History:**
In the 2023 General Election for the 33rd Legislative District:
- Democratic candidates Gabe Rodriguez and Julio Marenco secured victory, maintaining Democratic control of the district.

Julio Marenco's commitment to public service and his diverse experiences make him a valuable representative in the New Jersey General Assembly.
