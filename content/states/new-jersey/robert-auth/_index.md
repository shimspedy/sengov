Robert Joseph Auth (born July 4, 1956) is an American politician from New Jersey. He has been serving as a member of the New Jersey General Assembly since January 14, 2014. Auth's commitment to public service and his background in insurance and economic matters have shaped his legislative career.

### Education
Auth earned his B.A. from New York University, where he studied History and Philosophy.

### Career
After replacing Bob Schroeder, who did not seek re-election, Auth successfully ran in the 2013 general election. He currently serves on several committees in the Assembly:
- **Financial Institutions and Insurance Committee**
- **Commerce and Economic Development Committee**
- **Judiciary Committee**

Auth is the Chief Executive Officer at Plaza Travel and Insurance Services Ltd., a company he founded in 1985. His expertise in insurance matters stems from his previous role as an aide to Gerald Cardinale. Additionally, Auth worked as a teacher in the North Bergen School District from 2004 to 2006 and served as an agency manager at the North Bergen Motor Vehicle Commission office.

In 2016, Auth made history as the first legislator from New Jersey to endorse Donald Trump for President of the United States. He was also invited to attend Trump's inauguration.

### Electoral History
In the 2023 general election, Auth secured re-election to the New Jersey General Assembly alongside fellow Republican John V. Azzariti. The 39th Legislative District voters reaffirmed their trust in Auth's leadership. In previous elections, he consistently held his seat, demonstrating his strong support among constituents.
