Yvonne M. Lopez (born July 28, 1957) is an American Democratic Party politician. She has represented the 19th Legislative District in the New Jersey General Assembly since taking office on January 9, 2018.

**Background and Career:**
- Born and raised in Perth Amboy, New Jersey, Yvonne Lopez has deep roots in her community.
- Prior to her political career, she worked in the private sector for 20 years at Wachovia Bank, where she served in the Community Relations department and eventually became a vice-president.
- Lopez ran and won in the 2017 election for John Wisniewski's seat, who had relinquished it to run for Governor of New Jersey.
- She has been a dedicated legislator, advocating for her constituents and working on various committees.

**Committees:**
- **Housing (Chair):** As the chair of the Housing Committee, Lopez focuses on housing policies and affordability.
- **Community Development and Women's Affairs (Vice-Chair):** In this role, she addresses community development issues and advocates for women's rights.
- **Transportation and Independent Authorities:** Lopez contributes to transportation-related legislation and oversight.

**Electoral History:**
- In the 2023 general election, she secured her seat with strong support from voters, alongside fellow Democrat Craig J. Coughlin.
- Her commitment to public service and dedication to her constituents have made her a respected figure in New Jersey politics.
