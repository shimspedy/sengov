Nelida "Nellie" Pou (born May 20, 1956) is a prominent figure in New Jersey politics. She has served in both the New Jersey General Assembly and the New Jersey Senate, representing the 35th Legislative District.

## Early Life and Education
Nellie Pou attended Kean College, Rutgers University, and the University of Virginia. Her educational journey laid the foundation for her future endeavors in public service.

## Career Highlights
### New Jersey General Assembly
- Pou's political career began when she was appointed to a vacant seat in the New Jersey General Assembly on January 29, 1997. She succeeded Bill Pascrell, who had moved on to the U.S. House of Representatives.
- Notably, Pou became the first woman and the first Hispanic to represent the 35th district. Her tenure in the Assembly was marked by active participation in various committees, including Appropriations, Budget, Education, and Joint Budget Oversight.
- She held key leadership positions, serving as the Assembly's Deputy Speaker from 2002 to 2005 and the Assistant Minority Leader from 2000 to 2001.

### New Jersey Senate
- In 2012, Nellie Pou transitioned to the New Jersey Senate, where she continues to represent the 35th Legislative District.
- As a senator, Pou has been a strong advocate for her constituents. She currently serves as the Majority Caucus Chair, demonstrating her leadership within the Democratic Party.
- Pou's commitment extends beyond her district. She has chaired the bicameral New Jersey Legislative Latino Caucus since 2006, promoting Latino representation and issues.
- From 2022 to 2023, she held the position of president of the National Hispanic Caucus of State Legislators, further amplifying her impact.

## Contributions Outside Politics
- Pou's career at the City of Paterson has been extensive. She has held roles such as business administrator, assistant business administrator, director of human services, and CETA training and education coordinator.
- Currently, she serves as a project coordinator for the Paterson Parking Authority.
- Pou's dedication extends to community service. She has been involved with the New Jersey Task Force on Child Abuse and Neglect and the Passaic-Bergen County HIV Health Services Advisory Council.

## Conclusion
Nellie Pou's legacy is one of breaking barriers, advocating for her constituents, and contributing significantly to New Jersey's political landscape. Her commitment to public service continues to inspire others.
