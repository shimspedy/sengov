Carol A. Murphy is an American Democratic Party politician who has served in the New Jersey General Assembly since January 9, 2018. She represents the 7th Legislative District, making her the first Assemblywoman to hold this position in 20 years. Born in Colorado Springs, Colorado, Carol's commitment to public service was influenced by her father, an Army veteran and Bronze Star recipient.

# Early Life and Career
Before her time in the Assembly, Carol held several key roles in the public sector. These included serving as the Community Relations Manager for the New Jersey Schools Development Authority, Chief of Staff to State Senator Linda R. Greenstein (District 14), and Director of Policy and Communication for Assemblywoman Gabriela Mosquera (District 4).

# Legislative Accomplishments
Carol has been an advocate for education. In 2020, she played a crucial role in sponsoring Assembly Bill 4454 (now N.J.S.A. 18A:35-4.36a), which mandates diversity and inclusion education in the school curriculum for students from kindergarten through twelfth grade.

# Current Roles
Carol has served as the Deputy Majority Leader from 2020 to 2021 and currently holds the position of Majority Whip. Her dedication to her constituents and commitment to progressive policies continue to shape her legislative career.

# Conclusion
Assemblywoman Carol Murphy's impact extends beyond her district. Her work reflects a passion for service, a commitment to education, and a drive to make a positive difference in New Jersey politics.
