## Early Life and Community Roots
Brian P. Stack was born on May 16, 1966, in Jersey City, New Jersey. His parents, Edward J. Stack (a PATH train conductor) and Margaret Stack (a building superintendent), instilled in him a strong sense of community involvement. The Stack family moved to Union City when Brian was young, and their apartment became a hub for neighbors seeking assistance or advice. It was here that Brian first participated in providing turkeys and holiday gifts to those in need, a practice he would later continue as mayor.

## Political Beginnings
Stack's early exposure to local politics fueled his interest. As a child, he attended political rallies in Union City, listening intently to local politicians. His passion for public service grew, and he eventually embarked on a remarkable political journey.

## Mayor of Union City
In 2000, Stack assumed the role of mayor in Union City. His tenure has been marked by a commitment to community welfare, exemplified by initiatives like providing holiday gifts and support to those less fortunate. Stack's leadership has made Union City a vibrant and inclusive place for residents.

## New Jersey General Assembly
From 2004 to 2007, Stack served in the New Jersey General Assembly. His dedication to public service continued as he worked tirelessly to address the needs of his constituents.

## New Jersey Senate
In 2008, Stack was elected to the New Jersey Senate, representing the 33rd Legislative District. As chairman of the Senate Judiciary Committee, he has played a pivotal role in shaping legislation. His influence extends beyond Hudson County, making him a prominent figure in North Jersey politics.
