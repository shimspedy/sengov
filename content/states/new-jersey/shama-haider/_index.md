Shama Haider (born December 20, 1948) is a Democratic Party politician who represents the 37th legislative district in the New Jersey General Assembly. She assumed office on January 11, 2022, and has been serving alongside Ellen Park. Haider's election to the Assembly marked a historic moment, as she is one of the first Muslim women to be elected to the New Jersey Legislature.

## Early Life and Background
Born and raised in Pakistan, Shama Haider attended the University of Punjab. During her time in Pakistan, she served as Secretary to the First Lady of Pakistan, Nusrat Bhutto. In 1977, she emigrated to the United States, where she would later embark on a successful political career.

## Local Politics and Community Involvement
Haider's commitment to public service began at the local level. She was elected to the Tenafly, New Jersey Borough Council in both 2001 and 2015. Her dedication to community development, women's rights, and environmental issues made her a respected figure in Bergen County.

## Breaking Barriers
In the November 2021 general election, Shama Haider achieved a significant milestone. Alongside her running mates Gordon M. Johnson (Senate) and Ellen Park (Assembly), she secured victory, becoming one of the first two Muslim women to serve in the New Jersey state legislature. Her election reflects the growing diversity and representation within the political landscape.

## Legislative Accomplishments
Since taking office, Haider has actively contributed to legislative efforts. In the Assembly, she serves as Deputy Majority Leader and holds key committee assignments for the 2024—2025 Legislative Session:

- **Children, Families, and Food Security (Chair)**: Haider's leadership in this committee focuses on ensuring the well-being of New Jersey's children and families.
- **Appropriations**: As a member of the Appropriations Committee, she plays a crucial role in budgetary matters.
- **Health**: Haider's commitment to public health drives her work on this committee.

## Electoral History
In the 37th Legislative District General Election of 2023, Haider secured re-election with strong support from voters. The results were as follows:
- **Democratic Ellen J. Park (incumbent)**: 26,942 votes (36.4%)
- **Democratic Shama A. Haider (incumbent)**: 25,943 votes (35.0%)
- **Republican Robert Bedoya**: 10,673 votes (14.4%)
- **Republican Katherine Lebovics**: 10,526 votes (14.2%)

In the 37th legislative district general election of 2021, Haider's initial victory demonstrated her popularity:
- **Democratic Ellen J. Park**: 33,532 votes (33.77%)
- **Democratic Shama A. Haider**: 32,797 votes (33.03%)
- **Republican Edward P. Durfee Jr.**: 16,193 votes (16.31%)
- **Republican Perley V. Patrick**: 15,863 votes (15.97%)
- **Children&Seniors First Natacha M. Pannell**: 923 votes (0.93%)
