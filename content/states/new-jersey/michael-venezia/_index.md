Venezia attended Rutgers University, where he studied political science and economics. During his college years, he became actively involved in student government and local community organizations. His passion for public service continued to grow, and he decided to pursue a career in politics.

## Political Career

In 2006, he ran for a seat on the Newark City Council and won, becoming the youngest council member in the city's history.

During his time on the city council, Venezia focused on issues such as affordable housing, education, and public safety. He advocated for increased funding for schools, improved infrastructure, and better community policing. His dedication to his constituents earned him respect from both colleagues and residents.

In 2014, Venezia ran for the New Jersey General Assembly and won. As an assemblymember, he continued to champion progressive policies. He worked on legislation related to environmental protection, criminal justice reform, and economic development. His commitment to social justice and equality made him a popular figure among progressive voters.
