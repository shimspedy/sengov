Elliot Burch, a member of the Mississippi House of Representatives, represents District 105. He assumed office on January 2, 2024, and his current term extends until January 4, 2028. Burch, affiliated with the Republican Party, secured victory in the general election held on November 7, 2023.

Biography:
Elliot Burch's educational journey includes an A.A. in general studies from Jones College (2005), a B.B.A. in business administration from William Carey University (2007), and a J.D. from the Mississippi College School of Law (2016). His professional background centers around his role as an attorney.

Beyond his political endeavors, Burch has a lineage steeped in equestrian history. Known by the name Elliott, he hails from a family of accomplished horse trainers. His father, Preston M. Burch, and grandfather, William P. Burch, both earned places in the Hall of Fame for their contributions to the world of horse racing. Additionally, Elliott served with distinction in the United States Army Signal Corps during World War II.

As a representative, Burch grapples with the challenges and responsibilities of serving his constituents in District 105. His commitment to public service and legal acumen positions him as a vital voice in Mississippi's legislative landscape.
