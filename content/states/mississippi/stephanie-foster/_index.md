Stephanie McKenzie Foster, born on March 8, 1967, is an American politician who is currently serving as a member of the Mississippi House of Representatives from the 63rd district. She was elected in November 2019 and assumed office on January 7, 2020.

Foster was born and raised in Jackson, Mississippi. After graduating from Raymond High School, she furthered her education by attending Hinds Community College and Mississippi College. Her commitment to education and community service began at a young age and continued into her adult life.

Before her political career, Foster was actively involved in her community. She served as the president of the Western Hills Homeowners Association and was a board member of the Association of South Jackson. She was also a member of the Parent-Teacher-Student Organization (PTSO) and the Parent-Teacher Association (PTA) at Utica Elementary Middle School.

In addition to her community service, Foster is a member of the Phi Theta Kappa Honor Society, an international honor society for two-year colleges and academic programs. She is married to Jimmie Foster and they are of the Baptist faith.

In the political arena, Foster has made significant contributions as a member of the Mississippi House of Representatives. She ran for re-election to represent District 63 and won in the general election on November 7, 2023. Her current term ends on January 4, 2028.

Throughout her tenure, Foster has been assigned to various committees including the House Agriculture Committee, House Education Committee, Military Affairs Committee, and House Municipalities Committee. Her work in these committees reflects her dedication to serving her constituents and making a positive impact in her community.

Foster's journey from a community leader to a state representative is a testament to her dedication and commitment to public service. Her work continues to inspire many and she remains a strong advocate for her constituents in the 63rd district.
