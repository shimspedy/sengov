Justis Gibbs is a Democratic Party member of the Mississippi House of Representatives, representing District 72. He assumed office on January 2, 2024, and his current term ends on January 4, 2028.

Biography: Justis Gibbs was born and raised in Jackson, Mississippi. His community instilled in him the values of faith, community, and respect. Gibbs attended New Hope Christian School, where he laid the foundation for his education.

He pursued higher education at Howard University, earning a bachelor’s degree in political science with a minor in legal communications. Later, he obtained his J.D. from the University of Mississippi School of Law. His academic journey equipped him with the knowledge and skills necessary for his future endeavors.

Gibbs’s professional career centers around law and advocacy. As an attorney, he has worked diligently to uphold justice and protect the rights of individuals. His legal expertise extends to various areas, contributing to the well-being of his community.

In addition to his legal practice, Gibbs actively participates in political affairs. He serves as a state executive committee member of the Mississippi Democratic Party, advocating for policies that benefit the people he represents.

Election Victories:

2023 General Election: Justis Gibbs secured victory in the general election for Mississippi House of Representatives District 72 on November 7, 2023. His unwavering commitment to public service resonated with voters, leading to his election.
Democratic Primary Runoff (August 29, 2023): Gibbs emerged victorious in the Democratic primary runoff against Rukia Lumumba. His dedication and vision propelled him forward, earning him the trust of constituents.
Democratic Primary (August 8, 2023): In the initial Democratic primary, Gibbs and Rukia Lumumba advanced to a runoff. Their combined efforts showcased their commitment to representing District 72.
