Gene Newman is an American politician from the Republican Party. He is a member of the Mississippi House of Representatives from the 61st District. He assumed office on January 7, 2020. His current term ends on January 4, 2028.

Newman ran for re-election to the Mississippi House of Representatives to represent District 61. He won in the general election on November 7, 2023. Newman's tenure has been marked by his affiliation with the Pearl Chamber of Commerce and Pearl Kiwanis.

Born on December 12, 1955, in Brookhaven, MS, Newman is married to the former Betty Miles, and they are of the Baptist faith. His career in politics has been marked by his commitment to his constituents and his party.

During his tenure, Newman has been assigned to several committees. In the 2023-2024 term, he served on the Apportionment and Elections Committee, Banking and Financial Services Committee, House Insurance Committee, Judiciary B Committee, Judiciary En Banc Committee, Military Affairs Committee, and House Public Property Committee.

In the previous term of 2020-2021, he was assigned to the Judiciary B Committee, Judiciary En Banc Committee, Military Affairs Committee, House Public Property Committee, Apportionment and Elections Committee, Banking and Financial Services Committee, and House Insurance Committee.

Newman's base salary as a representative is $23,500/year with a per diem of $157/day. His dedication to his role and his constituents is evident in his active participation in the legislative process and his commitment to public service.
