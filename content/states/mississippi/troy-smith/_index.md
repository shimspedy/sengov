Troy Moss Smith (born June 29, 1965) is an American politician and businessman. He currently serves as a member of the Mississippi House of Representatives from the 84th district. Smith assumed office on January 7, 2020.

## Education
Smith graduated from Quitman High School in Quitman, Mississippi, and attended Jones County Junior College.

## Career
Beyond his political role, Smith wears multiple hats. He is the proprietor of Long's Fish Camp, a beloved seafood restaurant nestled in Enterprise, Clarke County, Mississippi. The establishment has become a local institution, drawing patrons from far and wide who savor its delectable offerings.

In addition to his culinary ventures, Smith has a history of public service. He previously represented the third district on the Clark County Board of Supervisors, demonstrating his commitment to community welfare and governance.

Smith's journey into the political arena culminated in his election to the Mississippi House of Representatives in January 2020. As a legislator, he advocates for policies that impact the lives of his constituents, striving to create a positive impact on education, healthcare, and economic development.

## Personal Details
Troy Smith is a Republican and adheres to the Baptist faith. He shares his life with his wife, Michell Ballard.

Representative Smith's dedication to both business and public service underscores his commitment to the people of Mississippi. His tenure in the House of Representatives provides a platform for him to effect meaningful change and contribute to the betterment of his community.
