Christy Armendariz is a dedicated and accomplished member of the Nebraska Legislature, representing District 18 from Omaha, Nebraska. She was elected to the Nebraska Legislature on November 8, 2022, and assumed office on January 4, 2023. Her current term is set to end on January 6, 2027.

Born on December 10, 1966, in Omaha, Armendariz has always been deeply connected to her home state. She pursued her education in Nebraska, earning a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration, Management & Human Resource Management from the University of Nebraska, Omaha, in 2009. She furthered her education by obtaining an MBA from Nebraska Methodist College in 2021.

Armendariz's career experience is as diverse as it is impressive. She has worked as a Strategic Sourcing Specialist for the Nebraska Methodist Health System, showcasing her skills in strategic planning and resource management. In addition to her role in the health system, she has also held professional sales positions at CenturyLink Business, inVentiv Health, and Pinpoint Network Solutions.

In the political arena, Armendariz ran for election to the Nebraska State Senate to represent District 18 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022. Her victory was a testament to her dedication and commitment to serving her community.

On a personal note, Armendariz is married to Ralph Armendariz. They tied the knot on December 24, 1991, and have three children: Lauren Gentsch and Alexander and Gabrielle Armendariz. Armendariz's family life is a source of strength and inspiration for her, fueling her passion for public service.

In her role as a state senator, Armendariz is committed to making a positive impact on her community and the state of Nebraska. Her work in the legislature is guided by her deep understanding of business administration and strategic sourcing, allowing her to make informed decisions that benefit her constituents.

Christy Armendariz's journey from a student in Omaha to a member of the Nebraska State Senate is a testament to her dedication, hard work, and commitment to public service. As she continues her term in office, she remains focused on serving her community and making a positive impact on the lives of the people in District 18 and beyond.
