Steven P. Neville was born on June 30, 1950, in Santa Fe, New Mexico. He is an American politician with a long history of public service and community involvement. Neville earned his Bachelor of Science (BS) in Agronomy and Genetics and his Master’s degree (MS) in Agricultural Economics and Animal Science from New Mexico State University. His academic journey at NMSU laid the foundation for his successful political career.

Since assuming office in 2005, Senator Neville has been a dedicated representative for District 2. His commitment to public service has been unwavering, and he has actively contributed to legislative matters in New Mexico. As a Republican member of the State Senate, Neville has championed various causes, advocated for his constituents, and worked towards positive change.

Neville’s re-election campaigns have been marked by strong support from voters. In the 2012 elections, he was unopposed in both the Republican Primary and the General Election, securing a significant number of votes. Similarly, in the 2004 and 2008 elections, he emerged victorious with substantial voter backing. His consistent electoral success reflects the trust and confidence that his constituents place in him.
