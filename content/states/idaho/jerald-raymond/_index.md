Jerald Raymond is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Idaho House of Representatives. He represents District 31, Seat A. He was born in Rigby, Idaho, and raised in Menan, Idaho. He is a proud graduate of Rigby High School and furthered his education at Ricks College.

Raymond has a deep connection with the livestock industry. He has been involved in all aspects of it, including artificial insemination (AI), embryo transfer, cow/calf management, and feedlot sectors. His commitment and contributions to the livestock industry have been recognized with service awards from Ricks College and Applied Reproduction Strategies in Beef Cattle (ARSBC).

Before his tenure in the Idaho House of Representatives, Raymond served as the president of the Idaho Cattle Association and was a commissioner for Jefferson County, Idaho. His leadership roles in these positions demonstrate his commitment to serving his community and the state of Idaho.

In addition to his professional and public service roles, Raymond is also deeply engaged in local civic and church activities. This involvement showcases his dedication to his community and his desire to make a positive impact on those around him.

On a personal note, Raymond is happily married to his wife, Cheri. They have been together for 45 years and are proud parents of six children. They are also blessed with 24 grandchildren. Raymond and his family reside in Menan, Idaho.

Raymond's journey from a rancher and livestock consultant to a respected member of the Idaho House of Representatives is a testament to his hard work, dedication, and commitment to serving his community and the state of Idaho.
