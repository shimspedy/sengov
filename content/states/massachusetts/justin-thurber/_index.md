Justin Thurber is a dedicated public servant and a member of the Massachusetts House of Representatives, representing the 5th Bristol District. He assumed office on January 1, 2025, after winning the general election on November 5, 2024. Thurber, a member of the Republican Party, succeeded Patricia Haddad in this role.

Before entering politics, Justin Thurber served in the U.S. Air Force Reserve, where he developed a strong sense of duty and commitment to his country. His military background has greatly influenced his approach to public service, emphasizing discipline, integrity, and a focus on the well-being of his constituents.

As a state representative, Thurber has been actively involved in various legislative initiatives aimed at improving the lives of the people in his district. He has sponsored and co-sponsored numerous bills, addressing issues such as tax relief for parents with children in private schools, reimbursement for cities and towns affected by the decommissioning of power plants, and property tax caps for seniors. Thurber is also a strong advocate for bodily autonomy and family integrity, as well as corporate transparency when dealing with local governments.

In addition to his legislative work, Justin Thurber is known for his dedication to community engagement. He regularly meets with constituents to listen to their concerns and gather feedback on how to better serve their needs. Thurber's approachable and empathetic nature has earned him the respect and trust of the people he represents.

Throughout his tenure, Representative Thurber has demonstrated a commitment to transparency, accountability, and effective governance. His efforts have not only benefited the 5th Bristol District but have also contributed to the overall progress and development of the state of Massachusetts. As he continues to serve in the Massachusetts House of Representatives, Justin Thurber remains focused on creating positive change and ensuring a brighter future for his constituents.
