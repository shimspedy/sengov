Becky Carney is a Democratic member of the North Carolina House of Representatives. She has been representing the 102nd district since 2003. Her district includes constituents in Mecklenburg county.

Carney was born on December 25, 1944. Before her political career, she worked as an educational liaison. She is also a homemaker from Charlotte, North Carolina. In 2002, Carney defeated Libertarian Daniel Elmaleh in the general election. She had previously been elected in 1996 to the Mecklenburg Board of County Commissioners and served three terms, serving as vice chairman several times.

Her legislative office is located at 16 West Jones Street, Room 1221, Raleigh, NC 27601-1096. She can be reached at her main phone number: 919-733-5827. Her email address is Becky.Carney@ncleg.gov and her legislative assistant is Beth LeGrande.

As of the 2020 Census, North Carolina state representatives represented an average of 87,116 residents. After the 2010 Census, each member represented 79,715 residents. Members of the North Carolina House of Representatives serve two-year terms and are not subject to term limits. North Carolina legislators assume office on January 1 the year after their election.

Carney is running for re-election to the North Carolina House of Representatives to represent District 102. She is on the ballot in the general election on November 5, 2024. The Democratic primary for this office on March 5, 2024, was canceled.

Throughout her tenure, Carney has been assigned to several committees including the Banking Committee, Education - Universities Committee, Energy and Public Utilities Committee, Ethics Committee, House Finance Committee, Health Committee, House Redistricting Committee, Rules, Calendar, and Operations of the House Committee, and House Transportation Committee.
