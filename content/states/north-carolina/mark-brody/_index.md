Mark Allen Brody, born on December 6, 1951, in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, is a Republican member of the North Carolina House of Representatives. He has been representing the 55th district, which includes constituents in Anson and Union counties, since 2013. His current term ends on January 1, 2025. Brody is running for re-election to the North Carolina House of Representatives to represent District 55. He is on the ballot in the general election on November 5, 2024.

Brody earned a B.A. in education from Concordia University in 1996. His professional experience includes working as a general contractor. He served in the United States Navy Reserve from 1985 to 1993. He has built homes in Union, Mecklenburg, and Gaston counties. He faced several civil suits in a decade that alleged he failed to pay for materials or services rendered, according to a news report in 2012; Brody claimed the allegations were politically motivated.

In the 2023-2024 legislative session, Brody was assigned to several committees including the Agriculture Committee, Appropriations Committee, Appropriations on Agriculture and Natural and Economic Resources Committee, Education - Community Colleges Committee, Local Government - Land Use, Planning and Development Committee (Chair), Pensions and Retirement Committee, and Regulatory Reform Committee.

As of the 2020 Census, North Carolina state representatives represented an average of 87,116 residents. After the 2010 Census, each member represented 79,715 residents. North Carolina House of Representatives District 55 is represented by Mark Brody (R).
