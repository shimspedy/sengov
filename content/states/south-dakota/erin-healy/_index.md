Erin Healy is an American politician who represents district 10 in the South Dakota House of Representatives. She has been a dedicated advocate for her constituents, focusing on legislation that improves conditions for families and children in South Dakota.

Background and Career:
- Erin earned her B.A. from the University of Nebraska at Lincoln in 2009 and her M.A. from the University of South Dakota in 2013.
- Her career experience includes working as a patient experience strategist with Sanford Health, an analyst with Midcontinent, and a consumer insights specialist with Lawrence and Schiller.

Legislative Involvement:
- Erin has served as a member of the South Dakota House of Representatives since 2019.
- During her second term, she was chosen as the House Minority Whip and sits on the House Health and Human Services, Education, and Legislative Rules Committees.
- She actively participates in mental health taskforce committees and is a commissioner for the Midwest Higher Education Compact.
- Erin's legislative priorities include early education, women's healthcare, and child advocacy.
- She collaborates across party lines to address South Dakota's challenges and seeks sustainable solutions.

Personal Life:
- On June 18, 2022, Erin married the love of her life and became a stepmother to two wonderful kids.
- Erin, along with her family, enjoys exploring Sioux Falls, visiting Falls Park, admiring sculptures, and supporting local businesses.

Erin Healy continues to make a positive impact in South Dakota through her dedicated service and commitment to her community.
