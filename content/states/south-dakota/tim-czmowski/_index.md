Tim Czmowski, a member of the Republican Party, serves in the South Dakota House of Representatives, representing District 6. He assumed office on February 18, 2025, following his appointment by Governor Larry Rhoden. His current term is set to end on January 12, 2027.

Tim Czmowski has a diverse background that combines public service, leadership, and industry expertise. Before stepping into his legislative role, he served in the South Dakota Army National Guard for a decade, demonstrating his commitment to his state and country. His academic journey led him to South Dakota State University, where he earned a bachelor's degree, laying the foundation for his professional endeavors.

Professionally, Czmowski has made significant contributions to the cheese industry. He is the president and technical consultant at CheeseWorld, LLC, and has over 40 years of experience in this field. His expertise has earned him the role of Assistant Chief Judge at the United States and World Cheese Contests, showcasing his leadership and technical skills on an international stage.

In addition to his professional achievements, Czmowski has been actively involved in political and community leadership. He previously served as the chairman of the Grant County Republican Party from 1998 to 2004, reflecting his dedication to grassroots political engagement and organizational leadership.

As a legislator, Tim Czmowski brings a wealth of experience and a unique perspective to the South Dakota House of Representatives. His diverse background equips him to address the needs of his constituents in District 6, which includes portions of Lincoln County. Czmowski's appointment to the House followed the resignation of his predecessor, Herman Otten, and he has expressed gratitude for the opportunity to serve the people of South Dakota.
