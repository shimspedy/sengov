Jana Hunt is a Republican member of the South Dakota House of Representatives, representing District 28A. She assumed office on January 14, 2025, and her current term is set to end on January 12, 2027.

Born in Dupree, South Dakota, Jana Hunt has deep roots in her community. Her professional journey has been marked by a unique blend of healthcare and agriculture. As a traveling nurse, she has provided critical care across various locations, showcasing her dedication to improving lives. Simultaneously, she has worked as a rancher, contributing to the agricultural backbone of her region. This dual expertise has shaped her perspective and approach to policymaking.

Jana Hunt's entry into politics was driven by her commitment to addressing the needs of her constituents in District 28A, which encompasses Corson, Dewey, Perkins, and Ziebach counties. Her campaign emphasized practical solutions to local challenges, including healthcare accessibility, agricultural sustainability, and community development. Her ability to connect with voters and understand their concerns played a pivotal role in her electoral success.

In the 2024 general election, Jana Hunt defeated her opponent, Carl J. Petersen, securing 57.5% of the vote. Her victory marked a significant milestone in her political career, as she transitioned from being a community advocate to a legislative representative. Her tenure in the South Dakota House of Representatives reflects her dedication to serving her district and contributing to the state's legislative process.

Her upbringing in Dupree instilled in her a sense of responsibility and resilience, qualities that continue to guide her work.
