Mark Lapka is a Republican member of the South Dakota State Senate, representing District 23. He assumed office on January 14, 2025, and his current term is set to end on January 12, 2027.

Mark Lapka's career is deeply rooted in agriculture, as he has extensive experience working as a farmer and rancher. His connection to the land and understanding of rural issues have shaped his political priorities and legislative focus. Representing a district that encompasses several counties, including Brown, Campbell, Edmunds, Faulk, Hand, McPherson, Potter, and Walworth, Lapka is committed to addressing the unique challenges faced by his constituents.

Before entering politics, Lapka was actively involved in his local community, advocating for agricultural development and rural sustainability. His leadership and dedication earned him the trust of his peers, paving the way for his successful campaign for the South Dakota State Senate. In the 2024 general election, he secured a decisive victory, reflecting the confidence voters have in his ability to represent their interests effectively.

As a state senator, Lapka focuses on policies that support farmers, enhance rural infrastructure, and promote economic growth in his district. He is also an advocate for education and healthcare access, recognizing their importance in building strong and resilient communities. His legislative efforts aim to create opportunities for future generations while preserving the values and traditions of South Dakota's rural heritage.

Mark Lapka's commitment to public service and his deep understanding of the issues facing his district make him a respected and influential figure in the South Dakota State Senate. Through his work, he continues to strive for positive change and meaningful progress for the people he represents.
