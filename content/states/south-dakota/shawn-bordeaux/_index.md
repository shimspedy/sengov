Shawn Bordeaux (Democratic Party) is an American politician who has been serving as a member of the South Dakota State Senate since January 10, 2023. His current term will end on January 14, 2025. Prior to his Senate position, Bordeaux served in the South Dakota House of Representatives from 2015 to 2022.

Biography:
Shawn L. Bordeaux was born on June 9, 1967. He earned a bachelor's degree in business administration and a master's degree in community and regional planning from the University of Nebraska at Lincoln in 1989 and 1995, respectively. His educational background reflects his commitment to public service and community development.

Career Experience:
Bordeaux's career has been marked by advocacy and leadership. He worked as the director of the Institute of Tribal Lands at Sinte Gleska University. Additionally, he served as the chairman of the board of directors of the Oyate Networking Project and was a board member of Dakota Plains Legal Services, Inc. His involvement in these organizations demonstrates his dedication to supporting tribal communities and promoting justice.

Committee Assignments:
During the 2023-2024 legislative session, Senator Bordeaux was assigned to the following committees in the South Dakota State Senate:
- Senate Education Committee
- Senate Health and Human Services Committee
- Senate Taxation Committee

Previous committee assignments include roles in the House of Representatives, where he contributed to various areas such as commerce, energy, transportation, and government operations.

Elections:
In the 2024 general election, Shawn Bordeaux is running for re-election to the South Dakota State Senate District 26. His opponent is Tamara Grove. The Democratic primary for this office, scheduled for June 4, 2024, was canceled.

Overall, Shawn Bordeaux's commitment to public service, community development, and advocacy makes him a valuable representative for District 26 in the South Dakota State Senate.
