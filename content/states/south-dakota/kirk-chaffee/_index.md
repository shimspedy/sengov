Kirk Chaffee (Republican Party) is a dedicated public servant with a rich background in South Dakota. Born in the state, he has deep roots in the community. Before retiring, Chaffee worked as a real estate appraiser, bringing valuable insights to the table.

Chaffee's commitment to public service extends beyond his professional career. For 31 years, he served as the director of equalization and planning for Meade County, South Dakota. His expertise in this role contributed significantly to local governance and development.

Additionally, Chaffee worked as a business consultant for the city of Sturgis, South Dakota. His insights helped shape policies and strategies to enhance the city's economic growth and overall well-being.

In 2018, Chaffee was elected to the South Dakota House of Representatives, representing the 29th district. Since assuming office in 2019, he has actively participated in legislative matters, advocating for his constituents and working towards positive change.

Now, Chaffee is running for election to the South Dakota State Senate, seeking to represent District 29. His dedication to public service, combined with his experience, makes him a strong candidate for this position.
