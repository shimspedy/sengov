Jeff Bathke is a Republican member of the South Dakota House of Representatives, representing District 20. He assumed office on January 14, 2025, and his current term is set to end on January 12, 2027.

Born in Mitchell, South Dakota, Jeff Bathke has a strong connection to his home state. He has served in the U.S. Army Reserve since 1989, showcasing his dedication to public service and leadership. Bathke pursued higher education at the University of South Dakota, earning a bachelor's degree in 1995 and a graduate degree in 2011. His professional background includes experience in management and serving as a Command Sergeant, roles that have honed his skills in organization and strategic planning.

Bathke is actively involved in his community and has affiliations with organizations such as Rotary, Veterans of Foreign Wars, the American Legion, and the Moose Club. These connections reflect his commitment to civic engagement and supporting local initiatives. His career and community involvement have positioned him as a dedicated representative for the people of District 20, which includes areas like Mitchell.

In the 2024 general election, Bathke ran as a Republican candidate for the South Dakota House of Representatives and secured his seat with a significant share of the vote. His campaign emphasized his leadership experience and his vision for addressing the needs of his constituents. As a legislator, Bathke aims to bring his expertise in emergency management and planning to the forefront, ensuring that the district is well-prepared for challenges and opportunities alike.

Bathke's journey from a dedicated public servant in the military to a state legislator highlights his unwavering commitment to serving his community and state. His tenure in the South Dakota House of Representatives is marked by a focus on effective governance and a deep understanding of the issues that matter most to his constituents.
