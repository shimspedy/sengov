Kristin A. Conzet is an American politician and a dedicated public servant. Born on November 18, 1970, she has been actively involved in South Dakota politics for several years.

Conzet first entered the political arena in 2009 when she was appointed to the South Dakota House of Representatives. During her initial tenure, she worked tirelessly to represent the interests of her constituents in District 32. Her commitment to public service led her to serve as Majority Whip, chair local government committees, and contribute to various legislative efforts.

In 2012, Conzet ran in the three-way Republican Primary, securing second place with 1,148 votes. Later that year, she emerged victorious in the three-way General Election, earning the first seat with 5,004 votes. Throughout her time in office, Conzet championed conservative values, collaborating with fellow legislators to address critical issues facing South Dakota.

Her dedication to public service continued, and in 2024, Conzet was reappointed to represent District 32. As a legislator, she has focused on key areas such as commerce, energy, health, and human services. Her leadership and commitment to her constituents have made her a respected figure in South Dakota politics.

Kristin Conzet's unwavering dedication to her community and her ability to bridge political divides make her a valuable asset in the South Dakota State Legislature. Her passion for public service remains steadfast, and she continues to work diligently to improve the lives of those she represents.
