Tamara Grove, a member of the Republican Party, serves in the South Dakota State Senate, representing District 26. She assumed office on January 14, 2025, and her current term is set to end on January 12, 2027.

Born in Yankton, South Dakota, Tamara Grove has cultivated a career marked by dedication and service. She earned her graduate degree from Huron University in 1993 and later pursued a Ph.D. at Colorado Technical University, completing it in 2015. Her professional journey includes significant experience as a consultant, showcasing her expertise in problem-solving and strategic planning.

Grove resides on the Lower Brule Sioux Tribe Reservation with her husband, Pastor Ian Grove. Together, they share their home with a lively group of chickens and an adopted cat named Church, reflecting her connection to community and family life. Beyond her professional and personal commitments, Grove has been actively involved in various organizations, including the Trump Diversity Coalition, the Community Revitalization Collective in Sioux Falls, and the Drug Court of Lower Brule Warriors Circle. These affiliations highlight her commitment to fostering positive change and addressing critical issues within her community.

Her political career gained momentum when she ran for election to the South Dakota State Senate in 2024, successfully defeating incumbent Shawn Bordeaux in the general election. Grove's victory marked a significant shift in District 26, emphasizing her ability to connect with voters and address their concerns effectively. As a state senator, she continues to focus on legislative priorities that align with her values and the needs of her constituents.

Tamara Grove's journey is a testament to her resilience, dedication, and unwavering commitment to making a difference in her community and beyond. Her leadership in the South Dakota State Senate reflects her passion for public service and her determination to create a better future for all.
