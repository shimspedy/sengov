James 'Jim' W. Bolin was born on December 31, 1950, in Portland, Oregon. He is an American politician with a long and distinguished career in public service. Bolin's journey in politics began when he served as the mayor of Canton, South Dakota, from 2007 until 2008.

Education:
- Bolin earned his Bachelor of Arts (BA) degree from Seattle Pacific University.
- He furthered his education by obtaining a Master of Arts (MA) degree from the University of South Dakota.

Career Highlights:
- Bolin has been a dedicated member of the South Dakota House of Representatives, representing District 16 since January 2009.
- His commitment to public service extends beyond the state level. Bolin has also made significant contributions to the community of Canton, where he served as mayor.
- As a Republican, Bolin has consistently advocated for policies that align with his party's values.

Elections:
- In 2016, after completing his fourth term in the South Dakota House of Representatives, Bolin was term-limited from running again in the House. However, he continued his political journey by running unopposed in the District 16 Republican Primary for the South Dakota Senate.
- In the general election, Bolin faced off against Chad Skiles, a Democrat from Canton, demonstrating his commitment to public service and his community.
