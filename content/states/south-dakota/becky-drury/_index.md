Becky Drury (Republican Party) is a dedicated public servant who has been actively involved in South Dakota politics. She currently serves as a member of the South Dakota House of Representatives, representing District 34. Drury assumed office on January 16, 2024, and her current term will end on January 14, 2025.

Background and Education:
Becky Drury earned her master's degree in human resources management/personnel administration from the University of South Dakota in 2009. Her commitment to education and professional development reflects her dedication to serving her community.

Career Experience:
Drury's career has been marked by her expertise in communications and marketing. She worked as a communications specialist and marketing coordinator at Golden West Telecommunications, where she honed her skills in effective messaging and outreach.

Committee Assignments:
During her tenure, Drury has actively participated in legislative committees. In the 2023-2024 session, she was assigned to the following committees:
- House Local Government Committee (Chair)
- House Military and Veterans' Affairs Committee
- House State Affairs Committee

In the previous session (2021-2022), Drury also served on the House Local Government Committee, House Military and Veterans' Affairs Committee, and House Taxation Committee.

Elections and Re-Election:
Drury is committed to representing her constituents. In the upcoming 2024 elections, she is running for re-election in the Republican primary for South Dakota House of Representatives District 34. The primary will take place on June 4, 2024, followed by the general election on November 5, 2024.

Appointment and District Change:
Originally elected to represent District 32, Drury was appointed to fill a vacancy in District 34 after relocating there. Governor Kristi L. Noem appointed her to District 34 on January 16, 2024, replacing Jess Olson.

Becky Drury's dedication to public service, combined with her background in communications and commitment to her community, makes her a valuable representative for South Dakota.
