Tina Mulally is an American politician and businesswoman who has been representing District 35 in the South Dakota House of Representatives since 2018. Elected in 2018, she assumed office in 2019.

Mulally is a resident of Rapid City, South Dakota, where she operates a small business. Her commitment to public service extends beyond her legislative role. In 2020, Mulally was selected to serve on the Mental Health of First Responders Task Force, demonstrating her dedication to addressing critical issues affecting her community.

As a member of the Republican Party, Mulally actively participates in various committees within the House of Representatives. These include House Appropriations, House Local Governments, House Agriculture and Natural Resources, Commerce and Energy, and Transportation. Her multifaceted involvement reflects her commitment to effective governance and her desire to contribute positively to South Dakota's legislative landscape.

Mulally's conservative values have earned her recognition from the American Conservative Union Foundation. She received the Award for Conservative Excellence in 2019 and the Award for Conservative Achievement in both 2020 and 2021. Additionally, Mulally has consistently received high ratings from organizations such as the National Rifle Association, SD Right to Life, Family Heritage Alliance, and SD Citizens for Liberty.

In the 2022 primary and general elections, Mulally emerged victorious against a Democratic challenger, securing her position as a representative. Notably, she played a pivotal role in founding the South Dakota Freedom Caucus in 2022, advocating for principled and effective governance.

Tina Mulally's dedication to her constituents, her business acumen, and her commitment to conservative values make her a respected figure in South Dakota politics. As she continues to serve, her impact on legislation and community well-being remains significant.
