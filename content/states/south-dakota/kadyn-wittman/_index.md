Kadyn Wittman, an unwavering advocate for equity and social justice, has made her mark in South Dakota politics. As a Democratic member of the South Dakota House of Representatives, she represents District 15 with passion and dedication.

Before entering politics, Wittman worked tirelessly in the nonprofit sector. Her tenure at the Bishop Dudley Hospitality House provided her with firsthand experience of the challenges faced by the homeless population in Sioux Falls. As a case manager, she navigated complex situations, offering support and compassion to those in need.

Wittman's commitment extends beyond her legislative duties. She actively serves on the board of Bridging the Gap (BtG), an organization dedicated to uplifting underrepresented communities. Through collaboration and leadership development, BtG strives to create positive change.

Additionally, Wittman played a pivotal role in The Transformation Project Advocacy Network (TPAN), advocating for transgender youth and their families. Her tireless efforts helped educate communities about gender identity and expression.

In 2022, Wittman ran for the South Dakota House of Representatives against incumbent Democrat Linda Duba and Republican candidates Joni Tschetter and Matt Rosburg. Her victory in the election demonstrated her ability to connect with constituents and address their needs effectively.

Kadyn Wittman continues to be a beacon of hope, working tirelessly to build a South Dakota where everyone has a fair chance to thrive.
