Kameron Nelson, a dedicated public servant, hails from Rapid City, South Dakota. He later found his home in Sioux Falls, where he has made significant contributions to the community.

Education and Background:
Kameron attended South Dakota State University in Brookings, where he earned a Bachelor of Science in Communication Studies & Theatre. His passion for theatre production led him to work in the industry for several years before discovering his love for philanthropy.

Political Journey:
In 2022, Kameron Nelson made history by becoming the first openly gay, male member of the South Dakota legislature. His election victory in District 10, centered on Sioux Falls (the state's largest city), marked a significant milestone for LGBTQ+ representation in South Dakota politics. As a member of the Democratic Party, Kameron brings a fresh perspective and a commitment to serving his constituents.
