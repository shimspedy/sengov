Randy Deibert is a Spearfish native, local business owner, and your Republican candidate running for re-election in State Senate District 31. With a commitment to public service, Randy actively volunteers his time with several boards, groups, and non-profit organizations in the local community.

Family and Values

Randy became a father of four when he married his charming wife of twenty-seven years. Together, they have nine grandchildren—one of whom smiles down on him every day. His family values and community-oriented mindset drive his dedication to serving the people of South Dakota.

Public Service and Leadership

For seven years, Randy served as your Lawrence County Commissioner, gaining valuable experience in local governance. Two years ago, voters entrusted him to be their Christian, common-sense conservative voice in Pierre, electing him as their State Senator for District 31.

Committees and Legislative Impact

As a legislative leader, Randy was appointed to summer studies that focused on property tax stabilization and reduction. His expertise and commitment led to meaningful policy development. Additionally, he served on five Senate Committees:

1. Agriculture and Natural Resources (Vice Chair): Randy actively contributed to discussions on agricultural and environmental matters.
2. State Affairs: He participated in shaping state policies related to various issues.
3. State and Local Government: Randy worked to improve governance at both state and local levels.
4. Transportation: His involvement helped address transportation-related challenges.
5. Retirement Laws: Randy played a role in shaping retirement policies for South Dakotans.

Legislative Achievements

As your Senator, Randy sponsored and co-sponsored several bills that have been signed into law by Governor Noem. His dedication to effective governance and thoughtful policy-making has made a positive impact on the state.

Favorite Quote

Randy lives by the words of Abraham Lincoln: "The best way to predict your future is to create it." He believes that active participation and thoughtful leadership can shape a better tomorrow for South Dakota.

In the upcoming Primary Election on June 4, Randy seeks the honor of your vote. Remember, it all starts with a vote—your vote!

Randy and his wife Lori continue to be pillars of their community, and their commitment to public service remains unwavering.
