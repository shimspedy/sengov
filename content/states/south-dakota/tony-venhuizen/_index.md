Tony Venhuizen is an accomplished public servant and legal professional. Born on September 30, 1983, he has made significant contributions to South Dakota's political landscape. Let's delve into his background and career.

### Early Life and Education
Tony Venhuizen grew up in Armour, South Dakota. He attended Armour High School and later pursued higher education. Venhuizen holds a bachelor's degree in history and political science from South Dakota State University. His passion for law led him to earn a law degree from the University of South Dakota.

### Political Career
Venhuizen's political journey began when he served on the South Dakota Board of Regents from 2003 to 2008. His commitment to public service and governance continued as he became the chief of staff for Governor Dennis Daugaard. During this time, he managed Governor Daugaard's successful 2010 gubernatorial campaign, gaining recognition for his strategic leadership.

### Legislative Role
In 2023, Tony Venhuizen assumed office as a member of the South Dakota House of Representatives, representing the 13th district. He shares this responsibility with fellow legislator Sue Peterson. As a Republican, Venhuizen actively contributes to shaping policies and advocating for the interests of his constituents.

### Personal Life
Venhuizen resides in Sioux Falls, South Dakota, with his wife, Sara. While he practices trust and estate law, Sara works as an electrical engineer. Their shared commitment to their community underscores their dedication to public service.

Tony Venhuizen's multifaceted background, legal expertise, and political acumen position him as a valuable asset to South Dakota's legislative landscape. His contributions continue to shape the state's future.
