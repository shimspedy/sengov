Larry P. Zikmund, born on January 25, 1946, is an American politician with a long-standing commitment to public service. He has been serving as a Republican member for District 14 in the South Dakota House of Representatives since 2015. In 2020, he won the vacant Senate seat and assumed office in the South Dakota State Senate.

Zikmund's legislative work reflects his dedication to various committees. He actively participates in the Commerce and Energy Committee, where he contributes to shaping policies related to business and economic development. Additionally, he serves on the Transportation Committee, focusing on transportation infrastructure and safety.

As a legislator, Zikmund chairs the Military and Veterans Affairs Committee, demonstrating his unwavering support for those who have served our country. He also plays a crucial role in the Retirement Laws Committee, ensuring that retirement benefits are fair and sustainable for South Dakota residents.

Outside of politics, Zikmund enjoys a fulfilling personal life. He shares it with his wife, Judi, and they have two daughters together. Their family has grown to include five grandchildren, bringing joy and laughter to their home.

Zikmund's motto, "Experienced. Trusted. Committed," encapsulates his approach to public service. With years of experience and a genuine commitment to his constituents, he continues to make a positive impact on South Dakota's legislative landscape.
