Tony Kayser is a Republican member of the South Dakota House of Representatives, representing District 14. He assumed office on January 14, 2025, and his current term is set to end on January 12, 2027.

Born and raised on a dairy farm outside Alexandria, South Dakota, Tony Kayser grew up as the fifth oldest of fifteen siblings. His upbringing instilled in him a strong work ethic and a deep appreciation for family values. After completing his education, Kayser embarked on a career in the financial services industry in 1983. Over the years, he has built a successful business, helping families and small businesses achieve their financial goals.

Kayser is deeply involved in his community and church. He is an active member and leader at Holy Spirit Parish and volunteers weekly at the Bishop Dudley Homeless Shelter. His commitment to service extends to initiatives like starting a garden program that provides thousands of meals annually to feed the homeless. Additionally, he serves on the Board of Directors for organizations such as the Catholic Men’s Business Fraternity and Brothers in Christ.

As a legislator, Kayser is committed to upholding his core beliefs and values. He advocates for pro-life policies, parental rights, limited government, lower taxes, and election integrity. His approach to governance emphasizes listening to all sides of an issue and making well-informed decisions. Kayser’s dedication to his constituents and his principles reflects his desire to serve both God and his neighbors while remaining true to his roots and values.
