David Kull is an American politician currently serving in the South Dakota House of Representatives. First elected in 2022, he has represented South Dakota's 2nd legislative district as a Republican since 2023. Prior to serving as a representative, he was a member of the Brandon City Council and worked as the chief of the police department in Brandon, South Dakota.

Kull's commitment to public service began during his tenure as a police officer, where he demonstrated leadership, integrity, and dedication to community safety. His experience in law enforcement equipped him with a deep understanding of the challenges faced by residents and the importance of effective governance.

As a member of the Brandon City Council, Kull actively participated in local decision-making, advocating for policies that improved the quality of life for his constituents. His collaborative approach and willingness to listen to diverse perspectives earned him respect among fellow council members and community members alike.

Since assuming office in the South Dakota House of Representatives, Kull has continued to champion the interests of his district. He serves on committees such as the House Health and Human Services Committee and the House Judiciary Committee, where he contributes to shaping legislation that impacts the well-being of South Dakotans.

Kull's dedication to public service extends beyond legislative sessions. He engages with constituents through community events, town halls, and social media platforms, ensuring that their voices are heard in the state capital. His commitment to transparency and accessibility exemplifies the values of a true public servant.

In the upcoming 2024 election, Kull seeks re-election to the South Dakota House of Representatives. His vision includes promoting economic growth, supporting education, and addressing critical issues such as healthcare and infrastructure. As a legislator, he remains committed to working collaboratively with colleagues to find practical solutions that benefit all South Dakotans.
