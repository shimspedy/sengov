Byron Callies is an American politician and a member of the South Dakota House of Representatives. He was first elected in 2022 and has represented South Dakota's 5th legislative district as a Republican since 2023. Before serving as a representative, Callies had a distinguished career in both the military and the energy industry.

Military Career:
Callies spent nearly 41 years in the South Dakota National Guard, retiring in 2006 with the rank of Lieutenant Colonel. His commitment to service and leadership skills have been valuable assets throughout his political career.

Energy Industry:
For most of his working life, Callies was employed in an Investor-owned Electric and Gas Utility. His final 17 years were spent working for the Department of Energy, specifically the Western Area Power Administration (WAPA), in Watertown. His expertise in the energy sector has informed his legislative work.

Political Journey:
In 2018, Callies ran for the state senate from District 5 but was defeated in the primaries. Undeterred, he continued to serve his community and eventually secured a seat in the House of Representatives. His dedication to public service and his commitment to representing the interests of South Dakotans have been evident throughout his career.

Committees:
- House Commerce and Energy Committee (Member)
- House Education Committee (Member)
- House Military and Veterans Affairs Committee (Member)

Byron Callies has been an advocate for responsible energy policies, education, and veterans' affairs. His experience in both the military and the energy industry uniquely positions him to address critical issues facing South Dakota. As a legislator, he continues to work diligently to improve the lives of his constituents.
