Mellissa Heermann (Republican Party) is a dedicated public servant currently serving in the South Dakota House of Representatives. She assumed office on January 10, 2023, and her current term will end on January 14, 2025. Heermann is running for re-election in the Republican primary on June 4, 2024.

Biography:
Mellissa Heermann earned her bachelor's degree in international business, trade, and commerce from the University of Nebraska at Lincoln. She furthered her education by obtaining a master's degree in operations management and supervision from South Dakota State University in 2020. Her academic background reflects her commitment to understanding the complexities of business and management.

Career Experience:
Heermann has held various roles that showcase her diverse skill set. Notably, she served as the revenue cycle director at the Brookings Health System, where she managed financial operations. Additionally, she worked as an IT manager with Daktronics, contributing to technology solutions. Her experience extends to project management, having been involved in mentorship programs with Florence Crittenton.

Community Involvement:
Beyond her professional accomplishments, Heermann has actively engaged with her community. She previously served as the mayor of Lake Preston, South Dakota, demonstrating her leadership abilities. She also contributed to local governance by serving on the Lake Preston city council and the Brookings School District board. Her commitment to education is evident through her involvement in the Brookings County Youth Mentoring Program.

Committee Assignments:
In her current legislative role, Heermann serves on the following committees:
- House Education Committee
- House Health and Human Services Committee

Elections:
In the upcoming general election for South Dakota House of Representatives District 7, Heermann will be competing alongside other candidates. The primary is scheduled for June 4, 2024, and the general election will take place on November 5, 2024. Her opponents include Penny Hauffe and Cole Sartell, both running as Democrats. Notably, there are no incumbents in this race.

Overall, Senator Mellissa Heermann's dedication to public service, combined with her educational background and community involvement, makes her a valuable representative for District 7 in South Dakota
