Sam Marty is a Republican member of the South Dakota State Senate, representing District 28. He assumed office on January 14, 2025, and his current term is set to end on January 12, 2027.

Sam Marty has a rich background that reflects his dedication to public service and his community. Before joining the South Dakota State Senate, he served as a member of the South Dakota House of Representatives, representing District 28B from 2015 to 2023. His tenure in the House was marked by his commitment to addressing the needs of his constituents and advocating for policies that align with his values.

Beyond his political career, Sam Marty has a deep connection to the land and the people of South Dakota. He is a rancher by profession, a role that has given him firsthand experience with the challenges and opportunities faced by rural communities. This background has informed his legislative priorities, particularly in areas such as agriculture, rural development, and land management.

Sam Marty also served in the U.S. Army from 1968 to 1969, demonstrating his dedication to his country. His military service is a testament to his sense of duty and commitment to serving others, values that continue to guide his work in the state legislature.

Throughout his career, Sam Marty has been recognized for his pragmatic approach to governance and his ability to work across party lines to achieve meaningful results. His leadership and dedication have earned him the respect of his colleagues and constituents alike. As a state senator, he remains focused on addressing the pressing issues facing South Dakota and ensuring that the voices of District 28 are heard in the state legislature.
