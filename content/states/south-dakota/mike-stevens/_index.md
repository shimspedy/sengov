Mike Stevens (born January 24, 1953) is an American politician and a Republican member of the South Dakota House of Representatives. He has been representing District 18 since January 12, 2021. Prior to his current term, Stevens also served in the South Dakota House of Representatives from 2013 to 2019.

Education and Legal Career:
Stevens graduated from the University of South Dakota Law School and has been practicing law in Yankton since 1978. His legal expertise has contributed to his active role in legislative matters.

Leadership Roles:
During his previous tenure in the South Dakota House of Representatives, Stevens was elected House Majority Whip on November 15, 2014. He also served as the chairman of the Judiciary Committee, demonstrating his commitment to legal and legislative matters.

Community Involvement:
Beyond politics, Stevens has a strong community presence. He served on the Yankton School Board for an impressive 21 years. Additionally, he was the former president of the South Dakota Trial Lawyers, showcasing his dedication to legal advocacy.

Election History:
- In the 2020 election, Stevens secured victory with 6,778 votes.
- His re-election in 2016 garnered 6,296 votes.
- In 2014, he was re-elected with 4,604 votes.
- Stevens initially entered the Legislature in 2012, winning the second seat in a four-way general election.

Senator Mike Stevens continues to contribute to South Dakota's legislative landscape, advocating for his constituents and upholding the principles of the Republican Party.
