Mike Derby (born c. 1957) is an American businessman and politician. He has been a prominent figure in South Dakota politics, contributing to both the business community and public service.

Early Life and Education:
Derby was raised in Rapid City, South Dakota, and graduated from Stevens High School. He went on to earn a Bachelor of Science degree in business administration from the University of South Dakota in 1979.

Business Ventures:
Derby is the owner of the Canyon Lake Resort in Rapid City, showcasing his entrepreneurial spirit. His business acumen has allowed him to contribute significantly to the local economy and tourism industry.

Political Career:
Derby's political journey began in the late 1990s when he served as a member of the South Dakota House of Representatives. During his first tenure, he chaired the Military Affairs Committee and the Ellsworth Task Force. Additionally, he held the position of vice chair of the South Dakota Legislative Executive Committee.

In 2020, Derby was re-elected to the South Dakota House of Representatives, representing District 34. He assumed office on January 12, 2021, succeeding Michael Diedrich. His commitment to public service continues to shape policies and advocate for the interests of his constituents.

Community Involvement:
Beyond politics, Derby has been actively engaged in community development. He served as the board chair of the Rapid City Area Chamber of Commerce, contributing to economic growth and fostering business relationships. His dedication to the region extends to his role as a board member of Visit Rapid City.

Conclusion:
Senator Mike Derby's multifaceted career reflects his dedication to South Dakota. From business ownership to legislative service, he remains committed to making a positive impact on the state's future.
