Steve Duffy, a Republican Party member, was born in Fort Pierre, South Dakota. He has deep roots in District 32, having lived there for over 40 years. His educational background includes a bachelor's degree from Arizona State University and a Master's in Business Administration from the University of South Dakota.

Duffy's career spans 35 years in broadcasting and cable TV. He worked as a television advertising executive and sales manager before venturing into business ownership. Currently, he owns several rental properties.

As a legislator, Duffy assumed office on January 10, 2023, and his current term extends until January 14, 2025. His commitment to promoting economic growth, balancing business interests with quality-of-life issues, and keeping the tax burden low underscores his dedication to serving both his city and state.

Committee Assignments (2023-2024):
- House Commerce and Energy Committee
- House Transportation Committee

Elections:
- In the 2022 election, Duffy ran for re-election to the South Dakota House of Representatives in District 32.
- The Republican primary for District 32 (2 seats) took place on June 4, 2024, where Duffy competed alongside John Robert James and Brook Kaufman.

General Election (November 5, 2024):
- Nicole Uhre-Balk (D) is also running in the general election for District 32.

Duffy's dedication to public service and his focus on economic well-being make him a valuable representative for South Dakota.
