Joshua Klumb is an American politician, businessman, and farmer serving as a member of the South Dakota Senate from the 20th district. Elected in 2016, he assumed office on January 10, 2017. Klumb earned a Bachelor of Science degree in business and marketing from Oklahoma Wesleyan University. Born in Mount Vernon, South Dakota, he has dedicated his career to public service and community development.

As a farmer, Klumb understands the challenges faced by rural communities. His commitment to agriculture and sustainable practices has been a driving force behind his legislative work. Throughout his tenure, he has championed initiatives to support local farmers, improve infrastructure, and enhance educational opportunities for South Dakota residents.

Klumb's leadership extends beyond the Senate floor. He actively engages with constituents, listening to their concerns and advocating for policies that promote economic growth, healthcare access, and environmental stewardship. His collaborative approach has earned him respect from colleagues across party lines.

In addition to his legislative duties, Klumb remains deeply connected to his roots. He continues to manage his family farm, balancing the demands of public service with the responsibilities of agriculture. His unwavering commitment to both spheres reflects his genuine passion for making a positive impact on the lives of South Dakotans.

Senator Joshua Klumb's dedication, integrity, and tireless work ethic exemplify the values that define our great state. As he continues to serve, we can expect him to tackle pressing issues with the same determination that has characterized his career thus far.
