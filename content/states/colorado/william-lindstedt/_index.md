William Lindstedt is an American politician who has served as a member of the Colorado House of Representatives since January 9, 2023. He represents Colorado's 33rd House district. He has also been a member of the Broomfield city council.

Born in 1992 or 1993, Lindstedt earned a Bachelor of Arts in political science from the University of Colorado at Denver. His career experience includes working as an assistant bench jeweler, a community organizer, and a staffer with the Colorado House of Representatives. He has served as the chairman of the Broomfield Transportation Forum, as a board director of the Denver Regional Council of Governments, and on the Broomfield City Council.

He was elected on November 8, 2022, in the 2022 Colorado House of Representatives election against Republican opponent Stacie Dougherty. He assumed office on January 9, 2023. His current term ends on January 8, 2025.

In his role as a legislator, Lindstedt sits on the Business Affairs and Labor Committee, Finance Committee, and serves as the Vice Chair of the Transportation, Housing, and Local Government Committee.
