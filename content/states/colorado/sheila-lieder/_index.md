Sheila Lieder is an American politician who has served as a member of the Colorado House of Representatives since January 9, 2023. She represents Colorado's 28th House district. Before her election to the house, she worked on the Joe Biden 2020 presidential campaign.

Sheila Lieder was elected on November 8, 2022, in the 2022 Colorado House of Representatives election. She assumed office on January 9, 2023. Lieder describes herself as Pro-choice.

Sheila Lieder's career experience includes working as a voter activation regional director in Colorado for a Joe Biden presidential campaign, a legislative director, and the Colorado and Wyoming legislative political director of the Communications Workers Of America (CWA).

Sheila Lieder (Democratic Party) is a member of the Colorado House of Representatives, representing District 28. She assumed office on January 9, 2023. Her current term ends on January 8, 2025. Lieder (Democratic Party) ran for election to the Colorado House of Representatives to represent District 28. She won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

The Democratic Party selected Sheila Lieder to run in the 2022 general election for Colorado House of Representatives District 28, after Leanne Emm withdrew from the race.
