Tisha Mauro is an American politician who has served as a member of the Colorado House of Representatives since January 9, 2023. She represents Colorado's 46th House district.

Electoral History:
She was elected on November 8, 2022, in the 2022 Colorado House of Representatives election against Republican opponent Jonathan Ambler. She assumed office on January 9, 2023.

Political Positions:
Mauro describes herself as Pro-choice.

Biography:
Outside of politics, Mauro is a Chile farmer. She is a graduate of Pueblo County High School and Colorado State University Pueblo.

She is a proud, lifelong Pueblo County resident, born in Pueblo, and raised on a farm and ranch south of Boone where she learned the importance of our farmers, ranchers and our water. She has worked in the auto industry for 20 years.

Committee Assignments:
She is a part of the Business Affairs & Labor and Transportation, Housing & Local Government committees.

Bills and Resolutions:
She has sponsored several bills and resolutions including HB23-1233 Electric Vehicle Charging And Parking Requirements, HB23-1221 Water Quality Data Standards, HB23-1181 Guaranteed Asset Protection Agreements, HB23-1179 Agricultural Products Inspection Cash Fund Reserve, and HB23-1062 Metropolitan District Tax For Parks And Recreation.
