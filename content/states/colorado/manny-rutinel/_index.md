Manny Rutinel is an American attorney and politician serving as a member of the Colorado House of Representatives for the 32nd district. He assumed office in October 2023.

Education:
Rutinel has an impressive educational background. He earned an associate degree from Pascoâ€“Hernando State College. He then went on to receive a Bachelor of Arts in economics and a Bachelor of Science in microbiology from the University of Florida. Furthering his education, he obtained a Master of Science in applied economics from Johns Hopkins University. Finally, he completed his education with a Juris Doctor from Yale Law School.

Career:
Rutinel's career is as diverse as it is impressive. From 2016 to 2018, he served as an economist for the United States Army Corps of Engineers in Upstate New York. After graduating from law school, he worked as an associate attorney at Earthjustice. Since 2021, Rutinel has worked as the CEO of Climate Refarm, a non-profit organization. In October 2023, he was selected to replace Dafna Michaelson Jenet in the Colorado House of Representatives.

Political Career:
Rutinel is a member of the Democratic Party. He was appointed to the Colorado House of Representatives District 32 on October 9, 2023, to replace Dafna Michaelson Jenet. His current term ends on January 8, 2025. Rutinel is running for re-election to the Colorado House of Representatives to represent District 32. He declared candidacy for the Democratic primary scheduled on June 25, 2024.
