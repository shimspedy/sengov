Matt Soper is a member of the Colorado House of Representatives from the 54th district. He is a Republican and his district encompasses parts of Mesa and Delta counties on Colorado's Western Slope.

Soper was born and raised in Delta, Colorado. He holds degrees from Colorado Mesa University, the University of Edinburgh School of Law, and the University of New Hampshire School of Law. He runs a research and writing business called Oxford Strategies, LLC.

In the 2018 elections, Soper ran unopposed in the Republican primary to replace retiring incumbent Yeulin Willett. He defeated Independent candidate Thea Chase in the general election with 66.01% of the vote.

In the 2020 elections, Soper ran unopposed in the Republican primary. He defeated Democratic nominee AliceMarie Slaven-Emond in the general election with 74.44% of the vote.

In the 2022 Colorado House of Representatives general election, Soper defeated his Democratic Party opponent, winning 73.80% of the total votes cast.

Soper has served on several committees including the Health and Insurance Committee and the House Judiciary Committee. He has also served as the Vice chair of the Legal Services Committee.
