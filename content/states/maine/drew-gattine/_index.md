Andrew Gattine is a member of the Democratic Party serving in the Maine House of Representatives for District 126. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term is set to end on December 4, 2024. Gattine’s career includes significant experience in healthcare management, and he is recognized as an expert on healthcare fraud, waste, and abuse. He has over 30 years of experience as a healthcare consultant, implementing and operating programs aimed at delivering fair, effective, and efficient healthcare.

Gattine has also served as chair of the Maine Democratic Party, elected by party leadership for a two-year term starting January 24, 2021. His legislative service is marked by his membership on the Joint Standing Committee on Appropriations and Financial Affairs and the Joint Select Committee on Housing. These committees handle matters related to general fiscal policy, federal funds allocations, budgeting methodologies, and financial evaluation of state government agencies, among other responsibilities.

Residing in Westbrook, Maine, Gattine has also served three terms as a Westbrook City Councilor, where he chaired the Council’s Finance Committee and Committee of the Whole. Additionally, he chaired Westbrook’s City Charter Commission. Gattine lives on a small family farm with his wife, Elizabeth, their two children, and a number of animals. His dedication to public service has been recognized by various organizations, reflecting his commitment to improving the lives of people in his community and beyond
