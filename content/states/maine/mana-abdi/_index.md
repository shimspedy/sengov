Mana Hared Abdi is a distinguished Somali-American politician. She has made her mark in the political landscape of the United States as a member of the Maine House of Representatives. She represents District 95 in Lewiston, a position she assumed after her Republican opponent, Fred Sanborn-Sanders, withdrew from the race.

Abdi’s educational background is rooted in Maine. She is an alumna of Lewiston High School and later pursued higher education at the University of Maine at Farmington. Her commitment to diversity and intercultural understanding is evident in her professional life. She has been associated with the Office of Intercultural Education at Bates College, contributing to the institution’s multicultural initiatives. Her journey serves as an inspiration, highlighting the significant role of immigrants in shaping the socio-political fabric of the United States.
