Raegan LaRochelle is an American politician and member of the Democratic Party. She is currently serving as a representative of District 59 in the Maine House of Representatives. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term ends on December 4, 2024.

Born and raised in Augusta, Maine, Raegan graduated from Cony High School. She furthered her education by earning degrees from Bowdoin College in 2000 and Yale University in 2006.

Raegan has had a diverse career. She owns a property management business and works as an economic development consultant. She has also served on the Kennebec Valley YMCA board of directors and as a commissioner and chairperson of the Augusta Housing Authority. In addition, she has been associated with Le Club Calumet and Augusta First.

Before her current role, Raegan served as an at-large councillor on the Augusta city council, starting in January 2020. She ran in the special election to succeed Justin Fecteau, a Republican in the 86th district, and won the election on November 2, 2021.

Raegan lives with her partner, Fred, their twin sons, and their cat named Barbara. She continues to contribute to her community through her various roles and affiliations. Her dedication to public service is evident in her numerous contributions to her district and the state of Maine.
