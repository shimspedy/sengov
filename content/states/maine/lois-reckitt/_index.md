Lois Reckitt, a member of the Democratic Party, served in the Maine House of Representatives, representing District 122. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her term ended on October 31, 2023. Reckitt’s political career was marked by her dedication to public service and her commitment to advocating for the rights and well-being of her constituents.

Born on December 31, 1944, in Cambridge, Massachusetts, Lois Galgay Reckitt was an only child to parents who were both polio survivors. She graduated from Watertown High School and pursued higher education at Brandeis University, where she earned a Bachelor of Arts in Biology in 1966. Reckitt continued her studies at Boston University, obtaining a Master of Arts in Marine Biology and Biological Oceanography in 1968.

Reckitt’s professional journey was deeply intertwined with her advocacy work. She was a prominent feminist and activist, known as one of the most influential advocates in Maine for abused women. Her career included roles such as the executive director of Family Crisis Services in Portland, Maine, for over three decades. She also served as the executive vice president of the National Organization for Women (NOW) in Washington, D.C., from 1984 to 1987.

In addition to her work with NOW, Reckitt co-founded the Human Rights Campaign Fund, the Maine Coalition for Human Rights, the Maine Women’s Lobby, and the first Maine chapter of NOW. Her contributions to the community were recognized when she was inducted into the Maine Women’s Hall of Fame in 1998.

Reckitt’s legislative work in the Maine House of Representatives included serving on the Judiciary Committee. She was known for her commitment to criminal justice and public safety, which were reflected in her committee assignments throughout her tenure.

Lois Reckitt’s life was a testament to her unwavering commitment to advocacy and public service. Her legacy continues to inspire those who work towards creating a more just and equitable society. Sadly, Lois Reckitt passed away from cancer on October 31, 2023, leaving behind a lasting impact on the state of Maine and the many lives she touched through her work.
