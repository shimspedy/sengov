Samuel Zager is a distinguished member of the Maine House of Representatives, representing District 116. Born in Subic Bay, Philippines, Zager has a commendable background that includes serving in the U.S. Navy from 1993 to 2004. His academic achievements are notable, with a B.S. in history from the U.S. Naval Academy, a master’s degree in economic and social history from the University of Oxford, and an M.D. from Harvard Medical School.

Zager’s career spans various roles, but he is primarily known for his work as a family physician. His commitment to public service is evident through his membership in the American Academy of Family Physicians, his role on the board of the Portland Public Library, and his involvement with the Maine Academy of Family Physicians.

In his political career, Zager assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term is set to end on December 4, 2024. He has a history of advocating for policies that reflect his dedication to the health and well-being of his constituents. Zager’s multifaceted experience and dedication to service make him a valuable asset to the Maine House of Representatives and to the community he serves.
