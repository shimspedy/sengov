Maureen Fitzgerald Terry, affectionately known as Mo, is a notable figure in American politics. Hailing from the Democratic Party, she represents District 108 in the Maine House of Representatives. Her political journey began in earnest when she assumed office as the Majority Leader of the Maine House of Representatives on December 7, 2022. Prior to this significant role, she served the 26th district from December 7, 2016, to December 7, 2022. She took over from Linda Sanborn in the 26th district and was succeeded by James F. Dill. Before her tenure as the Majority Leader, she was preceded by Michelle Dunphy.

Beyond her political career, Maureen Terry is a proud resident of Gorham, Maine. She is an alumna of Mount Ida College and the Art Institute of New York City. In addition to her political endeavors, she is also a small business owner. Her contributions to her community extend beyond the political sphere, reflecting her commitment and dedication to her constituents. Her multifaceted career continues to inspire those around her.
