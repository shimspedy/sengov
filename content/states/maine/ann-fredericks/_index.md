Ann Marie Fredericks is a member of the Maine House of Representatives, representing District 143. She assumed office on December 7, 2022, and her current term is set to end on December 4, 2024. Fredericks is affiliated with the Republican Party and was victorious in the general election held on November 8, 2022.

Born and residing in Sanford, Maine, Fredericks has a distinguished educational background with a bachelor’s degree in nursing obtained in 1985, a master’s degree in public administration in 1992, and a master’s degree in nursing in 1999. Her professional experience includes working as a nurse practitioner. Additionally, Fredericks has been involved with several organizations, including the American Association of Nurse Practitioners, Association of Cannabinoid Specialists, and the Sanford YMCA.

During her tenure, Fredericks has been assigned to the Health and Human Services Committee, where she contributes to shaping policies and legislation pertinent to the health and welfare of the citizens of Maine. Her commitment to public service is evident through her active participation in the legislative process and her dedication to her constituents in District 143. Fredericks’ career reflects a blend of healthcare expertise and public administration, which she brings to her role in the Maine House of Representatives.
