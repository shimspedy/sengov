Christopher Kessler is a member of the Democratic Party serving in the Maine House of Representatives for District 121. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term is set to end on December 4, 2024. Prior to his tenure, the District 32 seat was succeeded by Steven Foster. Kessler’s compensation includes a base salary of $16,245.12 for the first regular session and $11,668.32 for the second regular session, with additional per diem allowances for lodging and meals.

Kessler’s educational background includes a Bachelor of Arts in music from the State University of New York at Oswego, which he earned in 2005. His professional experience encompasses business ownership, specifically Kessler Home Consulting, and work as an energy auditor.

In his legislative role, Kessler has been assigned to the Energy, Utilities and Technology Committee for the 2023-2024, 2021-2022, and 2019-2020 sessions. His advocacy extends to energy efficiency and renewable energy, and he has been involved in community service through various associations and boards.

A resident of South Portland, Maine, Kessler is also known for his contributions to housing rights. He founded the South Portland Tenants Association and played a pivotal role in establishing the City of South Portland’s first housing security ordinance. Kessler is married and has two daughters, with whom he resides in South Portland.
