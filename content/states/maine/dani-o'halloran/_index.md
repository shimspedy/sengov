Dani O'Halloran is a dedicated and passionate member of the Maine House of Representatives, representing District 20. She assumed office on December 3, 2024, and her current term will end on December 1, 2026. O'Halloran is a member of the Democratic Party and has a strong commitment to her community and constituents.

Before entering politics, Dani O'Halloran had a successful career as a realtor, which provided her with valuable insights into the housing market and the needs of her community. Her professional experience has been instrumental in shaping her approach to policy-making and her dedication to improving the lives of her constituents.

O'Halloran's journey into politics began with her involvement in various local boards and committees. She served on the Brewer School Committee, the Brewer City Council, and the United Technologies Center Board. Additionally, she was a board member of the Realty of Maine Cares Board and the Brewer Federal Credit Union Board. These roles allowed her to gain a deep understanding of the issues facing her community and to develop strong relationships with local leaders and residents.

As a state representative, Dani O'Halloran is a member of the Judiciary Committee, where she works on legislation related to the legal system and the protection of individual rights. Her primary focus is on supporting measures that enhance the state's economic development and create opportunities for all Maine families to succeed. O'Halloran is particularly passionate about policies that address affordable housing, education, and healthcare.

Throughout her career, O'Halloran has been known for her dedication to her constituents and her ability to listen to their concerns. She believes in the importance of open communication and transparency in government, and she strives to be a responsive and accessible representative for the people of District 20.

Dani O'Halloran lives in Brewer, Maine, with her husband, Dana Andrews, and their two daughters, Rowan and Riley Andrews. Her family is a source of inspiration and motivation for her work in the legislature, and she is committed to creating a better future for all Maine residents.
