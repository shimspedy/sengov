Michel Lajoie, a member of the Democratic Party, is currently serving in the Maine House of Representatives, representing District 96. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term is set to end on December 4, 2024.

Michel Lajoie lives in Lewiston, Maine. He earned an associate degree in 1991 and has had a diverse career. Notably, he served as the chief of the Lewiston Fire Department. In addition to his political career, Lajoie has been involved in various community activities. He has served on the board of directors of the Gendron Franco Center and has been affiliated with the Knights of Columbus Holy Family Council 10019 and the Franco-American War Veterans Post 31.

In the general election held on November 8, 2022, Michel Lajoie ran for election to the Maine House of Representatives to represent District 96. He emerged victorious, defeating the incumbent Jonathan Connor.

Before his current tenure, Michel Lajoie had served in the Maine House of Representatives, representing District 58. He is now serving his fifth non-consecutive term in the Maine House of Representatives. He sits on the Criminal Justice and Public Safety Committee. A lifelong resident of Lewiston, Rep. Lajoie is a former small business owner and Lewiston Fire Chief who served the city for over 31 years.
