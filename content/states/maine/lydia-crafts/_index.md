Lydia Crafts, a dedicated member of the Democratic Party, currently holds the position of a representative in the Maine House of Representatives, serving District 46. Her commitment to public service materialized as she assumed office on December 7, 2022, with her current term set to conclude on December 4, 2024.

In the pursuit of continued service, Crafts sought re-election to the Maine House of Representatives for District 46, securing victory in the general election held on November 8, 2022. As part of her engagement with the electorate, Crafts completed Ballotpedia's Candidate Connection survey in 2022, providing insights into her perspectives and priorities.

Residing in Newcastle, Maine, Lydia Crafts brings a wealth of academic achievement and professional experience to her role. She earned a bachelor's degree in anthropology from Smith College in 2005 and went on to obtain a master's degree in social work from the University of Maine at Orono in 2013. Crafts has contributed significantly to her community as a clinical social worker and the behavior program director of Bristol Consolidated School. Her affiliations extend to the Newcastle Board of Appeals, reflecting her commitment to addressing community concerns.
