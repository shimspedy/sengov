William Bridgeo, a member of the Democratic Party, represents District 60 in the Maine House of Representatives. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term ends on December 4, 2024. Bridgeo ran for election to the Maine House of Representatives to represent District 60 and won in the general election on November 8, 2022.

He lives in Augusta, Maine, and earned an M.P.A. from the University of Hartford in 1979. In terms of his responsibilities, Bridgeo was assigned to the Environment and Natural Resources Committee for the 2023-2024 term.

In the general election for Maine House of Representatives District 60, Bridgeo defeated William Clardy, securing 59.7% of the votes. It’s worth noting that there were no incumbents in this race.

Before his political career, Bridgeo had a distinguished academic career. He served as the first Dean of Science at Saint Mary’s University from 1967 to 1977 and was a leader in the development of the Faculty. He was also heavily involved in the Water Resources Sub-Committee, including serving as Chair from 1971 to 1973. His contributions to the field of science and education are noteworthy and have left a lasting impact.

Overall, William Bridgeo’s career spans academia, science, and politics, demonstrating a commitment to public service and leadership. His work in the Maine House of Representatives continues this legacy.
