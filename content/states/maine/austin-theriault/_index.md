Austin L. Theriault, born on January 23, 1994, is a multifaceted American figure known for his roles as a professional stock car racing driver and politician deeply engaged in driver development. His diverse career has seen him navigate the fast-paced world of NASCAR as well as the intricate realm of politics.

In his racing pursuits, Theriault made his mark in the NASCAR Cup Series, notably piloting the No. 52 vehicle for Rick Ware Racing during his last part-time stint. Simultaneously, he showcased his prowess in the NASCAR Gander Outdoors Truck Series, steering the No. 92 Ford F-150 for RBR Enterprises.

One of Theriault's significant achievements was clinching the ARCA Racing Series championship in 2017, a testament to his skill and determination behind the wheel, all while racing for Ken Schrader Racing.

Beyond the racetrack, Austin L. Theriault has made notable strides in the political arena. Representing the 1st district of the Maine House of Representatives since 2022, he has proven his dedication to public service. A member of the Republican Party, Theriault secured his position in the legislature after winning an uncontested Republican primary and subsequently earning an impressive 71% of the vote in the general election. His tenure officially commenced on December 7, 2022.

Looking ahead, Theriault continues to expand his political footprint. In a bid to further contribute to Maine's political landscape, he is actively participating in the 2024 Republican primary for Maine's 2nd congressional district.
