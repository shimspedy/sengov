Benjamin Collings is a member of the Democratic Party serving in the Maine House of Representatives for District 114. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term is set to end on December 4, 2024. Collings was successful in his re-election bid for the same district in the general election held on November 8, 2022.

Born and raised in Portland, Maine, Collings holds a bachelor’s degree in social science from the University of Maine at Farmington. His professional background includes extensive experience as a political and business consultant. Prior to his current role, Collings has been actively involved in politics for about 30 years. He has served as a legislative aide in the House Democratic Office during the 120th Legislature and has lobbied in Augusta and Washington D.C. on behalf of various groups including Native American tribes and labor unions.

In the 2016 presidential campaign, Collings played a significant role in Bernie Sanders’ campaign efforts in Maine and conducted national Native American outreach for the campaign. His legislative priorities include increasing the financial well-being of the residents of Portland and the state of Maine, with a strong emphasis on education, infrastructure, and technology investments. Collings is also an advocate for restoring civility, integrity, and visionary leadership in Augusta.

During his tenure, Collings has been assigned to several committees, including the Appropriations and Financial Affairs Committee and the Veterans and Legal Affairs Committee. He is known for his commitment to veterans’ programs and has a track record of supporting legislation that benefits the hardworking people of his district and state.

Collings resides in Portland and is a father of three children. His dedication to public service and his constituents is evident in his work and the policies he supports.
