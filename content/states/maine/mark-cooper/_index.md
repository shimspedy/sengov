Mark Cooper is serving his first term as the Representative for District 107, which includes part of Windham. He assumed office on December 3, 2024. His current term ends on December 1, 2026.

A lifelong resident of Windham, Mark is the owner-operator of L.C. Cooper, Inc. Building Contractor, Cooper’s Royal Heritage Farm, and Cooper’s Maple Products. His farm produces beef cattle, maple syrup, greenhouse products, miniature horses, and honey bees.

Mark graduated from Windham High School and the University of New Hampshire, earning an Associate’s Degree in Animal Science. He serves as the Director and Superintendent of the Cumberland Farmers Club-Fair and is a Director of the Maine Maple Producers Association.

Mark is an active member of several organizations, including the Maine Farm Bureau, Maine Maple Producers Association, Cumberland Fair, Maine State Beekeepers Association, and the American Aberdeen Association.

He is married to his wife, Gaylene, and they have two children and two grandchildren. Mark’s favorite activity is spending time with his grandchildren.
