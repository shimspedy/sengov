Flavia DeBrito is a dedicated and passionate community organizer who currently serves as a member of the Maine House of Representatives, representing District 64. She assumed office on December 3, 2024, after winning the general election on November 5, 2024. Her current term is set to end on December 1, 2026.

DeBrito's journey into politics began with her strong commitment to her community. She earned a bachelor's degree from Thomas College in 2020, which laid the foundation for her career in public service. Before her tenure in the Maine House of Representatives, DeBrito served as a Waterville City Council member, where she worked tirelessly to address the needs and concerns of her constituents.

Throughout her career, DeBrito has been actively involved in various organizations, including the Maine State Parents Ambassadors and Educare. Her work as a community organizer has allowed her to connect with people from all walks of life and advocate for policies that promote social justice, education, and healthcare.

In the Maine House of Representatives, DeBrito serves on the Health and Human Services Committee, where she continues to champion the causes she is passionate about. Her dedication to her constituents and her unwavering commitment to making a positive impact on her community have earned her the respect and admiration of her peers.

Flavia DeBrito's story is one of perseverance, dedication, and a deep-rooted desire to make a difference in the lives of those she represents. Her work in the Maine House of Representatives is a testament to her commitment to public service and her belief in the power of community-driven change.
