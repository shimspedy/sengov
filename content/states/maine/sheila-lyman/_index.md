Representative Sheila Lyman, hailing from Livermore Falls, is currently in her second term representing House District 76, encompassing the towns of Livermore Falls, Jay, and a portion of Livermore. Within the 131st Legislature, she actively contributes to the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs.

A graduate of Leavitt Area High School, Rep. Lyman holds an education degree from the University of Maine at Farmington and devoted her entire career to elementary education. Over 36 years, she served as a dedicated teacher at the same school, assuming leadership roles in district grade level work, curriculum, and assessment development at both local and state levels. Beyond the classroom, she advocated for educational change, sharing her insights at various schools across the state and country. Her commitment to providing students with foundational educational experiences has left a lasting impact on their development as responsible community members.

With a knack for asking challenging questions that delve into the heart of a matter, Rep. Lyman isn't hesitant to stand as a lone voice in a crowd. In the face of differing opinions, she works diligently to identify common ground, fostering effective plans. Drawing from her experiences in educational politics, she believes her skill set is well-suited to represent the citizens of District 76 in the Legislature.
