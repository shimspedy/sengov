Mark Blier is a member of the Republican Party serving in the Maine House of Representatives for District 138. He assumed office on December 7, 2022, and his current term is set to end on December 4, 2024. Blier successfully ran for re-election in the general election held on November 8, 2022.

Residing in Buxton, Maine, Mark Blier has a professional background in real estate. His legislative service includes assignments to the Appropriations and Financial Affairs Committee and the Government Oversight Committee. Previously, he was also a member of the Health Coverage, Insurance and Financial Services Committee.

As a state representative, Blier’s responsibilities involve addressing the needs and concerns of his constituents from towns such as Cornish, Limington, and parts of Buxton and Hollis. His work in the legislature focuses on various aspects of state governance, including financial affairs and government oversight. Blier’s commitment to public service is reflected in his active participation in the legislative process and his dedication to the well-being of the residents of District 138.
